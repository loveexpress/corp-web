A Life Changing Event-A Unique Concert

Natalie Grant

In 1992 a LIFE CHANGING event would happen to Len and Andrea Deo…Len recalls:

“When our son was a junior in high school my wife and I found ourselves in his health class for back to school night. The gentlemen sitting next to us challenged the health class teacher and said “I heard that, God forbid, if my daughter finds herself pregnant, that you as her health teacher could take her for an abortion without my approval as her father. Is that true?” The answer was YES! Andrea and I were shocked, as I am sure many of you would have been. The Good LORD used that incident to move Andrea and me to DO SOMETHING! I just felt the burden to do something for all those parents and grandparents out there who I know feel exactly like me and Andrea did but perhaps did not have the circumstances to make a life changing move.”

“After many “Godcidences” and after a six month period of time devoted to prayer, we made the decision to move forward and in association with Focus on the Family (FOTF) we founded the New Jersey Family Policy Council (NJFPC). Today, we defend family friendly policies in the public square. We oppose hostile policies that threaten the integrity of the family, the free expression of religion and parental rights. We also provide venues for citizens, churches and clergy to have a significant voice in the public square. We do all this in association with CitizenLink, the public policy partner of FOTF, the Family Research Council, Alliance Defending Freedom and 37 other state-based Family Policy Councils partnering together.”

Speaking of partnering together the NJFPC is looking for people who want to change their world, those who want to become culture changers! Each year the NJFPC calls together Christians from all corners of New Jersey who resonate with the mission…”To champion family values by creating culture changers at home, in the church, in the voting booth and in the public square. Deo states “we have a big vision.. we see New Jersey as a state that honors God, where religious freedom flourishes, families thrive and life is cherished.”

So we would like for you to get to know us and for us to get to know you by extending an invitation to join us for our annual partnership event. This year we will hold a concert featuring Christian mu­sic superstar Natalie Grant, 6 TIME DOVE AWARD WINNER, Grammy nominated, and founder of Hope for Justice to fight the evil of modern-day slavery and human trafficking in the sex industry. Deo concluded “Natalie not only talks the talk but she walks the walk, and that’s why we at the NJFPC so admire this gifted recording artist!

PLEASE JOIN US ON November 20, 2015. For more information go to www.njfpc.org and click on Natalie Grant or call us at 1-800-653-7204. We look forward to seeing you there!
