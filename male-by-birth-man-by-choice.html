"Male by Birth,
Man by Choice"

by Scott Caesar

In the thirteenth chapter of 1Corithians, God inspires and Paul writes to males who believe they are men. “When I was a child I used to speak as a child, think like a child, reason as a child. When I became a man, I did away with childish things”. 1Corinthians 13:11

It is interesting to note that the progression of immaturity described in this verse parallels that of a child/adolescent. A child speaks (acts) first then thinks as he then reasons the consequences. A Mature Man reasons, understands then acts.

The word child here in the Greek as defined by both Key Word and Strong’s Dictionaries means “infant.” In Biblical terms, “a loveless immature Christian man.” For you and I to engage this verse in context and apply it to our lives today, we must first know that love is both the steel and velvet of mature manhood as Christ-likeness and manhood are synonymous. A man’s muscle is measured in his sacrificial love for his fellow man.

Let us visit what male immaturity looks like.

• Immature males speak (act) before they think. Many broken relationships and business failures might have been spared had a mature man been on one end of it.

• Immature males believe every new philosophy about their faith, finances and family while never fully grasping the truth that discipleship and discomfort go hand and hand. Eph 4:14

• Immature males are mentally lazy, dull and need to be told things over and over instead of hearing it once and completing the task at hand. A Lazy man is not one who does nothing, but a man who only does what he wants, when he wants.

• Immature males are victims, complaining about circumstances and making matters worse instead of playing the man and failing forward as a victor.

• Immature males go to their wives with every sniffling little trouble in their life instead of playing the man and speaking with her from the victory side of life.

Finally in chapter 4 of Galatians we are told that outside of salvation an immature man is really in the same mental state of bon­dage that he was before he received Christ as Lord. A slave to the world and it’s ways. Paul, in contrasting the mature side of manhood, says when he became a man he put away immature things.

Man of God, Scripture bears witness to the fact that if we do not mature in our faith that we are no better on earth now than when we were of the world. Maturity in spirit is only achieved through the training of the soul.

The Bible says “Instead train yourself to be godly. Physical training is good, but training for godliness is much better, promising benefits in this life and in the life to come.” (1Timothy 4:7b-8) How do I train myself for godliness? What’s a guy to do? Glad you asked.
