From the Editor:

Hurricane Sandy

Unifies The Body of Christ

by Edith M. Stranges

Many of the readers of The Love Express have watched and are grieved over the heartaches and damage that occurred during Hurricane Sandy and the Northeastern storm. The Love Express suffered no physical damage but many pastors and congregations suffer losses. This tragedy is ongoing as people try to rebuild their lives. I’ve listed several of the groups that are helping in this effort.

NYSUM immediately put a plan in effect, (Pastor Jorge Vega, Full Gospel Tabernacle, Far Rockaway; Pastor Connie Hulla from Coney Island Gospel Tabernacle said there are many people in Coney Island without food, electricity and heat. Staten Island was one of the areas hardest hit by Sandy. Pastor Daniel Delgado, Executive Director of Third Day Missions, arrived on the scene shortly after the storm hit. Delgado stated one of the hubs, Christian Pentecostal Church, which is the main headquarters during and after for storm relief. Oasis Christian Center was an outreach to the community. A few of our church members were also affected by the Hurricane Sandy. Jim Esposito of NYCRC is working on the effects of children suffering from Post-Traumatic Stress. Juan Galloway of The Relief Bus in Elizabeth, NJ, He and his staff were immediately on the scene with clothes and food.

Concerts of Prayer of Greater NY ministry leaders on Friday, Nov. 2nd began to intercede and speak words of life over a region that has suffered unprecendented damage. Abounding Grace Ministries of NYC led and continues to lead an effort to distribute food, water, blankets to the needy. Star 99.1 FM started immediately on the radio to be the voice of calm in the storm.

My family spent many summers at Seaside Heights, New Jersey with my cousins and their families, walking the boardwalk then and now seeing it totally destroyed is very disturbing to me, But we will rebuild; Trust God.

People are in need of help and thank God for the help they are receiving and will receive.
