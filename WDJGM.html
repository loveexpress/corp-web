Why Didn't Jesus Get Married?

Julius Pearson
Writer & Director

Marriage and relationship are two entities that go hand in hand. It’s the relationship that generally leads to marriage. Marriage is an institution or- dained by God. Out of marriage, the structure of family is established. Why does one marry? Marriages are formed out of many reasons. To satisfy the flesh, he or she looks good (nice body, good hair), has a good job, I’m pregnant and my father’s the Pastor and we can’t make the family look bad. There are some marriages that are motivated by love. But what is love? Love is God. When love is the primary reason, there is nothing that can come between your marriages. When Jesus is the center of your marriage along with an open line of communication, there is no problem that can’t be rectified. But what is going wrong?

Marriages today are ending in divorce at an all time high. It’s sad when the non-Christian divorces, but its disgraceful and very sad when Christians divorce. Where is the God that you talk about, that can do all things but fail? This indicates that someone has taken their eyes off Jesus and the communication is broken.

Here’s a Play that deals with marriage from a mans point of view. A Play that shows what happens when we don’t seek the counsel of God. This Play started in June 2008 and has been life changing. An example is a young lady who was guilty of not allowing her children’s father to see his children. After seeing the Play she was convicted and vowed to change her ways. Many came and saw themselves and where their marriage used to be or is presently.

This Play has impacted people in many ways and also stirred up a little controversy. People have said ‘this is blasphemy’ simply from the title and not from seeing the Play. How judgmental! It is actual a comedy with drama and inspirational but not the typical gospel Play. The whole family will enjoy it!
