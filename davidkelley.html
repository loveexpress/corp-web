In the Midst of the Storm,

There are Blessings all around You.

Rev. Dr. David L. Kelley, II

As you emerge out of the Utica Ave “A” train station and look up towards the heavens you will notice a 36 foot white steeple that sits 135 feet high in the air. It is suspended atop of a blue roof, that serves as the dividing line between Gods magnificent blue sky and Gods amazing new “Blue Church” of Brooklyn. This edifice is the place of worship for the Christ Fellowship Baptist Church. Under the leadership and vision of Rev. Dr. David L. Kelley II, the members of Christ Fellowship through faith, built a brand new place of worship as a testament to God’s blessings over the past 28 years.

On July 2006, Christ Fellowship Baptist Church had their final worship service in the old building; a quaint white two story, 8000 square foot church with seating capacity of 400. To the Glory of God, the Christ Fellowship family, out grew this space and stepped out on faith to build a larger church.

On October 14, 2006, Christ Fellowship broke ground in commitment to their vision. Physical construction began in January 2007 which commemorated the church’s 25th Anniversary.

Pastor Kelley teaches that the church should be supported through tithes and offerings. As a testimony to these teachings the initial funding for this project was obtained through the practice and commitment of tithes and offering. After the generous giving from the members, the church sought to secure a bank loan. The bank agreed to finance a loan but first the church would have to raise an additional $400, 000. With prayer and sacrifice the church raised the money in six months.

The recession of 2007 put a financial strain on this project and extended it from 18 months to 42 months. During this time the congregation was asked to give sacrificial offerings to meet the obligations of the church. There was no campaign or set amount. Pastor Kelley left it up to the individual members to give what they could.

As the membership demonstrated their faith, God honored their sacrifice and Christ Fellowship Baptist Church, held their first worship service on Sunday, February 21, 2010, in their new home; “The Blue Church of Brooklyn” located at 11 Sumpter Street.

This beautiful 38,000 square foot edifice, equipped with an elevator, has three floors. The first floor has 15 classrooms, administrative offices, Pastor’s study and a chapel, which converts into multi-purpose rooms. Located on the second floor is the sanctuary which seats 1000. The third floor, which is not completed, is our Community Life Center and will be used for community functions. It will contain a theater, a 600 seat banquet hall, and basketball court.

Our church building is a testimony of faith and how awesome our God is.

This article was written by Francine Crawford.
