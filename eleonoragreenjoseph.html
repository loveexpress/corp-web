A Celebration of Life
A Virtuous Woman and a Woman of Grace

Rev. Dr. Eleonora Green Joseph
Dec. 31, 1929-Feb. 28, 2013

Rev. Dr. Eleonora Joseph was born in Cristobal, Canal Zone on December 31, 1929 in the Republic of Panama. She was the third child born to the late Edward and Eldora Green.

Dr. Eleonora Joseph is lovingly known to her family, friends and those in Ministry as, “Nors, Nora, Sis. Jo, Mo­ther Jo, Momma Joe, MaJo, Mom, Tia, Aunt Nora, Babes, Queen E, Nanny, Nana, grandma and Dr. Eleonor.” She loved people and loved having people around her. She was elegant and full of style, always the belle of the ball. However, she especially loved God with all her heart and those who knew her knew it.

Dr. Eleonora was an accomplished woman. She possessed the qualities of a born leader and exhibited the grace and charm of a queen. She has been a photographer, disc jockey, politician, Boy Scout leader, life insurance salesperson, entrepreneur, chaplain, counselor, author of monthly devotionals -“He Shall Cover Me,” author of the book “Seasoned Saints,” columnist for The Love Express who started writing in May 1995.

Mother Jo was married to the late Bishop Harold Lloyd Joseph. They loved each other, accepted the Lord together in 1966 and served together in ministry. The two were really one.

In March of 1968, Rev. Joseph made a tough decision to leave her family and migrate to the United States. She was determined to pave the way for her family’s success. Bishop Joseph later joined her in 1969.

In 1973, Dr. Eleonora was ordained to the ministry as an Evangelist. In 1976, along with Bishop Joseph, they founded Triumphant Full Gospel Assembly, Inc. in Brooklyn, NY. She had a Bachelor of Theology from United Christian College, a Bachelor of Arts from College of New Rochelle, a Master of Theology from United Christian College, a Master of Theology from Logos Bible College and a Doctor of Ministry from Vision University. She was also a Chaplain of Pastoral Education - Lutheran Hospital. Dr. Joseph was the co-pastor of Triumphant Full Gospel Assembly until they passed the baton in 1994 to their eldest daughter Lilia and continued coaching.

Rev. Joseph leaves to celebrate her life her children, Apostle Lilia A. Bayne, Elder Jose Hyatt, the late minister Isaac “Mario” Hyatt and the Rev. H. Aurelia Jemmott. Her “sons”-in-law, Rev. Melvin E. Bayne, Sr. and Bishop Roberto J. Jemmott. A daughter in law, Cecilia Hyatt. Grandchildren - Dr. Melvin E. Bayne, Jr. Mark E. Bayne, Lamar A. Bayne-Thomas and her husband Jemehl, and Ashleigh B. Jemmott. One great grand, Colin J. Thomas. Brothers in Panama - Arthur Green and Sylvester “Johnnie” Green. Sister - Felicia Green, Cousin - Juanita Green. Sisters-in-law - Dorothy Brooks, Olga Griffith, Rev. Claudette Joseph, Hazel Shaw and Gloria Joseph. One Brother-in-law, Frank Joseph. A host of nieces and nephews, Spiritual sons and daughters, A. “Bestie” for 58 years - Cynthia “Terry” Roper. Many other relatives and friends too numerous to mention.

Rest Mother. You have fought a good fight!
