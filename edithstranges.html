Bishop Frank Duprée

Edith Stranges

Bishop Frank Duprée, founder of Metro Apostolic Network, based in the Grea­ter Newark, NJ area, was asked to be the Spiritual Overseer for Unity Day after Marvin Cratch and Adrienne Wilson met him at one of the Gatekeeper Ga­therings he holds in Newark. They wan­ted to realize a greater impact upon the Churches and Ministries of the Greater Newark, NJ area and they believed that his involvement would facilitate that since Bishop Du­prée is also a Leader in the Transformation Newark movement.

Bishop Duprée immediately began to call forth the “Spirit of Unity” among the Churches and it is being de­monstrated by the increase in participation of Chur­ches from many different backgrounds and denominations! African-American, Brazilian, Spanish and Latino, Haitian and White Churches are all coming together to make Unity Day 2014 a “Celebration of The Body of Christ”! And, because of the increase in the number of Churches being involved the new Administration of the City of Newark, led by the new Mayor Ras Baraka, has thrown the support of the City behind this years Unity Day Fesitval! This is proving that when the Churches unite the world stands up and takes notice!

Bishop Frank Dupree

Last month’s article on racial reconciliation in the tri-state area, it come to my attention that chur­ches, pastors and organizations are banding toge­- ther to make a difference in their coummunities and cities. I’m so thankful for the responses to the articles and to the people of God that have God’s heart, to work together.
