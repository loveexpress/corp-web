Purpose, Passion, Movement


by Bryan Gonzalez

I believe with all that I am that life at it’s core is fairly simple with of course it’s random spurts unexpected jargin. We wake up, we go to sleep and somewhere in between we live a little thing we like to call life. Oddly enough most of us are okay with this notion but some, who have summoned the courage, ventured off into the unknown discovering all the mysteries our amazing world has to offer.

Ironicly, this is what makes life so exciting and thrilling to experience. That these “spurts” of unexpected events would define our lives in more ways than we can really hope to understand. We see that a simple and seemingly insignificant decision can alter the very course of lives. Who could have possibly known that a women who said, “yeah, sure” to a harmless date with a man who at the time was just another average Joe who would become her beloved husband of 40 years?

But by now you probably thinking, “what does it have to do with Fuel Conference?”

Well, Fuel Conference is about as simple as it gets. People are hurting, they’re tired and broken. Unfortunately there’s more than meets the eye because it’s not just the unsaved who are suffering but its God’s people who share this same pain.

This Conference is about bringing restoration back to God’s people, giving them back their purpose and passion. This conference is about reaching out to the lost, the weary and the broken. Lavishing them with the same love that Christ has shown us everyday of our lives. this conference is about bringing these two people under one banner, the banner of Christ, standing together as one movement and that together we can do things we could never do if we didn’t stand together.

So we woke up, went to sleep and between we lived a little thing called life. A life that is defined by the call Fuel Conference. Passion and purpose...A movement.

Fuel Conference Oct. 5-7, 2012. Faith Fellowship Ministries, 2707 Main St. Extension, Sayreville, NJ 08872. www.fuelconf.com for tickets & more info.
