War Room Wins Big
With The Power of Prayer

War Room’s Priscilla Shirer (Elizabeth Jordan) and her husband T.C. Stallings (Tony Jordan) are a couple who on the surface have it all: solid careers, a beautiful home, and a cute middle schooler. The reality is quite different than appearances. They live in a metaphorical “war zone” and they quite regularly ambush each other with harsh and cynical words. The couple’s main weapon of destruction is a festering anger that permeates every fragile interaction. Tony treats his wife like an insurgent; he is irritated (bordering on incensed) at his wife daily. The film follows the couple with Elizabeth Jordan taking a clear lead as the main strategist as they reach a peace agreement of sorts.

Elizabeth, a real estate agent, meets Miss Clara, an elderly woman, who wants to

sell her house in which she has a prayer closet that intrigues Elizabeth. Miss Clara

encourages her to pray for her failing marriage. At first, Elizabeth is reticent, but encouraged by Miss Clara’s prayer life and confronted by the grim reality that if she doesn’t do something, her marriage will fall apart, Elizabeth relents. Left alone much of the time because her husband is often away on business trips, Elizabeth starts to pray in her own closet. Soon, Elizabeth see’s a change in herself and later in her husband, especially when he is caught stealing from his employer. The message of the film is that prayer works and for Elizabeth it saved her marriage.

Jesus in War Room

War Room is the fifth film from Christian filmmaking duo Alex and Stephen Kendrick. As with their other films,(Flywheel, Facing the Giants,Fireproof, and Courageous) Jesus is prevalent throughout the movie. War Room illustrates the power of prayer effectively through dialogue, action and the plot. For instance, it is the prayers of Elizabeth that help her love and respect her husband in spite of his shortcomings.

The film also explores Biblical themes of forgiveness and mercy. When Tony returns the pharmaceuticals he stole, his boss has mercy on him and doesn’t send him straight to jail. However, Tony does have to face some consequences for his actions and pay back the stolen money.

I sat in my chair with my feet pressed firmly on the floor ready to cringe at the boss’s decision. Something told me (not sure what) that Tony would get off scot free which I think contrived. I was somewhat relieved when To­ny had to pay back the mo­ney he stole. But, then as I drove home from the theatre, I felt convicted. Even if Tony didn’t have to make re­com­pense, that doesn’t make his confession any less admi­ra­ble since he was willing to confess in full light of the consequences. Thinking it over, if Tony’s boss forgave the debt completely, it would have been alright with me. Debt cancellation is Biblical. After all, that’s what Jesus did for us.

Rated: PG

Opens August 28, 2015

Starring: Priscilla Shirer and T.C. Stallings
