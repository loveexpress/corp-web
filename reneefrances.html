Rev. Dr. Renee Frances Washington Gardner Celebrates 10th Pastoral Anniversary

Rev. Dr. Renee Fran­ces Washington Gardner is a woman of many firsts. When Har­lem’s renowned Bishop Preston Washington, Sr. passed away, she became not only the first woman elected Pastor of an historic Baptist Church in Harlem but the first wife to succeed her husband and Memorial Baptist Church’s first female Senior Pastor.

Fondly called Rev. Re­nee by the spiritual community of Harlem, she and her late husband Bishop Preston Washington, Sr. founded The House of Hope, a community revitalization project for homeless single parents and their children. During the AIDS epidemic, Rev. Renee and Bishop Washington, Sr. were on the frontline in the fight against the disease. Under their administration, Memorial Baptist Church became one of the first Harlem-based churches to openly address the issue from the pulpit. Their leadership and commitment was instrumental in the formation of The Balm in Gilead, Inc. a leading international non-governmental organization (NGO) that partners with houses of worship to advocate and educate communities of color on HIV/AIDS. The couple were pioneers during Har­lem’s expanding mega church renaissance and served as ambassadors to Harlem Spi­ritual Tours where they played an instrumental role in nurturing the world-re­nowned Japan Gospel Choir and warmly welcome tourist from all over the world to receive a true worship experience.

In 2006, Rev. Dr. Renee married Rev. Dr. Sean P. Gardner, Sr., Senior Pastor of Eastward Missionary Baptist Church and became Harlem’s first Baptist preacher to serve as Senior Pastor and First lady. Memorial Baptist Church and Eastward Missionary Church are members of the United Missionary Baptist Association, a fellowship of over 100 Baptist churches in New York City, Bronx, and lower West­chester, where Pastor Re­nee serves as the first women to be elected as the Second Vice Moderator.

Continuing to operate in her own gifting, Pastor Re­nee formed The Memorial Baptist Church Women of Worship Ministry. Over the years the ministry, which assists women in maturing spiritually, has grown to include a youth and young adult wo­men’s ministry, which celebrated their first retreat earlier this year. In the 10 years at the helm as Senior Pastor, Pastor Renee instituted a finance committee, single’s ministry, senior’s ministry, marriage ministry, missionary ministry, and mission / evangelism ministry to name a few.

Pastor Renee also took on the challenge of renovating and modernizing Memorial Baptist Church. Under her administration the historical edifice has a newly remodeled sanctuary, fellowship hall, bathrooms and kitchen. It has a state of the art sound system and new awning. Keeping up with the times, Rev. Renee also initiated a website, established social media channels and created a monthly newsletter called The Messenger.

In August 2008, Pastor Renee held a special ribbon cutting ceremony for the Rev. Dr. Preston R. Washington Sr. Educational Wing. Her husband, Rev. Dr. Sean Gardner served as construction site manager. The center, which will include an upcoming computer lab and executive conference room, was developed for academic and career enrichment and a resource center to build cultural awareness, civic pride and responsibility for all those who live in, love and visit Harlem.

A Brooklyn native, Pastor Renee Frances Washington Gardner is a graduate of the NY Theological Semina­ry/Eastern Baptist Extension Program in Urban Ministry and is a respected cosmetologist with extensive training in mortuary science. She received an honorary Doctorate of Divinity from St. Thomas Christian College, is a current Chaplain for the NYC Transit Authority and is currently enrolled in the Veterans Affairs Chaplaincy and Clinical Pastoral Education Program. She is the recipient of numerous awards and citations including the NAACP’s Women’s History Month Award for Outstanding Community Service; the Woman of Excellence Award from the Rev. Al Sharpton’s National Action Network, and the Ringling Brothers and Barnum Bailey Circus’ Community and Business Leader Appreciation Award.

Recognized as an anoin­ted preacher, counselor and spiritual leader, Pastor Re­nee was appointed to the Ordination Council of the United Missionary Baptist Association.

Pastor Renee has served in the gospel ministry for over 28 years and is the mother of five sons and grandmother of ten. She and Rev. Dr. Gardner, Sr. continue to share their love for the LORD with other chur­ches and houses of worship through leadership training, workshops and symposiums throughout the nation.

In October 2014, Pastor Renee Frances Washington Gardner will cele­- brate her 10th Anniversary of service to the Har­lem and Greater New York spiritual community as Senior Pastor of Memorial Baptist Church, 141 Bishop Pres­ton R. Washington Sr. Place (W. 115th St.), Harl­em, NY 10026. For more information call 212-663-8830.
