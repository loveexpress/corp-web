January Tax Check List

By Donald Reid, MBA

Happy New Year! Having made it into 2016 where do we go from here? What do we need to know so that we can be on the cutting edge of Financial Planning in this new year.

Answer: Consult your Tax Advisor if any of the foregoing situations were experienced by you last year.

1. Having a child.

2.  Adopt a child.

3.  Put a child in day care. 

4.  Send a child to college.

5.  Death of a spouse or dependent child.

6. Provide home for parent or sibling.

7. Take a foster child into your home.

8. Receive proceeds from lawsuits.

9. Receive an inheritance / gift.

10. Receive social security for first time.

11. Receive retirement benefits for the first time.

12. Inherit an IRA, pension or annuity.

13. Receive/pay alimony.

14. Receive a fee as Executor.

15. Serve as a paid Trustee.

16. Receive a Director fee.

17. Win the lottery.

18. Win a raffle prize or sweep­stake.

19.Have high medical expen­ses.

20. Make non-cash charitable contributions.

21. Refinance your mortgage.

22. Begin using your car for business.

23. Start a business for profit.

24. Purchase or sell business equipment.

25. Invest in mutual funds for first time.

26. Sell personal property at a profit.

27. Sell securities.

28. Sell / buy personal residence.

29. Invest in rental property.

30. Create a living trust.

31. Last but not least, received a notice from the I.R.S.
