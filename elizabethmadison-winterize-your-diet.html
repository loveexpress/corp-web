Winterize Your Diet

By Elizabeth Madison

So you spent the summer eating healthy and exercising outdoors. Well, now it’s getting colder, and the days are getting shorter. You’ll be indoors more often, which can mean less exercise and cravings for heavier comfort foods. ‘Winterize’ your diet to stay on track with your good habits as the temperature drops:

Be aware. If you’re getting bored with salads and starting to crave warmer, heartier meals, this is a signal for you to change your food plan. Start incorporating soups and stews into your weekly menu now.  Pull out your crockpot or slow cooker and start making delicious, filling one-pot meals. You can freeze these entrees in individual containers for healthy meals at the ready.

Break the rut. Who says you have to eat breakfast foods for breakfast? Why not try some black bean stew over a bed of brown rice and spinach, a baked sweet potato topped with almond butter and dried fruit, or a whole grain burrito with eggs, salsa and avocado? These easy prep, delicious meals are all high in protein, fiber and antioxidants that will keep you feeling full until lunch. Be creative!

Eat the rainbow. Take advantage of winter fruits and veggies to get in all your vitamins, minerals and antioxidants. Toss spaghetti squash with beans and tomatoes, roast Brussel sprouts with garlic and cauliflower, or make a tummy-warming carrot-parsnip soup. Blend green drinks daily that feature superfoods like kale and beets to keep your immune system humming.

Spice up your diet. Herbs and spices boost flavor and strengthen your immunity. Cinnamon, ginger and garlic, among others, contain medicinal compounds that are protective against viruses and bacteria. Add to green drinks, soups, stews and veggie dishes.

Lay off the sweet stuff. Eating sugar can lead to cravings for more sugar–adding unwanted pounds and wreaking havoc on your immune system!

Get distracted: make a list of non-TV related indoor recreational activities to distract you from mindless eating. If something is in your hand like knitting, a crossword puzzle or a good book, you’re less likely to sit and graze.

Have an exercise plan B: Always have a backup plan when you can’t get to the gym or exercise outdoors. Purchase (or borrow from the library) exercise DVDs. DVR free exercise programs on cable! Even if you just walk in place during commercials, the goal is to stay active!

Remember your goals: Write down your goals and post them on the refrigerator or the inside of the medicine cabinet along with your favorite scripture. This will help keep you focused.

These tips will keep you healthy and on track throughout the winter.
