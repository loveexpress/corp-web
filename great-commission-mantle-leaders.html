Great Commission "Mantle" Leaders

by Jim Biscardi Jr.

For many years as a workplace leader, I was responsible for leading and growing 25 subordinate supervisors who led and trained their people. Also, as President of New Jersey Christian Ministries (NJCM) from 2000 – 2011, the Lord gave us hundreds of instructors and exhibitors who mentored Convention attendees and provided them with the latest resources in almost every area of Christian mini­stry. It was my awesome privilege and responsibility to help thousands of Christians, from various parts of the Bo­dy of Christ, mature as leaders by receiving instruction in God’s Word. They left the Conventions with knowledge, skill, and materials to mentor others.

Many Christians are unaware of their importance to the Lord. Do you realize, for example, that you are a lea­der for Him? There are many different kinds of leaders: workplace supervisors, pastors, deacons, husbands and wives, parents and grandparents, teachers and coaches. Are you one of them? Then you are a leader. Are you a Christian believer? Then you are Christ’s ambassador, an officer in the army of the Lord! (2 Corinthians 5:20). And you’ve been given the Great Commission (Matthew 28: 18-20) to go and make disciples of all nations - to lead others to Christ and help them become mature believers. So you too are a leader.

In this article, I’d like to share some general qualities of Christ-like leaders we can imitate wherever we are in life or in the Church. The mantle garment, also translated as “cloak”, worn by all God’s prophets, helps us understand what is needed to effectively develop the people entrusted to us –children, spouses, teams, church members, other believers or unbelievers. The mantle was a sleeveless garment made from theskins of animals with the hair left on it. (2 Kings 2:12-14).

The mantle helps us know what’s needed for people to be productive, empowered, and creative, who “live by faith” and who take responsibility and feel accountable for their actions. It shows us how to “free” our people to be all they can be in their roles as employees, church members, children and grandchildren, students, Chrisian believers, etc. Je­sus clearly demonstrated these same qualities, and left them as a legacy for us to emulate.

Here are some things the mantle shows us:

The mantle served the pro­phet, it wasn’t served. Besides using it as a cloak, the pro­phet might have used it as a pillow or small shelter. So our attitude as leaders is one of servant-leader. In John 13: 1-17, Jesus washed the feet of His disciples. He then told them [and us] to wash one another’s feet - to do as He had shown them. He said that they would be happy if they did this. Jesus said the greatest person in the kingdom of God is the servant of all. (Luke 9:48; Mark 9:35).

The mantle also provi­ded warmth for the pro­phet. We leaders need to give our people the warmth of knowing that they are our greatest asset and highest concern. We need to take the time [like Jesus always did] to get to know them and let them know us. God came in the flesh to show us who He was - to gain a heart-to-heart commitment from us. (John 10: 10, 11, 14, 15). We leaders need to build a heart-to-heart relationship with those entrusted to us.

The mantle also provi­ded protection from the elements. It couldn’t have holes that would let the rain in or be so thin that it gave no resistance to the wind. It needed to have “integrity.” As the prophet depended upon his mantle and gained confidence in what it could do, so our people need to be able to depend upon our sound judgment and wisdom. James 3:17 tells us that the “wisdom from above” means among other things having pure motives, being compassionate with failure, and gaining respect by being fair, genuine, and keeping our word.

A mantle isn’t able to “push” anything - a cloth garment has no pushing power. It can, however, be tied around something and pull it. We leaders need to have this “pull power”-like Jesus.  He always says things like “follow me” (John 12:26), and “come unto me” (Matthew 11:28). All believers need to have this “pull power” to attract the lost, unite believers, and disciple each other.

If we embrace these “mantle” leadership qualities, our resulting actions will send this message to our people: “Follow me as I follow Christ.” (1Corinthians 11:1). Several actions that result are: Being a good listener; Building up and encouraging our people; Being loyal and building loyalty; Promptly informing our people, and involving them in setting goals; Building uni­ty and family; Never taking our people’s good performance for granted; Providing timely feedback; Developing in our people a heart for serving others.

In these last days, all believers need to be Christ-like leaders willing to tie themselves to others in the pro­cess of discipleship - to be ready to lead as the Lord brings the harvest of new believers into our churches. Don’t leave that work to the pastors and deacons alone.

Let’s be the Lord’s “believer leaders” and emulate His “mantle” leadership qualities. And let’s also maintain a “new wineskin” heart – one that will stretch as the Lord continues pouring His truth into us. (Luke 5: 36-39).
