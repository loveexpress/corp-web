BattleCity 2009

From Event To Movement

by Jack Redmond
BattleCry Co-Chairman

As BATTLECRY returned for its second year to the NY Metro Region, some wondered if there would be a let down in attendance or the power of the event. Both of those questions were answered by a sold out arena of over 13,700 teens and leaders, and wave after wave of the move of God! Friday began with powerful worship as teens from NY & NJ opened up with a local youth mass choir consisting of many teens from different churches led by James Morales, worship leader from Christ Tabernacle.

Later on Ron Luce shared a message and asked the teens to make a commitment to Jesus. Teens poured down the aisles, filled the front area and then filled up the aisles and kept coming! It got to the point where Ron asked them to kneel down wherever they were and pray because there was no more room for them to go to!

Israel Houghton then began worship again and the arena exploded. A one song transition turned into a powerful time of worship. Jentezen Franklin then preached on the Spirit of the Python. In 12 years of ministry, this may have been the most talked about sermon I have ever seen as teens really examined their lives and committed to make changes.

As I sat in the Steering Committee box with another leader from the west coast who works extensively with crusades, he said that he had never seen that type of energy at an event like this go on for so long into the night!

Saturday continued with Tye Tribbett, Unhindered, Jeremy Camp, Hezekiah Walker and LeCrae each leading times of praise, worship or rap along with speakers Pastor Adam Durso, Erwin McManus and Ron Luce once again. Last year, BATTLECRY was a new and exciting event where many people came because of the names involved, this year in addition to the great ministries, teens and leaders embraced the event and BATTLECRY movement!

Let’s continue to share the message of Christ with every teen in the NY Metro region and train teens to be ministers to their generation!

As Co-Chairman of BATTLECRY and local Youth Pastor, I am committed along Pastor Adam Durso, BATTLECRY Co-Chairman and many others to continue to network our youth ministries, train our teens and leaders, and share with congregations the importance of building a strong youth ministry in every church!

Larry Acosta from Urban Youth Workers Institute spoke to 1200 youth leaders and pastors at the Youth Leader training along with Edwin Pacheco from 20/20 Vision and the Coalition of Urban Youth Workers. Davie Tyree, from the NY Giants then encouraged these leaders. BATTLECRY will be returning next year April 16th–17th to the Izod Center! You can pre-order tickets at battlecry.com or by calling: 903-324-8970.

If you would like to be connected to ongoing Youth Ministry Events and trainings, go to www.4thgen.org or send an e-mail to info@4thgen.org to be added to an e-mailing of ongoing regional youth events. This will help you stay connected or get involved with the many growing and thriving youth ministries in our region. There is a generation to be won to Christ!
