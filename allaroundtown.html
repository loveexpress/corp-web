AllAroundTown

The Praise ship is on the move once again. Thanks to gas prices continue to be at an all-time low Praise the Lord.

Our first stop was in Springfield Gardens, Queens at Antun’s Banquet Hall for the 10th anniversary and celebration of citizenship for Pastor Trina K. Thompson. I’ve had the honor and plea­sure to be a guest at many church banquets, anniversaries, and all types of celebrations, but I must say Worship Center International knows how to celebrate.  Pastor T. Thompson and her congregation took worship to a whole new level. They presented different varieties of ministries to minister to us, but the word from Overseer Bishop Dr. C. Redmon even more delightful. It was a night filled with laughter, tears, and praise for such an awesome wo­man of God, who loves her people and loves God even more. Thank you for the invite and making me feel so welcome among friends.

Next stop was in Manhattan, at BMI for the Cd release listing celebration for none other than the Planet Brooklyn’s very own son, Professor James Hall and Worship and Praise (WAP). Listening to James Hall and WAP’s new CD is truly going to be one of the best of this season; 28 years and still going strong. I sat down with James whom I’ve known for so long that we consider each other family. I asked the Professor how long you have been playing. He re­plied “Since the age of 5 trained and I am trained in classical music”. Yes, he is really the real deal. I went on to ask James what advice would you give musicians today. He stated “Musicians today should start with the basics, know your hymns, make sure you have a pra­yer life so that you can continue to hear from God and what He has to say and what He desires to hear.   He went on to say other groups have their sound, but his group has a sound that God has anointed them with whether it is 20 or 5 the tone is the same and different from anyone. That sound has gi­ven WAP their longevity. I agree, his style is like no other; always classy and well put together.  The CD is all of that and then some, my favorite is “Sovereign” because in this particular song, it states God does exactly what he wants to do, mighty words to live by.

Next we landed in Allentown PA at the Resurrection Worldwide Fellowship with Overseer Elise Lawrence. My 1st Lady and Prophetess J. Williams preached like a crazy woman for God and gave a prophetic word for the season of that house. Our Apostle L. Williams also ministered and took the service to a whole new level. I believe this formidable couple is a force to be reckoned with.

The Praise ship’s last stop at Studio 21 in New York City for the CD release concert of one of my favorite psalmist Deitrick Haddon presenting his new CD called ”Masterpiece.” This is one CD that must be in your collection. Deitrick shares music and ministry straight from his heart.  Deitrick always brings you to another level of excellence in Christ and music. I honestly feel honored when Deitrick complimented me for being a true friend to him and his ministry for over 20 years. He still recognized me after not seeing me for about 5 years. I thank God that I am not new to the industry, but true to the industry. I try to support a many as possible, but know this; I support those who invest in me. Merry Christmas to eve­ryone and remember to keep Christ in Christmas.

Dr. Gee
