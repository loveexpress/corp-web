AllAroundTown

by Ms Gee myprayereagle@aol.com

Once again the Praise Ship has taken off for parts known and unknown. With gas slowly going back down, the opportunity to spread our wings and territory is getting better every day.

        First stop was in Queens at the Vetro by Rus­so’s on the Bay, for a tribute to the Virtuous woman presented by Greater St. Paul Women’s ministry & Shepherd’s board, where Pastor M. Hodges is the Senior Pastor. I was so honored to be invited by my best friend, Pastor Karen Connor-Wilson, one of the honorees. It is always wonderful to see a group of women who has worked in the vineyard and rewarded for their work done. Some of the women honored were Mother Williams, Co-Pastor Adrian Knight, Pastor C. Croutch, Apostle Anita Shiver Kennedy and many others. The night was filled with singing and the awesome Jones Sister ministry in dance. We thank Pastor Hodges for thinking of others and spreading the joy among women who sometimes may feel overlooked.

        Then we landed back in Brooklyn in the backyard of the Calvary BC of Red Hook, where Rev. Dr. John A. Credle Jr. is the Pastor, for a play called “For Better or For Worse,” written by Racquel M. Jones. This play depicts  what some folks go through every day making the wrong decisions.God gives us choi­ces and it is up to us to choose wisely. This play is worth investing in to see. This play is well written and the acting superb. A bonus was added to the show in form of Bettina Dowtin, whose sing­ing can make the angels in heaven rejoice.This play gets my vote. Please support it. It’s worth your time.

        Flying from Red Hook across Brooklyn to the Mt. Zion P.H.C. where Apostle G. Scolfield is the Senior Pastor for a platform service, where Dr. D (Elder-elect) was one of the speakers. If you know anything about Dr. D then you know he is an excellent teacher. His topic was “The Blood Justifies.” He really is a prolific teacher, I am not just bragging because he’s my husband. The other speakers did a wonderful job as well, they really came forth mightily.  

     While still on the Planet, the Praise Ship landed at the Faith Tabernacle Mission Church for NYC CCFM Kingdom Conference hosted by Bishop & Co-Pastor Knight. We were there for the final night where Bishop Blue, of Marion SC, wretched the


place. It is always a blessing when a minister can tune into your brokenness as he tuned into mine, God bless you Bishop Blue. The Ministry of Maurette Brown Clark and David Bratton and God’s blueprint set the atmosphere. If you were not in the room you truly missed a treat. Bishop Blue is a man of God who knows and lives the Word of God.

        Next stop was in Brooklyn at Greater Temple of Praise, where Bishop Jeffrey White is Senior Pastor, for the 24th Church Anniversary of “Oil of Joy Ministries,” Inc, where the Senior Pastor is Apostle Dr. Patricia A. Wiley. During their celebration, they honored some awesome women in ministry, (see pho­to) and Pastor Darius Nixon of Inner City Prophetic Mini­stries brought such a prolific word from God. He is such an amazing man of God.

        Last stop was in Levittown, LI, at Old Country Buffet for the 83rd birthday celebration for my mother, 1st Lady Lillie M. McCray. This occasion was put toge­ther by my mother’s granddaughters who did a won- derful job. The torch has been passed from her children to the next generation. I thank God that my mom is still here to see her great grand-children and that her family can give her flowers while she can smell them.

Dr. Gee
