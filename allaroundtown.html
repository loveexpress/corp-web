FEATURES

AllAroundTown

by Ms Gee
myprayereagle@aol.com

The praise ship has taken off again; first stop was in Hempstead, NY for the “Word of Faith Deliverance Ministry” with the beautiful anointed host Pastor Betty Brown. This really was a beautiful luncheon with a room full of powerful anointed women. Their theme for this conference was “I hear the sound of the Abundance of rain.” Pastor Brown is one of the most humble and loving wo­man I have ever come across and I just love to be in her presence. The luncheon started out with a flag dance of praise by Teisha Moore, who danced unto the Lord and also gave a short word about the rain coming, a change is about to come.

Prophetess Francine Norman of Dallas, Texas, a true anointed woman of God gave us a mighty word from God to keep us and sustain us. She went on to say we need to reconstruct our inter-structure and form a dream team to help us complete the vision God has given us. The five points were: you need a confidant, coach, friend, play mate and a Judas. These are the five points she elaborated on and opened our eyes and ears to better understanding of what vision God has given us and what to do with it. I thank you Pastor Brown for the invite. It was just what I needed.

Next stop was in Jamaica, NY at the New York Hilton at JFK Airport for the Intercessory Ministry International Seventeenth Annual Prayer breakfast. Our host Rev. Newton Gabbidon started this ministry with wanting to formulate a pra­yer band for the people of Jamaica, started with family members and friends and expanded to churches all over the world to include everyone in prayer. Rev. Gabbidon introduced me to the speaker of the hour Bi­shop Joseph Garlington, PhD. Bishop Garlington is the Senior Pastor at Covenant Church of Pittsburgh, a multi-cultural and cross-cultural community of three thousand strong. This church was founded by Bishop Garlington and his wife. This was one of the times where you interview someone and you thoroughly enjoy the Q & A. Bishop Garlington is charismatic and anointed man of God. He made my job so easy for me. I really can understand why Bishop’s church has the growth it has because he is so easy going and knowledgeable. More than that, he loves people and meets the need of the people whether it is the youth, seniors, or cultural. Bishop Garlington I salute you.

Next stop the LE Praise ship made a solo flight back to the beautiful land of Har­lem NY. While Harlem’s very own radio station, WHCR 90.3FM (WHCR.org), was playing quite loudly in my headphones, I landed on 125th Street in the back of the LaGree Baptist Church for the homegoing service of my friend, the legendary Edgar Kendricks.

        Mr. Kendricks, to me Bro. Edgar, was entitled “the unofficial mayor of Sunday morning churchin.” I don’t think there’s a church in Har­lem that Bro Edgar hasn’t been their pianist or organist. As one of Harlem’s flashiest dressers and the 1st cousin to the Temptation’s Eddie Kendricks, during the late 1960s and early 1970s, Ed­gar led a house band at the Apollo Theater, where he performed with the likes of Marvin Gaye, O. C. Smith and Jerry Butler. He headed a gospel ensemble called “Listen My Brother”; a gospel group which included a teen­age Luther Vandross. He al­so wrote and arranged children’s songs for Sesame Street, including educational songs like “Count to 20” and “Song of the Alphabets.”

        It was a packed house as many of Edgar’s family, church family, friends, and fellow musicians came to pay homage to such a great man and musician. Many of Har­lem’s WHCR,  radio personalities and DJs, who played his music on a regular, was also on hand to celebrate his life. As I lifted off after the saying goodbye to his sisters Georgia and Ollie, I threw in one of Edgars CDs and played my favorite song all the way back to the Love Express office, “I’m Happy Tonight”. Bro. Edgar, we are all gonna miss you.

        Last stop was in Floral Park, NY at the Floral Terrace for the wedding of Marc and Christie Thomas, my babies. There are times in our lives where we grow so attached to certain people they become either your children or new parents. For me they have become my babies whom I am so very proud of. Marriage is not an easy institution, nor is it to be taken likely. Marc and Chris­tie honored God by becoming man and wife and be- coming one with God. May God bless this union and may it last until death do they part. Congrats to you both. Welcome back to school to all students and teachers.
Ms. Gee
