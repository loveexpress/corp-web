AllAroundTown

It’s that time again where the Praise ship takes off to places known and unknown.

Our first stop was in  Planet Brooklyn at the Brooklyn Senior nursing home. 

I know you are probably  saying  to  yourself, “why  is  Dr.  Gee  writing  about  something  that  happened  during  the Christmas holidays? To be honest the elderly is a passion of mine, I feel they are neglected by

society, as well as their families at times.  I was invited by Donna White, the Parent Coordinator for  PS  149,  where  the  principal  is  Ms.  Yvette  Donald.    Their  children’s  choir,  along  with  their music extraordinaire,  Mr.  Will  Mosley,  sang  and  danced  for  the seniors  and  put  the  biggest smiles  on  their  faces.    The  seniors  rocked in the chairs,  bobbed their head, clapped their hands and sang  along  with the children. After the children  performed, the  seniors screamed encore, encore.

The children gave out hand designed cards and gifts for each of the seniors,  and  even  took  pictures  with  them.  Christmas  is  about  giving  of  yourself  and  these children did this and more.   

Next  stop  was  at  Ginny’s  in  the  Village  of  Harlem  for  a  sit  down  brunch  with  Alex &  Stephen Kendrick who gave us the powerful movie War Room.

It was a wonderful time to be able to talk to the two brothers who work together to bring us such wonderful movies and explain how they even chose their location and who would be casted.     They both stated God orchestrated every step of the movie and on set they had  a  prayer  room  on  set  so  someone  was  always  before  God  for  the movie.      As  we  ate  we were  ministered  to  by  Damian  Sneed  and  the  Levites  who  had  the  place  upside  down.    If  you don’t know Damian Sneed and the Levites you better ask somebody.  My girl Liz Black was our host for the evening.  If you do not have the DVD War Room in your repertoire I would suggest you run, buy it, and watch it with your loved ones Next  stop  right  back  in  Brooklyn  for  a  Women  of  Power  fellowship  which  is  a  sisterhood where  women  embrace, educate, empower,  and  encourage  one  another  using the  gifts  and talents God has given each and every one of us.  This fellowship there is no big I’s and little u’s, just a bunch of women being there for each other and weathering life storms together.  If you are a female and want to have a place to share your dreams and don’t know how to begin, we invite you every first Monday of the month, with the exception of July and August- vacation time.    I  would  love  for  you  to  come  and  fellowship  with  us  at  1958  Fulton  Street,  near  Ralph Ave,  in  room  501.  We  thank  its  visionary,  Tonya  Lewis,  for  seeing  the need and  answering  the call.

Next  I  had  the  honor  and  the  pleasure  to  interview  our  newest author,  Pastor  Undena Jackson, Pastor of     

Power Praise Deliverance Church Inc. with her  self-autobiography book “Can You Dance Before the King Naked”.

All I can say her transparency in this book will make some of you  uncomfortable  especially  when  you  see  yourself`  in  this

book.    This  book  had  me  on  the end  of  my  chair  biting  my  nails,  which  I  never  do  having  nails  it’s  kind  of  difficult.    This  book

provokes  all  kinds  of  feeling  and  thoughts.      It  took  Pastor  Jackson  30  days  to  write  this  and years for it to come to manifestation.  I interview people and read their books all the time, but

this  book  is  so  much  more  than  the  interview.    This  book  will  change  lives  and  make  a  person want to change their ways, lifestyles and run to Jesus as quick as they can.  This book is not just an  autobiography,  but  a  reference  to  the  word  of  God,  every  page  uses  the  Word  of  God  as guide  to  apply  to  every  step  in  her  life.    If  it  were  not  for  the  Word  of  God,  I  can  truly  say  we would not have the pleasure to read this next number one best seller.

Lastly the LE Shuttlecraft, under the solo flight of Dr. D.,

landed in South Jamaica, Queens for a double-header   of   events.   First   we   had   the   honor   of   photographing   the “Night   of Remembrance”- at the  Crowe  Funeral  Home  where  Chaplin  Brewster  and  staff  hosted their annual event for the families they served in the past year. Being new to the funeral industry as a  funeral  photographer,  I  found  it  amazing  that  a  funeral  home would appreciate  those  they served  in  such  a  tangible  way.  Not  only  was  the service  beautiful  and  heart  touching,  but  gifts were  giving  out  to  the  children  from  under  the  Christmas  tree  and  gift  cards  were  raffled to adults.  If you’re a  funeral  home  with  special  services  like  these,  share  with  our readers  by  having  our  staff photographer shoot it.

Finally  we  head  over  to  our  friends  at  the  Bethel  Gospel  tabernacle  with  Bishop  and  1st  Lady

Caesar.  Their  guest  artists  that  night  was  Kurt  Carr.

Between  his  great  singing  and  life  testimonies  of  how  God  inspired  him  to  write,  saints  left there inspired and giving God thanks. One of the highlights of the night was the mother and son talents  of  the  Kurt  Carr  singer  member  Timiney  Figueroa  and  her son Miles Cantor  Not  only  did  they sing  well  together,  but  little  Miles held  his  own.  Bishop  and  1st  Lady  Caesar. Thank you not only for sharing God’s Word through all those creative plays, inspiring sermons and  concerts  over  the  years,  but  your  continued  support of  us  here  at  the  Love  Express.  As  a fellow  photographer,  I  truly  enjoy  our  times  of  sharing  and photography  talks  Bishop  Caesar and  look  forward  to  traveling  with  you  one  day  to  capture  other  aspects  of  your  ministry worldwide. May God continue to bless those good folks over at Bethel Gospel.

Dr. Gee
