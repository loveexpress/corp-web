Combat Child Obesity OR
Kids Eat Right Month

By Elizabeth Madison

September is Kids Eat Right Month, an initiative to combat childhood obesity, launched by the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics as an extension of First Lady Michelle Obama’s Let’s Move campaign. In 2012, more than 33% of children and adolescents were overweight or obese. Obesi­ty can lead to heart disease, type 2 diabetes, asthma, and sleep apnea. Preventing childhood obesity starts in the home, with parents taking the lead. As it says in Proverbs 22:6, Direct your children onto the right path, and when they are older, they will not leave it (NLT). Creating a healthy home involvement and role modeling healthy habits helps everyone eat, feel and live better. Here are 6 strategies to get your kids – and the whole family - to eat right:

Plan a weekly menu. Planning is the key to healthy lifestyles. You can’t wing it because that increases the likelihood of making unhealthy choices. At the beginning of each week create a menu, and then shop based on that menu. Try a new healthy food and recipe every week to expand your options. Check out www. MeatlessMonday.com for easy healthy recipes.

Shop on a full stomach. If you and the kids are hungry, you’ll both want to buy unhealthy foods. Have a snack before you hit the store.

Make family time. Get everyone involved in planning and prepping for meals. This is a great opportunity to involve picky eaters, as they are more likely to try new foods if they have a say in the decision-making process. You’ll also reinforce children’s reading and math skills by having them read directions and measure foods. When enjoying the meal, be sure to turn off all distractions.

Healthy eating. Incorporate more fruits and vegetables to every meal: add a fruit to breakfast, and make ½ your plate veggies at lunch and dinner for everyone—not just the kids. Crackdown on sugary, fatty and fried foods. Instead, opt for fresh fruit to aid your sweet tooth; choose baked, grilled or broiled foods instead of fried.  

Get going. Schedule some physical activity time at least 2-3 times a week for at least 30-40 minutes. Jam out together to favorite tunes, take a nature walk, wash the car together or walk the dog. Visit www.letsmove.gov for more ideas. 

Pray.  Good health starts in the spiritual realm, so make time for family prayer so that everyone has the same mindset for a healthy home.
