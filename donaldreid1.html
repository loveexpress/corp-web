How to Fix Tax Errors!

By Donald Reid, CFP
www.reidtax.com

 The Tax Season has come and gone but in a sense it is still not over yet. Reason: The letters are now beginning to come in from Uncle Sam telling us what we did wrong on our tax returns.

Here are some advices which we have to offer to assist you in answering some of the most commonly asked questions regarding tax errors and/or omissions.

Amended Returns

 In order to “fix” or correct errors on a tax return which has already been filed we are required to submit an amen­ded 1040 Form called 1040X.

Generally, Form 1040X must be filed within 3 years after the date of the original return was filed, or within 2 years after the date the tax was paid, whichever is the later date.

Uncle Sam may give you more time to submit your Form 1040X, if you are physically and/or mentally unable to manage your financial affairs.

Keeping Records

We are required to keep copies of our tax returns and all the accompanying documents until the statute of limitations for those returns expire.

However, copies of important documents, for example,the closing statements on property purchased, ought to be kept in a permanent file, for both income tax and/or estate tax purposes.

Needless to say, it is near impossible to survive an audit without adequate books and records to justify either our income and/or expenditures.

Travel Expenses

Business travel, meal and entertainment expenses are not deductible unless you properly substantiate your expenses as follows: (A) Time and place of travel or entertainment; (B) A dollar amount spent per activity; (C) Business relationship of participants; (D) A business purpose of activity.
