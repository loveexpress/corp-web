Editorial: Persistent Prayers

Editor & Publisher: Edith Stranges

As we look back on 2015 we realize how much we needed the Lord and His awesome po­wer and mercy on us. I have written about pioneers many times, yet I sense we the readers and pastors need to be reminded of what is a pio­neer.

Pioneers are mostly misunderstood and their efforts of pioneering are often trivial. As a result many would-be pioneers give up and throw in the towel before they reach their goal.

Being a pioneer isn’t ea­sy. Being the first, the forerunner tackling unchar­ted territory and paving the way; a pioneer is always looking to build, occupy and doing tasks that require, courage, stamina, perseverance, patience,  endurance and faith.

Sometimes we forget about the failures and setbacks that eventually come to be stepping stones on the way to glory. The road is of­ten filled with unexpected hurdles, unprecedented hardships and haters. People who aren’t pioneers don’t appreciate pioneers. It requires discipline, confidence and knowing how to keep secrets.

Through the years I’ve seen and met many folks who have a dream to fulfill but, they don’t wait on the Lord. They jump into the ri­ver and in a very cocky attitude they swim into a swamp and their dream is swallowed up by crocodiles. They must wait on the Lord and He will fulfill the desires of their heart. The Lord is testing you. Repent and start that dream again, and wait on the Lord and listen to godly counsel. Having failures does not make you a failure in the New Year.

Remember to fast and pray Ja­nu­ary 4th-February 2nd for the nation, the city, yourchurch and pastors  and for you to have a deep love for souls.

God bless, Edith
