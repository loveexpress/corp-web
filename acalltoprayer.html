A Call To Prayer

By Evang. Sheilah A. Vickers

Join us as we cry out for anyone in ministry and their families. The Bible warns us of end times when there will be an all-out assault from the enemy against spiritual leaders. Satan knows what we must be reminded of today: “‘I will strike the Shepherd, and the sheep will be scattered,’” (Mark 14:27, NKJV). I wholeheartedly believe that unless we learn to provide our spiritual leaders with a protective shield of prayer, our churches and ministries will never fully change our cities with the gospel.

Hundreds of Pastors, like you, were surveyed by a popular Christian magazine and resulting numbers are staggering:

97% say that they were inadequately trained for the challenges they face

80% say pastoral ministry has a negative effect on their children

71% say they are in financial trouble

70% say they constantly fight depression

70% say they feel underpaid

70% say they do not have someone they consider a close friend

65% say they have thought about quitting the ministry within the last thirty days.

A crisis is at hand where pastors are suffering from massive heart attacks, strokes and unfortunately some are succumbing to suicide. I believe that God is calling believers to pray prayers of protection over their leaders so the entire church can prevail against the attack of the enemy, and move forward and take cities for God. When we support our leaders with prayer it protects them from enemy fire so they can advance and make a way for us to come into our places.

It is my confidence that reconciliation, financial provision, miracles, healings and personal breakthroughs more than ever before will occur if we will support those whom God has placed over us. When we back our leaders, we are victorious, and the gospel goes forth in new power.

I am writing to ask that your congregation join ministries of the NY tri-state area in prayer for you and every Pastor, Ministry Leader and their families across the world! This is not a membership campaign nor will it be a denominational or political rally. We will gather to PRAY, PRAISE and WORSHIP and return to our local assembly. We would be honored if you would join us also if your schedule permits. If not, we would appreciate participation from your congregation.

By Evang. Sheilah A. Vickers, Mason Temple COGIC. Supt. Arcell Vickers, Sr., Pastor.
