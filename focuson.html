Focus On The Children:

Silent No More! - Love, Love, Love!

by Elder Charlotte Hall

Hello Beloved! Love is in the air. Really??? "What's love got to do with anything, you ask? My answer would be EVERYTHING! The love that we're going to think about now is not a feeling, it's a decision and an action. Right now the world is gearing up for that once a year all out, Love Day. We can be so thankful that God doesn't just choose to demonstrate His love for us once a year. But what about us?  Do we demonstrate the love of Christ that is written for us in I Cor. 13., the action part. Did you know that Va­lentine's day began with an act of love. Love is not just a warm fuzzy feeling and a million calories of yummy cho­colate, ( that we "All" delight in.) Love is an Action and a year round endeavor. Words without action is just vapor.

In the year 302 A.D., the early church set aside February 14th to remember the heroic death of a man named Valentine, who was behea­ded on a pagan altar because he refused to renounce Jesus Christ. According to historic tradition, Valentine was born into wealthy Ro­man family, and converted to Christianity as a young man, sometime around 240 A.D. It was an era when the church was being severely persecuted by the degenerate Ro­man emperor, Claudius ll.  Thousands of Christians were imprisoned in the dark, tomblike jails of Rome. Wealthy Romans of the time considered it "entertainment" to visit the prisons and watch as Christians were tortured and put to death. Valentine took full advantage of his family connections to visit the prisons too-but he made it his mission to smuggle in small gifts of food to those facing martyrdom, always accompanied by a short note expressing LOVE and encou- ragement.

Valentine, devout and strong-hearted as he was, eventually got arrested himself. He was martyred in Rome about 270 A.D. Though the origin and tradition of Va­lentine's day is rarely spoken about or even remembered or disguarded, it's rooted in Christianity. Look what ACTS of real LOVE demonstrated by one man, that we know of, has produced.

On this Valentine's Day, share this valuable history with your children, grand children, co-workers and friends, don't be silent or politically correct. Think about this opportunity to reach out to persons that may be shut-in, or a Sunday school class, children that are hospitalized and/or un-churched. The field is white and we are the labo­rers. What a perfect time to share the real love of Christ, the Christian roots of this day and the salvation message. That ought to give the devil a migraine! Remember in December, we said that Jesus is the reason for EVERY season. Well, here He is again. His love is the reason for this Valentine's season too, and its only the second month of the New Year. So be bold and shout it from the housetops. Love rejoices in truth! 1 Co­rinthians 13: 6. Happy V-Day! But thanks be to God, which giveth us the VICTORY through our Lord Jesus Christ.  1 Corinthians 15:57.
