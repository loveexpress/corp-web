7 Superfoods for Weight Loss

By Elizabeth Madison

If weight loss is your goal this year, then start strong by adding these 7 Superfoods into your daily plan. They are all full of nutrients that will stoke your metabolism and keep you feeling full. Added bonus: they are full of antioxidants for a strong immune system.

Beans and legumes. Jazz up salads, stews, soups, burgers or even breakfast daily for a big boost in protein and fiber without the ex­tra cholesterol, keeping hun- ger at bay. Popular favo­rites include chickpeas (garbanzo beans), black beans, red kidney beans, split peas, and lentils.

Eggs are excellent sources of quality protein and contain many nutrients including vitamins B, A, D, E, K, iron and other minerals. Only 70 to 80 calories each, they keep you full with only 2 grams of saturated fat. If you have high cholesterol, three whole eggs per week is the limit; otherwise up to 7 per week is fine.

Quinoa is a gluten-free, nutrient-packed powerhouse, containing fourteen times the amount of fiber and five times the amount of protein as white rice. Like eggs, quinoa is a complete protein source (it provides all 8 essential amino acids) AND is also a good source of iron, magnesium, vitamin E, and potassium.

Avocados contain fiber and B vitamins, and are high in “good” fat, boosting heart health and fullness. Moderation is key however:  a 1/4 of a Hass avocado will cost you 60 calories.

Cauliflower is so versatile!! High in vitamin C and antioxidants, you can mash it for a low-carb version of mashed potatoes or dice it up to pass it off as a low-carb “fried rice.” Even better, enjoy it raw as a crunchy snack. Because it takes longer to chew than other foods, it allows extra time for your brain to stop you from overeating.

Bulgur is a quick cooking form of whole wheat, taking 10 minutes or less. It is a great substitute for white rice and pasta, boasting high fi­ber, iron, vitamins E and B6. Fiber not only keeps you full longer, it helps prolongs the insulin response so you don’t have blood sugar spikes. Vitamins E and B6, and iron are all important nutrients for metabolism, helping you stay energized.

Greek yogurt is high in protein and contains important nutrients like calcium, vitamins D, B12, B6, potassium, and phosphorous, and of course, probiotics. Like other weight loss Superfoods, the protein will keep hunger at bay, while B vitamins work hard to keep you energized.
