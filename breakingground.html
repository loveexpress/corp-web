FEATURES

“Breaking Ground For Kingdom Building”

Rev. Dr. Clarence J. Baker

Nehemiah 2:18
“Then I told them of the hand of my God which was good upon me; as also the king’s words that he had spoken unto me. And they said, ‘Let us rise up and build.’ So they strengthened their hands for this good work.”

Trinity Pentecostal Church of God is a chartered affiliate of the Pentecostal Church of God International, headquartered in Joplin, Missouri. We have been serving the City of Paterson since 1983 under the leadership of Rev. Dr. Clarence J. Baker.

From the humble beginnings of a 9-person prayer meeting in a living room to a vibrant congregation of over 300, we have seen numerous demonstrations of God’s faithfulness and favor.

Like many other growing churches in Paterson, Trinity has seen her share of cyclical challenges. She is constantly making adjustments in order to accommodate the growing demands presented by the congregation, the community and the economy.

Our Mission Statement, executed under God, is our driving principle which keeps us both consistent and relevant. Relevance requires change that is often painful but that pain is tempered when change comes in the form of a vision that is clearly communicated and properly executed.

A vision that was conceived in the mind of the Pastor ashe was confronted with the constant demands of a growing church, lack of available resources and the need for appropriate accommodation. A conversation with the Pastor revealed that he has been pondering this vision for ten years and, after numerous agonizing prayers, with no release in his spirit, he decided just 3 years ago, to share it with his executive board.

Two of the most asked questions we’ve received since the announcement of this project are: 1). Why build in Paterson? 2). How can we build in this economy?

In response to these questions I will quote directly from the pastor’s answers during his interview.

1. Why did you decide to stay in the City of Paterson? Pastor: There is a great need in Paterson, NJ. I don’t feel that the Lord led me anywhere else. There is a special burden for this community and I strongly feel that God wants us to stay here so He can work through us in order to lift that burden and touch lives.

2. How are you able to build in this economy? Pastor: We are able to build because our mission is independent of the economic state. We have prayed, we have planned and we have prepared. Furthermore, the present facility does not have the necessary space we need whenever we organize and host large events. The cost of acquiring external venues always proves counterproductive to our financial goals.

Finally, we are a growing church and it is critically important that we create infrastructures capable of providing hope, help, and a home to not only our congregants, but to other churches and especially, to the community we serve. This is the reason we are hosting our Groundbreaking Ceremony for the expansion of our facility. This will take place on April 2, 2011 at 550 East 23rd Street, Paterson, NJ 07514. A warm welcome awaits all!

Submitted by Marisa A. Damallie.
