A Christian Perspective of Our Nation's Racial Tension.

By Sherri Sharpe

When I was a young teen perhaps thirteen years old or so, my Father God blessed me to be the one chosen to deliver the message for our “Youth Day” at Mt. Lebanon Baptist Church in Peekskill, New York. I recall it was a very volatile time in our then small town, located in the beautiful Hudson River Valley of upper New York State. The turmoil arose from racial tension that stemmed from a shooting which had recently taken place in a local bar.

During that time (the mid-sixties), murders were a rare occurrence in our town. So when a young African-American teen by the name of Wayne Buffalo had been shot by a White man (whose name I do not recall), the news spread rapidly and became the “talk of the town.”

It was in this kind of climate of brewing unrest, hatred, and racial strife, that God gave me, a young teen­ager, a platform from which to express my views concerning this crisis. I now understand why my Father gave me this opportunity from which to speak at such a significant time. It was because He knew that I would speak forth the words that He put in my heart and mind to speak. He knew that although I was still practically a child, I would speak forth the words of wisdom and virtue that He gave me to express to His dear people in our local church.

....So I did; when I sat down to write out my sermon, God flooded my heart with His insights and His perspective. As I wrote down these ideas, somehow I knew in my heart that I was writing God’s will and God’s viewpoint of all the confusion and turmoil that was transpiring in our city.

So on “Youth Sunday” when I stood up to deliver my message; this is the basic theme of what God had put in my heart to communicate. I said that we as a race of people should not resort to hate because of the murder that took place. I said that it was wrong for us to blame the entire white race for the act of one individual. I reminded the people in my congregation that it was not an entire race of people that pulled the trigger that killed Wayne Buffalo, but that it was only one man that committed this terrible crime!

Now beloved, although several decades have pass­ed since I preached my very first sermon as a youth, we find ourselves once again reliving the same racial tension in our nation that many of us recall from the 1960s’. Once again my Father has given me a message to share with His people straight from His heart. The only difference is that now, as a born-again disciple of Jesus, and a Minister of the Gospel, the message my Father has given me is taken directly from His holy written Word.

“Be submissive to every human institution and authority for the sake of the Lord, whether it be to the emperor as supreme or to governors as sent by him to bring vengeance (punishment, justice) to those who do wrong and to encourage those who do good service.

For it is God’s will and intention that by doing right (your good and honest lives) should silence (muzzle, gag) the ignorant charges and ill-informed criticisms of foolish persons.

[Live] as free people, [yet] without employing your freedom as a pretext for wic­kedness, but [live at all times] as servants of God.

Show respect for all men(Emphasis mine) [treat them honorably]. Love the brotherhood (the Christian fraternity of which Christ is the Head.) Reverence God. Honor the emperor.

[You who are] household servants, be submissive to your masters with all [pro­per] respect, not only to those who are kind and considerate and reasonable, but also to those who are surely (overbearing, unjust, and croo­ked.) (Emphasis mine)

For one is regarded favorably (is approved, acceptable, and thankworthy) if, as in the sight of God, he endures the pain of unjust] suffering. (Emphasis mine)

[After all] what kind of glory [is there in it] if, when you do wrong and are punished for it, you take it patiently? But if you [bear patiently] with suffering [which results] when you do right and that is undeserved, it is acceptable and pleasing to God. For even to this were you called [it is inse­parable from yourvocation]. For Christ also suffered for you, leaving for you [His personal] example, so that you should follow in His footsteps.

(Emphasis mine) 1 Peter 2:13-21 (Amplified Version)

To help put these ver­ses into our current context, please re-read this same passage with the following substitutions: (1) Replace “emperor” with President; (2) Replace “governors” with policeman and women; (3) Replace “household servants” with employees; (4) Replace “masters” with employers, managers, and supervisors.

I realize beloved that what God is exhorting us to do in these verses is not politically correct, popular, or even easy to obey. However, as believers in and followers of Jesus, we do not have the luxury of picking and choosing which Scriptures we’re going to obey. If we are genuine, committed disciples of Jesus, then we will be willing to submit our own ideas, wills, opinions, emotions, and attitudes to God’s way of conduct and lifestyle for us which are clearly outlined in the Bible!

We are called to live according to a much higher standard than the world. And those of us in the Body who are in positions of leadership, whether apostles, pro­phets, evangelists, pastors, or teachers, are called to an even higher standard than the laity of the Church! (See James 3:1)

Therefore, the prevailing question for Christians today is, “How are we to respond to the racial tension and turmoil taking place in our nation today?” The answer is simple. We are to be the “light of the world” and the “salt of the earth.” However, we cannot be salt and light apart from the Word of God. Remember, the Word is a “lamp to our feet and a light to our path.” First and foremost we are called to pray. However, after we pray, we must seek out God’s wisdom found in the Scriptures so that we will not become guilty of following the evil ways of the world. Concerning racial tension, we are not to take the side of either the Blacks or the Whites. We as Christians are to take the side of RIGHTEOUSNESS and TRUTH found only in the written Word of God! We must show respect and honor to all men, especially those in positions of authority. We are not to hate or criticize White policemen or politicians we disagree with or disapprove of. We are to show respect to all leaders whether civic, state, or national, and teach our children to do the same. Our respect for authority is not based on their level of integrity and, or justice. The Bible clearly states in the above passage that we are to honor all men, whether they are just or unjust; and that if we suffer unjustly, then God will honor and bless us!

On a recent Christian talk radio program, a gentleman called in who referred to himself as Bishop. During his discourse with the talk show host, who is a fellow Christian, he accused the host of being influenced by the KKK,” simply because the host was saying that as Christians we should be willing to wait to hear all the facts, before passing judgment on the Michael Brown case in Ferguson, Missouri!

Our Bishop brother did not realize that he was of the wrong spirit! And so many others of our precious brothers and sisters in Christ have adopted the wrong worldly spirit of hatred and prejudice against White policemen or even against White people in general. This is wrong for it is allowing their emotions to be manipulated by Satan, who is the spirit of strife and division!

We do not have to condone or agree with all the darkness and evil going on around us. However, God has called us to be the solution to the world’s problems; we are not to become a part of the problem!

In conclusion beloved brethren, Jesus has warned us that in these “last days” (which we are obviously in now), there would be perilous times. We are in these times right now. But remember, as the world grows dar­ker and darker, the Church of Jesus Christ should be growing brighter and brigh­ter. However, the Church will only increase in as each one of us individual members allows our individual “light to shine!”

In my recent Sunday School class, each child had to share one way they were going to let their “light shine” for Jesus. I’m asking you be­loved to do the same thing. Please prayerfully consider at least one way you too can “let your light shine more brightly for Jesus” in this world of darkness, confusion, and turmoil.

Lastly, my challenge to you is to RESIST the temptation to take sides with the world; don’t be ruled by your emotions. Instead commit yourself to daily prayer, Bible study and drawing closer to God than you’ve ever been.

You’ll be surprised at how God will then begin to use you as a “voice” to speak forth His truth and wisdom, and His “Good News” to this lost and dying world!

Minister Sherri Sharpe
