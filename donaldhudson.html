From The Pastor's Desk

Paradigm Shift

by Pastor Donald Hudson, Common Ground Ministries

As I was with Moses, so shall I be with you, kept ringing in my head after the death of my father, Bishop Arnold Z. Hudson in October 2003.

Some of the things you do affect you for a moment, some a season and others for a lifetime. I believe that some of the problems in the body of Christ are because of a lack of true obedience to the mandate that God has given to us as leaders.

Joshua, take the shoes off your feet, means, humble yourself, listen and obey. In other words; "Do what I tell you, say what I give and go where I send you."

That in essence, was the terms under which, Jehovah God was willing to be with and lead Joshua and the children of Israel into the Promised Land.

The move of the Ministry from Brooklyn to Long Island does not mean we won't continue to be a blessing to our people in Brooklyn. But, I believe our obedience to God will allow us to be a greater blessing to Brooklyn, Queens, Manhattan, Bronx, Staten Island, Long Island and through out the Diaspora. As it was said to me, "That's what makes us Common Ground."

It has been asked of me, "Pastor do you believe you are following in your Grandfather's and father's footsteps?" I answered yes and this why; every leader must be in submission to God. Submission is in essence, the same from generation to generation, but the instructions may be different.

I share our paradigm shift with you because many churches, people and families are living and operating under generational curses. Obedience to God and his orders is what destroys Generational curses. Obedience to God means "Do not compromise!"

We are called the Joshua generation; the pronunciation for Jesus in the Hebrew language is Joshua. We are the generation that is to usher in the coming of the Lord Jesus Christ. By being subject to his teachings and following his example in submitting to God. By accepting Him as Lord and Savior.

So, for the sake of having a choir, the church has allowed many, knowingly bound by homosexuality, to direct the choir, and loose the spirit of homosexuality in the Church. So because we think we need music so bad, the church has allowed known whoremongers, drug addicts, alcoholics, and even drug dealers to play the music. Thus loosing the spirit of alcoholism, drug addiction and whoredom in the midst of the Church. As goes the Church, so goes the community.

I dare to say, when we as leaders, because of fear or out of the want of fortune and fame or maybe just to hear our praises ring, give up the authority that Jesus gave to us to be builders, by compromising, we become nothing more than pimps, hustlers, and hypocrites, prostituting the Church. This is what has put us under generational curses. So, our generation must do as our Fathers and their fathers did - "Dare to stand under the mandate of God, to destroy generational curses." Follow the order of God, do what He tells you, say what He gives you, and go where he sends you, following the example of Jesus, that's what brings the paradigm shift or rather a lifetime change. Jesus said in Mark 16:17, "And these signs shall follow them that believe; in my name shall they cast out devils....."
