Power Of One 3rd Annual Unity Day Festival

The Power of 1 Unity Day Festival was crea­ted to unite the community, church and city through corporate prayer, music, activities, vendors who provide delicious food from varied nationalities, merchandise of all types and valuable information from lo­cal and national brand corporations. The goal is to entertain, educate and enhance the quality of life within the community.

In 2012 after a bullet came through the window of Good Samaritan Church, which at that time was loca­ted on Avon Avenue in New­ark, NJ; the pastor gave Marvin Cratch a challenge to do something to change the community.God gave Marvin a vision to shut down the vio­lence on Avon Avenue just for one day and within 40 days the vision came into fruition with the help of New­ark Council President, Mildred C. Crump, then Council- woman-At-Large, Mayor Ras Baraka, then Newark South Ward Councilman and their teams, Good Samaritan Church and the participation of ten local pastors and their congregations. On that day men and women of God, community activist and city officials spoke and inspired the crowd and over 4,000 people attended. We had music and performers; more than 50 vendors provided food, merchandise and services; and the setup and cleanup crew was second to none. Unity Day was a big success with no rain, incident or confusion.

Our second festival, which was held on Saturday, September 28, 2013 with the theme "Healing the Land" from 2 Chronicles 7:14 was what this festival's main focus was all about. That year Marvin met Adrianne Wilson and formed the company “The Power Of One 3C Foundation.” We created a website and was able to have our headquarters at St. John’s Lutheran Church under the leadership of Rev. Dr. Appolles Sweatte located at 333 Avon Ave. As with the previous year, we closed Avon Avenue from Treacy Avenue to 14th Street - 8am to 8pm.  But at 9am the Shofar was blown, everything stopped and we all began to pray. On the main stage sin­gers, dancers, ministers and political leaders filled the airwaves with words of truth, encouragement and worship to God.  Healing took place with a God-inspired phenomenon as pastors and ministers of over 10 denominations gathered around a 10 foot wooden cross presented by the Franciscan Friars of the Renewal; and with a hammer and a nail every one of them nailed their difference to the cross, as a gesture of unification with a song of praise and fervent prayer. God did it once again and it is marvelous in our eyes! 

This is the year 5774 in the Hebrew calendar - the year of “Open Doors”.  Our 3rd year, and we have seen the mighty hand of God move in miraculous ways. The councilman of the Southward is now the Mayor of Newark and with this being one of his first major events as mayor, plans are underway to make this the largest and productive citywide event of its kind in Newark’s history. Thus, we have added plans to attempt to set a Guinness World Record for the Largest Praise Dance and are asking everyone to sign up on our site www.the powerof1unityday.org, wear your church colors and name with TShirts that we can provide. We also plan to facilitate group counseling sessions at our headquarters. As with the two previous years, activities and attractions include; a Main Stage with various Christian artist, such as soloist, groups & choirs, spoken word, and rap – prolific & inspirational speakers. A Kidz Zone, Health & Wellness Pavilion, Food Court, Horseback Rides, Communi­ty Choir and an awesome Praise Dance Ministry.

Our goal this year is to raise enough capital to open the “Amiri Baraka Outreach Center” This facility will provide - Jobs & Training, Transitional Housing, Food Dis- tribution and Counseling – To Decrease Crime, Restore Hope, & Transform Newark. Some ask, why we chose this name well as a Critic, Scholar, Civil Rights Activist, and Poet, we believe that God used Amiri Baraka to bring positive change and that is the mission of the Outreach Center.

An event of this magnitude takes a lot of time, mo­ney, and volunteers. There- fore, we are asking everyone to be a part of this God-Inspired event by going to our website www.thepowerof1 unityday.org; finding out what God wants you to do and then sign-up or donate!

The Power Of One 3C Foundation was created to establish a relationship between the Church, Community and City; To develop a platform were parishioners and neighbors can volunteer time and donate money toward projects that will assist in the redevelopment of their local community; and to promote fellowship among the local churches to become one body.

Evangelism Team

The aim of our Evangelism Ministry is to go out in to the community to share the Gospel of Christ. We encourage individuals to accept Jesus as their Lord and Savior and assist them to become His Disciples.

As the Power of One Unity Day Evangelism Team we plan to:

• Carry the saving and healing message of Jesus Christ to every nation and islands of the earth.

• Proclaim the whole Gospel of Jesus Christ to every person on earth in our generation.

• Recognize and support unity in the Body of Christ, so that the world will know we are His disciples.

• Operate, articulate and demonstrate to the world, the five-fold ministries of the Church as outlined in Ephesians 4:11 for the perfecting of the saints, for the work of the ministry, for the edifying of the Body of Christ.

If you have a passion for ministering to the needy and those who live in the surrounding areas, we welcome you into our Evangelism Ministry.
