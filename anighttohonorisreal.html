A Night to Honor Israel

We are excited to gather for the first ever NYC Night to Honor Israel on Tuesday, Sept. 15. We are so grateful to Pastor Terry Starks and the leadership of Crenshaw Christian Center–East for their partnership in hosting this gathering.

Just recently in the Bronx four men were apprehended planning to bomb local synagogues and local points of interest to security around New York City. Weeks ago at the Holocaust Museum in Washington, a white supremacist, anti-Semite, claiming the Holocaust didn’t happen, attacked and killed a security guard. The president of Iran continually denies the holocaust and threatens to wipe Israel off the map.

These times demand a response from believing Christians.

We as Christians cannot stand idly by, we have a biblical mandate to pray for the peace of Jerusalem, and to be the answer to our prayers through our demonstration of material support to the Jewish people (Romans 15:27). We are living in times not unlike the days predating WWII, and we need Dietrich Bonhoeffers and Corrie Ten Booms to rise up in our day.

We are living in an unprecedented hour.

Our heart is that this gathering would be a time of demonstrating our united love, support and solidarity for Israel and the Jewish people.

Join other Christians and Jewish leaders on this night @ 1 West 96th Street at Central Park West, NYC. Telephone: (212) 749-9323.
