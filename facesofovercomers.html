Faces of Overcomers: Collyde Summit - Joe & Charlene Narciso

Joe & Charlene Narciso

How do you rise above something that keeps getting bigger? How do you leave something in the past that is not going to go away? How do you keep fighting when you know your opponent is probably going to win? How do you overcome something that can’t be overcome?

We were a young married couple. We had beautiful children. We were suc- cessful.  We were happy. By all measures, we were blessed. 

I was a busy actor, working in Movies, TV and Commercials. Charlene was starting a new career as a Casting Director. We weren’t just happy we were secure.  Secure in our careers. Secure financially. Secure in our love… Life couldn’t get any better. Then… I was diagnosed with Parkinson’s disease, an incurable progressively debilitating neurological disorder. I was barely 30. Publicly, I put my head firmly in the sand and kept on living.  Ignoring the illness. Working as much as possible. Not telling anyone. All the while the disease progressed. But after nearly a decade of outrunning Par­kinson’s the disease caught up with me. Almost all at once, I stopped getting hired and had to go on disability. Just for added intrigue this was 2008 and the Wall Street markets crashed. I went from having everything I ever wanted to having almost everything taken away. Almost everything.

The lowest and darkest moment came on July 3rd 2009 when on a family outing, our minivan door slammed shut on Charlene’s head… hard.  At the hospital, she didn’t know where she was, or who she was or why she was there. The doctor was very concerned and asked me to take my kids out of the hospital, not wanting them to be there if the worst were to occur. We were four hours from home so I had no one to care for the kids, for me. I remember taking my young and terrified children out of the hospital and leaving their mother, the love of my life, lying on a gurney.

I prayed. I begged. I cried.

It took almost a year.  A year of sitting in a dark room whispering to each other before Charlene was back to some semblance of normal. But just as she was feeling better, my Parkinson’s medication was losing its effectiveness.  I was falling, I couldn’t sleep and I was having trouble breathing.  I was taking seven medications and 19 pills per day.  My doctors recommended brain surgery.

As I lay on the gurney Charlene cried and prayed for me.  When they wheeled me in, I asked for a second and I prayed.

“Our Father in heaven

Hallowed be thy name

Thy Kingdom come

Thy will be done…”

… I stopped. I thought I’m just going to leave it there. I told the doctors I was ready. And I am. Our future won’t be easy. Parkinson’s and life will make sure of that… But we’re ready and His will be done. And with Him, we’ll continue to overcome.
