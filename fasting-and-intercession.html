Fasting and Intercession for 2015

My Journey of Prayer

About 2 years in the making of The Love Express, the Lord spoke to me, and gave clear direction concerning The Love Express. He told me it would be, and always re­main a clearing house of what the Holy Spirit would be doing in the Christian Community.

It was different doing the paper. It would be a means of commitment with rough roads to climb over. Many churches said, “No,” and closed their doors, but I knew that this wonsetas God’s agenda, God’s newspaper. I died to my flesh, picked myself up, and with the help of the Holy Spirit, here we are 26 years later. From the it was hard getting information from churches about what was happening in their churches and ministries.

Thank God, there are plenty of activities listed in The Love Express where you can take your children. Whatever is your cup of tea, whether Baptist, Lutheran, Charismatic, etc., that’s what makes The Love Express unique. Thank God  for chur­ches and ministries that are coming to the rescue, and providing activities.

The Love Express has been serving the Body of Christ for 26 years. Our first paper was eight pages which came out in Septem­ber of 1985. Throughout these years, you the readers have been right there with us. We have strived to build relationships with pastors, and build bridges over the many obstacles that hold the Body of Christ back from true unity. Prayer has al­ways been the underlying foundation of all that we do. We pray when our computers don’t function properly, pray for our distributors and families, pray for advertisers, pastors and churches that receive The Love Ex­press, pray for our printing company, and pray for the fi­nances of The Love Ex­press.

The Love Express has been reporting the ‘Good News’ for over 26 years in the tri-state area and I know the paper has been a blessing to you. Churches, mini­s­tries, writers, business peo- ple, singers, have all said how much the paper is needed.

I realize that I am really inadequate to completely fulfill the task God has set before me, but I’ve learned to rest in Him to run the largest “Christian” newspaper in the Tri-state area. Sometimes the juggling of the work of the ministry, and the newspaper at the same time, is so staggering, so immense, that if I were to sit down and think about it, I would be completely overwhelmed. So what makes me keep going to face the numerous obstacles that are before me? It’s knowing that my God is with me in my prayers, praise and worship. These are my food: my substance. God has enabled me through supernatural power to fulfill this calling on my life. Be en­couraged.

Without prayer, we don’t have a ministry. With­out prayer, we are not servants of the most high God. Without prayer, we will ne­ver discover the secret place with the Lord.

Never get so busy that you have no time for prayer.

I can remember as if it was yesterday the first ever Love Express hot off the press. It was an exciting time. I walked by blind faith. I saw the body of Christ as one, functioning as one unit, working today as one body. It was scary to venture out of your protective garden (or religious circles) to meet and greet Pastors.

Many Pastors I’ve heard about, but I never had the opportunity to visit their ser­vice. Why are we so afraid, so uncomfortable of the unknown.

I guess you can read between the lines the Lord was doing a mighty work in my heart. In God’s eyes I was ready for my destiny yet in my mind I was not ready.

Unity, the Father’s heart cries, that we all be one in his son Jesus Christ. In spite of our differences in mi­nistering the word of Christ, “We Are One” and the Lord desires that the world see us as one in Him.

Working on The Love Express has taught me much about patience, love and forgiveness, and how we are not to love and cry unity, when we pick and choose those ministries or ministers that reflect our likes and taste, that is not God’s unity.

“WE ARE ONE”not because we are the same color, or because we speak at the same meet­ings, “WE ARE ONE” be­cause of our love of Je­sus Christ.

In the month of Jan­ua­ry many churches and mini­s­tries are planning to fast and seek the Lord for 10, 20, 30 and even 40 days. Plan to join your church for a time of corporate fasting and pra­yer. Fasting is an act of de­nying yourself and placing God back where He always belongs, in the center of our lives, our families, churches businesses and nation. If you never fasted, start slow­ly, if you are under a doctor’s care, ask your doctor whether you’re able to fast.

Why fast? Fasting blesses every area of our mental, spiritual and emotional life. It breaks habits and strongholds in our life that we aren’t aware of. Start slowly and each day begin with prayer; read Isa­iah 58 where it states “Is this not the fast I have chosen...,” list those areas of your life that needs to be set free, and begin to fast.

I remember when I first heard about fasting; the first thing I did was to gather scripture pertaining to fasting; I read about sanctifying yourself and turning your heart over to the Lord. I must admit it was not easy; the flesh had such a hold on me, but I kept prevailing, and now after 30 years it has become a way of life. Fasting cannot be done without prayer. Find a place to get alone with God, confess your faults to Him, pray and wait before Him. It is important at this time that the Body of Christ come to­gether with fasting and pra­yer and wait on Him. Is there any good reason why not to fast. If you fail, start all over again.

PRAYER is the backbone of the ministry. God’s restoring prayer to our rea­ders, churches and minis­tries.

Types of Fasts

There are several types of fasting. The one you choose is between you and God. He will honor your best sacrifice.

Full Fast: Drink only liquids (you establish the number of days).

The Daniel Fast: Eat no meat, no sweets and no bread. Drink water and juice. Eat fruits and vegetables.

3-Day Fast: This fast can be a Full Fast, Daniel Fast or give up at least one item of food.

Partial Fast: A partial fast is from 6am-3pm or from sun up to sundown. You can select from three types of fasting-a Full Fast, Daniel Fast or give up at least one item of food.

Scripture References for Fasting: Matthew 6:16-18, Matthew 9:14-15, Luke 18: 9-14.

Relation to Prayer & Reading of the Word: 1 Samuel 1:6-8, 17-18, Ne­hemiah 1:4, Daniel 9:3, 20, Joel 2:12, Luke 2: 37, Acts 10-:30, Acts 13:2.

Corporate Fasting: 1 Sa­mu­el 7:5-6, Ezra 8:21-23, Nehemiah 9:1-3, Joel 2:15-16, Jonah 3: 5-10, Acts 27:33-37.

Remember that it is the attitude of a heart sincerely seeking Him to which God responds with a blessing (Isaiah 58, Jeremiah 14:12, 1 Corinthians 8:8). May God greatly bless you as you fast!

PS. Check with your doctor if you are on medication before you go on the fast.

How to Begin Fasting

If you have never fasted before, do not start off with a 40-day fast unless you are very sure God has called you to do this. The body grows accustomed to fasting by degrees, and God does not usually ask us to run before we have begun to walk. Start with a partial fast, or fast one day until supper. The next time, extend the fast till retiring, breaking it with a light meal or fruit.

When you can manage on a one-day fast without feeling faint or famished (not without feeling hungry!), you will be ready for the call of God to a longer fast. But the longer the fast, the more certain you need to be that God has called you to it.

Beware of being carried away by carnal enthusiasm. “Keep back thy servant from presumptuous sins” (Ps 19:13, KJV) must be your constant prayer. Remember that asceticism may be as much “of the flesh” as over­indulgence; the latter is a sin of the body, the former of the spirit.

If the fast is to be for some days, there is a temptation as it approaches to overindulge. Better far to bend our wills in advance to the battle of self discipline; it will ease the later conflict.

Finally, it is of supreme importance to approach this spiritual exercise as a fast of God’s choosing. It may help to ask yourself the following questions.

 1. Am I confident that this desire is God given? Would He have me undertake a normal or just a partial fast? “Jesus was led by the Spirit into the desert” (Mt. 4:1)

 2. Are my motives right? Is there any hidden desire to impress others? “Your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you.” (Mt. 6:4)

 3. What are my spiritual objectives in this fast? Personal consecration? In­tercession? Special burdens? Revival? “I press on toward the goal.” (Phil. 3:14).

4. Are my objectives self-centered? Is my desire for personal blessing balanced by genuine concern for others? “Each of you should look not only to your own interests, but also to the interests of others.” (Phil. 2:4

 5. Am I determined above all else to minister to the Lord in this fast? “They were worshipping the Lord and fasting” (Acts 13:2).

You should expect fasting to be, as it was for your Master, a time of conflict with the powers of darkness. Sa­tan will often try to take advantage of your physical condition to launch an attack. Discouragement is one of his weapons. Guard against it by maintaining a spirit of praise.

Read through Ephe­sians 6 and avail yourself of the whole armor of God. Make use of the shield of faith to quench all Satan’s flaming darts. In hand-to-hand encounter, use the sword of the Spirit, and tell Satan, “It is written...” De­clare the victory of your great Captain over every principality and power.

Do not judge the efficacy of your intercessions by what you feel. Often in pra­yer and fasting you will find the going harder instead of easier. This is often when most is happening. This is wrestling. This is heavenly warfare. Your Captain did not promise a walk­over but a fight, and He gave you the weapons to win. Often you will not see the results until later, but the promise stands: “Your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you.” (Mt. 6:4).

From strength to strength go on, Wrestle, and fight, and pray; Tread all the powers of darkness down, And win the well-fought day.

- Charles Wesley
