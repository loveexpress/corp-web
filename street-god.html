Street God:

The Explosive True Story of a Former Drug Boss on the Run From the Hood - And The Courageous Mission That Drove Him Back

The Love Express is so proud of the newest venture from Pastor Dimas Salaberrios–his new book Street God. It chronicles his explosive true life story of being a former drug boss, being radically transformed by the power of Jesus Christ, and then going back to the streets to pull others out of it’s grip. It just landed in bookstores all over the country and is already extending its global reach by being translated into two languages–Dutch and Finnish. Josh McDowell literally "dares anyone to read Street God and come away unchanged," crowning it "The Cross and the Switchblade for a new ge­neration." Dr. Tim Keller adds, "You won't find a more amazing account of how God's grace can change lives," and Dr. A.R. Bernard calls it “powerful.” What a joy it is to see other great leaders affirming the faith and trust we at the Love Express placed in Pastor Dimas years ago! "My first cover page was in the Love Express,” Pastor Dimas shares, “an extremely important paper for the Tri-State region. I sincerely appreciate the value this paper adds to the faith community here. Its consistent reporting unites the Body of Christ together on a monthly basis and should ne­ver be taken for granted. Edith Stranges believed in me and several of my colleagues who are all working tirelessly to make Christ known outside of their own churches.”

Street God has already reached bestseller status, debuting on some of the top TV and radio programs in the nation like the #1 cable news morning show Fox & Friends, TBN’s worldwide popular Praise The Lord show and much more. Yet far more impressive than sales perhaps is the phenomenal reach of Pastor Dimas’s tome, often impacting those in forgotten places.  For the entire month of August, Pastor Dimas struck a deal with his publi­sher Tyndale Momentum that for every book purchased on their StreetGod Book.com website, one would also be given to a prisoner.  The kingdom-mindedness of that action alone speaks volumes about this new author. “Inmates and their family members write to me and Facebook me testimonies of how the book Street God is going from cell to cell assisting in converting hearts. Mothers are reading it weeping at the hope it swells within their hearts as they then give the book to their sons. Young men are giving their lives to Jesus in droves totally rocked by the power of the blood of the Lamb and the word of this strong testimony. Churches are poring over it as a blueprint on how to revamp their evangelistic efforts. I never imagined this book would become like a new Bible tract. I am grateful that what I went through with all its many trials and triumphs is being a blessing to so many lives,” said Pastor Dimas.

Street God is an authentic New York story masterfully detailing the crack boom era that impacted the entire city, and is a literary “Boot Camp” for anyone’s faith! The book offers an intimate case study of just how easily kids get snatched into the drug underworld and how God miraculously dethroned Satan from a young man’s heart. Recalling some of the intense pain of his past, Pastor Dimas shared with us that he was literally shaking at times while writing the book. A cursory glance at some of Street God’s reviews on Amazon makes clear that many readers are also riveted by this story as they experience the same twists and turns as they read it. Also notable is that the book doesn’t just end at Pastor Dimas’s transformation in Christ, but also details his evolution to becoming one of the great leaders in this, the greatest city on earth! 

Pastor Dimas said, "My hope is that this book empowers families to not give up on their kids. I broke my mother’s heart. My Mom was an ele­men­tary school principal and my Dad was a former Air Force man and captain of corrections. I hailed from a family of educators and professionals. Leaping from taking karate lessons in middle school to becoming a kingpin drug dealer was never the trajectory my parent’s planned for me, yet something I willfully and stubbornly chose. One of the greatest blessings in my life is that my mother left this world proud of my decision to follow Jesus and impact other’s lives across the world. I had the privilege of serving her in my home as she passed before my eyes.”

Dimas was introduced to the drug world at the tender age of 11 years old by a fellow classmate at a predo­mi­nately white middle school. Drawn to the money-making images he’d seen in the mo­vie Scarface, Dimas soon threw himself into drug dealing, and by age 15 and 16, he served 2 stints in the notorious Rikers Island jail for drug trafficking charges. At 17, he escaped police custody while handcuffed, dres-sed up like a woman on Halloween in order to evade the authorities and traveled all the way down to Winston-Salem, NC where he finally achieved his goal of becoming a "street god," a drug kingpin who headed a thriving drug and gun-dealing enterprise.  But then he had an encounter with 3 brave women that completely changed his life. They prayed for him and he immediately changed his allegi­ance from the violent, crazed world he was in, to the peace that came with his spiritual conversion. Wanting to finally obey the laws of the land even though he was facing at least 7 years in prison for escaping police custody, he willingly traveled back to NYC, walked into a police station and turned himself. The judge who oversaw his case miraculously pardoned him, discerning that he was indeed a changed man and sending him back to jail would only ruin him.

In the 20 years since Dimas received his pardon, he has traveled to every continent except Antarctica hoping to rescue other people from the same entanglements with the underworld that he had experienced as a teen­ager. He started a church in the Bronx River Housing projects - the literal birthplace of hip hop, which is also located in the poorest congressional district of the entire US. The week his church started, a homicide had taken place in Bronx River. But over the course of 10 years, Pastor Dimas and Infinity’s powerful impact was cited by former NYC Police Commissioner Ray Kelly with being the primary reason homicides were eliminated in Bronx River and drug dealing brought to an all-time low in that community. Street God should be required reading as a case study for parents and teens.  The lessons to be learned therein are life-changing.

Baltimore-Reaction to Judge Won’t Drop Charges Against Cops in the Freddie Gray Case. The night of the riots in Baltimore, there were over 200 arrests as the world watched in horror as yet another inner city was set ablaze. On Day 2, Pastor Dimas boldly went “into the fire” so to speak, specifically targeting tough guys, gang members and those set on defiantly ignoring the curfew that was to go into effect that night. While hundreds of pastors that critical 2nd night were meeting, organizing and praying indoors, Pastor Dimas and his team were on the ground, strategically redirecting the negative energy of those young men and causing them to think beyond just that night.  As you’ll see, it definitely wasn’t all pretty, but proved to be remarkably effective(side note ...after Dimas’s on the ground approach, there were only 2 arrests that 2nd night).

Pastor Dimas will bring rich analysis to the very real frustrations police officers are sensing nationwide in the wake of cop killings and hateful speech attributed to the Black Lives Matters movement. Pastor Dimas has personally experienced both good and corrupt cops. His work in the Bronx River community working WITH law enforcement has been very effective in that community.  A video of cops playing basketball with kids recently went viral - those were kids from Bronx River who attend Pastor Dimas’s church, Infinity Bible Church - see 2 NYPD Cops Play Basketball With Neighborhood Kids.... & KILL IT! He will do an incredible job highlighting how vital relationship-building is to transforming the angst between officers and community members.

Heroin Epidemic: The Crack Boom in the Inner Cities in the 90s is the He­ro­in EPIDEMIC in the Suburbs Today…a Timely Back to School WARNING! Pastor Dimas will sound the alarm, “Parents in America: WAKE UP”! The image of the shady, older-looking drug dealer lurking in alley shadows trying to lure young kids his way is GONE!  Today’s heroin dealers look just like your kids and are specifically targeting middle schools, so that by high school, they have ready-made, full blown addicts. Remember, Dimas was only 11 when he took his first drug offered by none other than his classmate. Heroin sells for just $2–$3 a hit!  Kids are using their lunch money to sniff it up, having been it will make them happy and have fun. Dimas will highlight his past - how if anyone should have escaped the drug world–a school principal’s and captain of corrections son living in the suburbs of Queens, he definitely see-med like a prime candidate.  He will offer both preventative tips he can offer having experienced all sides of the drug world. His life sto­ry is also a beacon of hope for those parents and teens who have already been caught up in a devastating downward spiral that is drug addiction. THIS is the back to school message both teens and parents NEED to hear!

DIMAS SALABERRIOS has served as senior pastor of Infinity Bible Church, which he founded in partnership with Dr. Tim Keller and Redeemer City to City since 2003. A former New York City police commissioner credited Pastor Dimas and Infinity’s powerful impact in the Bronx River Housing projects as being the startling reason homicides have been eliminated and drug dealing reduced to an all-time low in that community for over a decade.

Pastor Dimas is a gifted storyteller and Bible teacher. As president of Concerts of Prayer Greater New York, he spearheads the largest, most diverse force of pastors in a tri-state area comprised of over 5,000 churches mobilized across various denominational, ethnic and economic lines.

A true servant-leader at heart, Pastor Dimas is often described as one unafraid of running into the fire. With real “boots on the ground”, Pastor Dimas is often advancing the cause of Christ during the nation’s most pressing issues, as well as boldly proclaiming the gospel on every continent (with the exception of Antarctica). Perhaps that explains his undeniable effectiveness and relevance—be it quelling the riots that recently raged in Baltimore or bringing hope and healing directly to those devastated by the vicious slaughter of the Emanuel 9 in Charleston.  Some of Pastor Dimas’s global ministry exploits include igniting a three-month revival in New Zealand; smuggling Bibles to the persecuted church in China; pastoral leadership training and humanitarian aid throughout Kenya; being a first-responder and lifeline to three refugee camps after Haiti’s devastating earthquake; guerrilla evangelism in in Amsterdam’s notorious red-light district, on the tubes of London, in the alps of Switzerland, in the hills of Venezuela and with the gangs in the South Bronx.

A passionate and exciting speaker, Pastor Dimas’s humor and inspiring style has opened doors for him to appear on Fox & Friends, TBN, the Word Network, the 700 Club, CNN, Fox News, ABC World News To­night, and NBC News as well as being featured in the New York Times, The Washington Post, Christianity Today, Outreach, On Faith and other publications. His weekly TV pro- gram The Dynamic Life with Pastor Dimas can be seen on the Word Network in over 80 million homes around the world, and his weekday radio show is broadcast over the Salem Radio Network.

Pastor Dimas holds a Master of Divinity degree from Alliance Theological Seminary and lives in the Bronx with his wife, Tiffany, and their two daughters.
