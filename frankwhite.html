"Prayer Power: 19 Years & Counting"

Bishop Frank Anthone White's Prayer Conference

By Bishop Frank Anthone White

 The Love Express sat down with Bishop Frank Anthone White to go behind the veil of his booming prayer movement which, now in its 19th year, continues to draw crowds of all ages from nearly every mainstream Protestant de­nomination. According to Bishop White, during the Crusade Schedule of FAW Evangelistic Ministries in the Spring of 1992 “the formation of this bold, vigorous adventure into the holy pre­cincts of prayer basically grew out of a three component process reinforced by the biblically defined ne­ces­sity of prayer – not as an event– but as a lifestyle:the application component, the clinical component and the fellowship component.”

      “Though we are commissioned by Scripture to ‘pray without ceasing’, in reality, the daily hustling and

accelerated rush-hour pace of today’s working population leaves us scarcely able to find the time to pray as we would like” Bishop White explains. The current cultural paradigm calls for an or­ganized setting for believers to connect with the power of God through corporate pra­yer. In responding to this growing reality, the Conference has provided an op­portunity for attendees to submit their personal pra­yer requests in writing and, through relevant seminars, share their personal experiences with the ultimate goal of expressing their gratitude over answered pra­yers through worship. This is what builds a climate of participation and application, positioning the believer to achieve contact with the shared strength and spiritual sincerity of those who are willing to literally “stand in the gap” on their behalf.

      The clinical component of the FAW Prayer Partnership was built around an eight-week Prayer Clinic during Fall of 1996. Comprised, in part, of participants from a 6 AM Prayer Ministry which ran for six consecutive months earlier the same year, the clinic welcomed an influx of ea­ger learners wishing to streng­then their perspective on the ministry of prayer. “It was amazing! As expected, many of the members of Zion Cathedral pressed their way to the early morning prayer. But what stood out was how a significant cluster of the surrounding neighbors would find themselves hurrying down the side streets of Babylon Turnpike just before sunrise to arrive at the sanctuary and kneel before God in prayer – five days a week, without fail.”

      Along with establishing a framework for the fundamentals of prayer, the pra­yer clinic covered topics in­cluding:the mandate of pra­yer, the levels of prayer, the urgency of prayer, the consistency of prayer, the purpose and spiritual mechanisms of intercession and the discipline of prayer as accessorized by fasting. Though totally unplanned, from this clinic, a flourishing pool of prayer warriors grew into an organized de­le­ga­tion known today as FAW Evangelistic Crusade Mini­stries Prayer Partners. This partnership has become a family of familiar faces throughout the year at Zion Cathedral of Freeport where Bishop White pastors alongside his father, Bishop Frank Otha White, both serving as re-elected officers of the Church of God in Christ’s International Body.       “There is nothing re­vo­lutio­nary about the global call to pray” says White. “In fact, the most sweeping revival of Pentecostalism of the previous century owes its impact to the foundation of prayer. It was at during the Azusa Street Revivals of Los Angeles, California from 1906-1909 that the Founder of the Church of God in Christ, Bishop Charles Harrison Mason, re­ceived the baptism of the Holy Ghost in a cotton gin house prayer meeting. From this same historic revival, emerged the brotherhood of Pentecostal As­semblies which yet flourish upon this very same foundational practice of unceasing pra­yer.”

      As the years have ad­vanced, the fellowship component of the Prayer Confe­rence has featured a five-star breakfast not unlike a weekend buffet at Marriott International. While attendees dine from a gourmet menu colorfully displayed across hot and cold stations stocked with unlimited breakfast amenities in Zion’s Lower Fellowship, prayer warriors assemble in the upper sanctuary in in­crements of between 250-300 from each of the three seatings, perfuming the worship atmosphere with the fragrance of prayer.

      Having become a widely observed Long Island tradition, the Prayer Conference has–and continues to attract – more than 3,000 prayer warriors from across the Tri-State area annually. It’s a special gathering, filled with profound testimonies of healing and deliverance, the reunion of long-lasting friendships and genuine prayer partnerships. Bishop White concludes, “The more you cultivate your prayer life, the less likely it be­comes that you will not be inspired by the words of Lord Alfred Tennyson who expressed – ‘More things are wrought by prayer than this world dreams of.’

Rodgers of Car­rol­ton, Te­xas, Bishop Darrell Hines of Milwaukee and the culminating An­nual Prayer Breakfast Speaker, Bishop Frank Anthone White, Conference Host and Founder.
