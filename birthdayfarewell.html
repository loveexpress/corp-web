Birthday, Farewell & Installation:
‘McAuley’ turns 140

1872. 140 years ago. Seven generations. Your great, great, great, great grandfather’s America. Just after the Civil War. Reconstruction in the South and gangs of New York in Manhattan. And revival, by God’s grace. Orphans, widows, the lame and other ‘deserving’ groups were served by Christian charities and missions. But not so the ‘undeserving poor’. Not until Jerry McAuley.

Jerry McAuley was a ‘river thief’, a terror and nuisance to all around him, and he went to prison as a result. That is where God found and forgave him, rocking his world. Years later, McAuley emerged from prison a changed man with a passion for the passionless, for those with a clear antipathy toward God and man. McAuley knew the type ... he WAS the type. More crucially, he knew God’s ability to melt the street savviest antipathy into an earthy passion for God and man.

And so his Helping Hand for Men (aka McAuley Water Street Mission aka NYC Rescue Mission) opened its door to the homeless in October 1872, without fanfare but with well-wishers, prayer-warriors and financial friends in NY, NJ and CT. Within months McAuley was stirring up Lower Manhattan, but more truly the Church was stretching--via rescue mission--into the gutter of the “unworthy poor”. Within a few years this ministry of feeding the hungry, resting the weary, and mending the broken had spread to Chicago, England and New Zealand. Within a decade many American cities had a rescue mission or missionesque ministry.

Within decades, hundreds of rescue missions served in North America--in the vast majority of cites, as they do to this day. The hungry are fed, the weary are given rest, and the broken are mended. And unsurprisingly the gospel is received with a transparency unusual for the church.

Indeed, 140 years after McAuley opened the Mission and ignited the rescue mission movement, we are not so much honoring Jerry and Maria McAuley--or even Jim and Anita VarnHagen, who are retiring after leading the Mission, rechristening it NYC Rescue Mission, and prepping it for a much needed expansion.

They’ll be the first to tell you we are celebrating the church--the body of Christ--breaking down barriers, serving the poor, preaching the Gospel and expanding the kingdom. We’re celebrating the McAuleys, the VarnHagens and Dr. Craig Mayes, who will be installed as our new executive director on Oct 2nd at Calvary Baptist Church in midtown Manhattan. And we’re celebrating the unsung Adventists, Assemblies of God, Baptists, Brethren, Episcopalians, Gideons, Lutherans, Mennonites, Methodists, Presbyterians, and other Christians who have fed the hungry and lead the hungry-in-spirit to Christ.

Come celebrate with us on Oct. 2nd. There are few places in life or history where the Christian Church shines as brightly or unitedly as in our sacrificial serving of the poor via rescue mission. Let’s face it: it isn’t easily or practically done in a typical church building. But if your church had an annex in Lower Manhattan devoted to the needy you would likely want to hear about its work and you’d probably end up serving there. You wouldn’t worry about messing up the furniture or getting entangled with a homeless person week after week. Truly.

Please celebrate with us in at least thress ways:

Meanwhile, thank you and God bless you--the Church in NY/NJ/CT and Missionaries from all over the globe--for 140 glorious, gritty years of blessing the least, the last and the lost in New York City!
