Serving Our City

Helping Hurting People Is God’s Plan
For Every Church

Every Church has been called to serve its city. Especially the people that no one wants. Someone once said; we must work with the people that nobody wants until they become the people that everyone wants. Our cities are filled with people that no one wants in their homes or even churches but when the church begins to serve those very people, according to scripture, God smiles from heaven and says….these are my sons and daughters with whom I am well pleased. When you do it unto them, you do it unto me.

It is not enough to pray and preach to and for our cities; serving is an essential part of the gospel. Bishop Joseph Mattera says: if you want to win a city, serve it.

We started serving the elderly in and around our congregation by helping them with the laundry, grocery shopping, cleaning their homes, etc. The number of amazing stories and positive testimonies that has come from this is priceless.

We have a neighbor around our church that appears to be using drugs. During our current basement repair project I offered to hire him for a day work and treated him the same as the other workers. Last Sunday he showed up for service and mentioned that he was so pleased and happy with our acceptance of him that he wants to hear more about the Jesus we serve and will bring his family. He also started helping with the cleanup of the church.

We have another neighbor who lives in a halfway home near the church and is not altogether well adjusted but attends the services and is made to feel loved and accepted. She tells everyone she meets about her church and started coming in early to help setup for the service and is always willing to run errands, etc.

One year ago our church connected with “Feed New York” via Time Square Church and began supplying needy families with free healthy groceries. This act of kindness has opened the door for so many un-churched neighbors to notice our church. If we begin to do ministry in the same manner that Jesus did, it is possible we can reach our cities with the gospel of service. We can help win our cities by serving it. Try it this week, let me know the results.

Pastor Matthew Singh, New Haven Ministries, 130-05 101 Avenue, Richmond Hill NY 11419 msingh@new havenministries.org
