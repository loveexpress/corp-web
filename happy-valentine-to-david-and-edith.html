Happy Valentine
to David & Edith

Edith and David

February is always a special month for my husband David and I. Valentine’s Day has a special meaning to both of us. It was our first date, February 14th, 1959; yes 1959. Fifty-seven years ago I was seventeen years old and running from God. Once a month from the time I was 14 years old and my father used to take my sister Rose and I roller skating with different churches throughout the five boroughs. David was a good friend of my father. He was a youth leader. This one Monday, my father, as he always did, would say, “Why don’t you roller skate with David?” (The same thing we say to our young people, “Why don’t you say hello to so and so?” “Why don’t you be nice to him?”) I didn’t dislike David, in fact he was friendly, patient and good looking. I just was rebelling at what he represented (The Church)) Oh, but God got a hold of me! You can’t run from God. Our lives are ordained by Him. Well, to make a long story short, we roller skated, and he asked me out.

Our first date was to a Spanish Church. (I know you must be laughing). I couldn’t believe it myself. That night he asked me to be his Va­lentine. My answer was No! Three months later we were engaged, and one and a half years later we married. We will be married 56 years in May. Am I his Valentine? Yes! Is he my Valentine? Yes! About 10 years into our marriage, David told me he put a fleece out to God that if I would roller skate with him, he would ask me out and then marry me. I said to David, “You asked God about us?” He said “Yes!” He saw the great potential in me that I couldn’t see. The devil had a veil over my eyes. (2 Corinthians 4:4) That’s why Valentine’s Day is so special to David and myself.
