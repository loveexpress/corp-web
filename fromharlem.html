"From Harlem with Love"

by Ms Gee
myprayereagle@aol.com

In Manhattan at the Cornell Private Club, for the book signing of Joseph Holland’s book “From Harlem with Love.” I am sure you ask the question who is Joseph Holland? Joe Holland the writer, advocate for the homeless, lawyer, politician, father, husband, actor, playwright and minister. All of these things make up this one incredible man. I find Joe to be one of the nicest men I have come across in a long time and now the author Joe Holland has written about his journey as an Ivy Leaguer’s inner city odyssey. Joe Holland the son of a diplomat, star athlete and graduate of both Cornell and Harvard with many opportunities to hobnob with the big boys, but instead desires to help the ones that need it the most. Joe believes it is all of our duties if you are financially solvent to help create jobs and opportunities for the less fortunate.

We sat and talked about how Harlem has changed in the 1920’s with the Renaissance period, with the great poets, writers and artists. Joe believes this can happen again if we work together and build our communities by controlling our own economic dynasty, along with our government attacking little problems before we go after big problems. We should deal with the little mole hills and work our way up to the mountains of problems. Our people must become self-sufficient and independent, by becoming creative entrepreneurs. Joe also said “Our homeless must be approached with holistic approach, so that they can be involved in their rebirth, obtaining skills so they can transition back into society enabling them to get jobs and apartments. Joe Holland is always trying to find ways to have affordable housing for people to live in, allowing them to have some money left over, maybe even to take a vacation. Joe Holland expressed how people were concerned about wealthy people coming and changing Harlem, when in fact people are being changed by Harlem. This wonderful book depicts the history, culture and memoirs down memory lane. This book brings to life Harlem in the eyes of Joe Holland, to bring about the rich history and culture of Harlem. If you do not have this book, I recommend this book to everyone.

By Ms. Gee
