Gospel Today Magazine
Returns to Print!

The resurrection of “America’s #1 Christian Lifestyle Publication”
by Teresa Hairston

In a season when America is witnessing unprecedented successes as well as debilitating challenges, there are any number of poignant stories that hit the airwaves and pages of the media each day. This story is about resiliency.

In September of 2008, Gospel Today Magazine, dubbed “America’s #1 Christian Lifestyle Magazine” was pulled from the shelves of over 120 LifeWay Christian Stores across the country. Why? Simply because the magazine covered a story of women who serve as pastors.

The magazine’s publisher and board were outraged! Efforts were made to contact and negotiate with the store chain, but to no avail. Additionally, there were campaigns to bolster the mag’s circulation through the community. However, in the midst of the shattered American economy, the magazine faltered. In Feb 2009, Gospel Today publisher, Teresa Hairston announced that the magazine would cease printing and switch to digital delivery.

During the months of March and April, the magazine faithfully served its constituency online, however, the outcry for the return to print was overwhelming.

In May, Hairston and her board agreed to return to press. The new issue, which has just released, tributes “Dads” and appropriately features President Barack Obama, a shining symbol of hope, on the cover.

Hairston stated, “We were getting tons of calls, emails and letters—people were demanding that we return to print. I am thrilled to be able to meet that demand. I appreciate the fact that people really love the magazine and that it fills such a unique space.”

After 20 years of consistent publishing, Hairston says that this is the first time the company has not printed an issue. “We look forward to serving our customers in excellence. All subscribers will be extended an extra issue for the one we didn’t print,” she added.

Hairston embodies the American Dream. In 1989, she started Gospel Today as a four-page newsletter (called the Gospel SCORE). She candidly admits, “The first issue, which focused on Gospel Music, didn’t look much better than a Sunday-morning church bulletin.”

Hairston had no investors, no background and no plan; and, within six months, was struggling to pay her personal and professional bills and was unable to move forward. As a single mom struggling to raise three children, she wanted to
quit and simply acknowledge failure, but somehow, she held on and believed in her dream and didn’t give up. In fact, she decided to leap forward—turning the newsletter into a glossy magazine. The leap of faith proved providential. The magazine became viable, and grew to a circulation of over 200,000—consisting of newsstand, subscription and church distribution. Along the way, Gospel Today garnered the support of major ministry leaders across the world and became a Christian lifestyle magazine focusing on faith, finance, fitness and fashion.
