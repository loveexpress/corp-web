Services to King Jesus!

Sacrificial service is motivated by Christ’s sa­crifice on the cross, rather than out of a desire to earn favor with God. Our motivation for serving others is a response to the grace shown us by Jesus, not an attempt to earn God’s favor through moral behavior. Through service, we witness a mutual transformation: both the server and the served grow in the process.

All over the city, pastors are beginning to come together in neighborhood-focused groups to talk about their plans of working toge­ther to serve their communities.

• In Brooklyn, a group of primarily Chinese pastors met to discuss the possibilities of afterschool programs badly needed for the many single-parent, working families in their area.

• In the Bronx, another group of pastors and leaders are beginning the process of planning an evangelistic outreach event that would include a massive distribution of food and clothing.

• In Long Island, a group of 20 churches are working on an 8-week sermon series together with a real push for evangelism. The purpose of the series is for people to invite their unsaved friends to explore God at churches.

• In Staten Island, the Engage Network, which consists of nearly 20 churches, have divided their region by zip code and will be working together to do community improvement projects and engaging in school outrea­ches.

Our goal is to make the largest impact possible through NY CityServe & CityFest. You are invited to be part of the conversation in your borough by joining in a strategic pastors gathering. These gatherings will also provide a forum for discussion around unique opportunities to share the Gospel in regionally-based outreach events this spring, as part of NY CityFest with Luis Palau.

NY CityServe is rooted in the Biblical values of justice, mercy, humility, unity and love, to be practiced through collaborative projects celebrating the diversity of God’s movement in New York City, in order to seek the peace of the city for the glory of God. NY CityServe is impelled by the Gospel, connected to churches, focused on neighborhoods, driven by relationships, and committed to diversity.

Throughout New York City, NY CityServe will celebrate and accelerate what churches are already doing to serve vulnerable populations. Faith communities around the city will be mobilized to serve foster children, through working with foster care organizations and encouraging greater involvement from believers in the fos­ter care system. Additionally, churches will collaborate withcity and community agencies to serve New York City’s homeless population by facilitating free health care and solutions for those living in poverty. Throughout New York City, believers will work together to bring hope to those without hope.

NY CityServe & City­Fest partner PULSE is working with students at City College of New York to organize the largest Christian outreach event to ever come to their campus, and they need your help. Student leaders are asking for churches like yours to be involved in helping lead their peers to Christ. You are invited to join pastors and ministry leaders from across New York City at the PULSE City College Pastors Lunch to learn more.
