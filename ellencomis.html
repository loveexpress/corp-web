Healing Through The Word

by Rev. Ellen Comis

Our precious Lord and Savior not only desires to heal us but His will is for us to live in divine health.  What might be the most important desire for your life right now?  Could it be healing?  You may be in pain and suffering from disease and your only thought is to receive freedom.  All through the scriptures we see that Healing belongs to us because of the cross.

Ps. 103:3   “who forgives all your iniquities, who heals all (not some) our diseases.”

Ps. 107:20 “He sent His Word and healed them and delivered them from their destructions.”

Mt. 15:13  “…every plant, which my Heavenly Father did not plant shall be uprooted.

God certainly did not plant cancer, tumors, growths, disease, etc.

Jesus wants you to have life in abundance.  That life is a life that overcomes what He defeated at the Cross. Does that mean you will never get sick? No not necessarily. That means if sickness and disease attack our body the abundant life of God in you is greater then the enemy’s hold on your body. Colossians 2:9-10 says, “For in Him dwells all the fullness of the Godhead bodily, and you are complete in Him, who is the head of all principality and power.”  What we need is a constant quickening of the Holy Spirit.  David said in Ps. 119:25 “Quicken me according to your Word.” How does the quickening start?  It begins when you desire more of the Life of God in you.  It happens when His presence becomes a passion and you yearn to be in His Glory and when you hunger for His Word.  In 2Peter 1:3 it says,  “His divine power has given to us all things that pertain to life and Godliness through the knowledge of Him who called us by glory and virtue.”

WHAT ARE SOME OF THE THINGS HIS DIVINE POWER HAS GIVEN TO US

Isaiah 53:5 and 1Peter 2:24 says, health and healing were already given to us at the cross.  Praise the Lord!  That means we do not have to beg for it. It was accomplished at Calvary. Even though it was won for us at the cross and given to us, there is a process of receiving it.  The Bible says,  that “all things are given through the knowledge of Him.”  So, if you do not have knowledge of Him, how can you receive “all the things that were given to you.”  Hosea 4:6 says, “My people are destroyed for a lack of knowledge.” We must begin to take responsibility for what Jesus already accomplished at the cross. 1 Peter 1:4 speaks of the great and precious promises that belong to us and David learned that believing, speaking and meditating on God’s Word will plant these promises in our heart. Then the Holy Spirit will cause them to grow. Knowing and believing the will of God is vital to receiving His promises. Pro. 4:20-22 says. “My son, give attention to my words; incline your ear to my sayings; Do not let them depart from your heart; For they are life to those who find them and health to all their flesh”. When you begin to do this,  Rev. 3:11 says, “…hold fast to what you have,” because in John 10:10 it says, “The thief does not come except to steal and to kill and to destroy. I have come that they may have life and may have it more abundantly.” Satan will always come and try to steal the Word out of your heart.

SOME OF GOD’S PROMISES

Phil 4:7 “And the peace of God, which passes all understanding shall keep your hearts and minds through Christ.”

1Peter 2:24 “That by His stripes we were healed.”

Mt. 11:5 “The blind receive their sight…and the deaf hear…”

Jer. 17:14 “Heal me O Lord and I shall be healed.”

2 Cor. 5:7 “For we walk by faith not by sight.”

Remember whatever the circumstances are saying to you they will never change the Word of God
