God’s Calling, Whew Man!!!!!

by Andre Harrison, Youth Pastor

F

irst off just let me re­iterate that I love You God. One of the most understandable things about You Lord is the one I thank You most for and that is Your grace. However I need to get a few things off my chest.

I love my calling as a youth pastor. There is nothing I else I want to do. Do­ing the Lord's work among today's youth is a great joy for me. If God has me be a youth pastor for the rest of my life I am content, however it is difficult at times. I have many ups and downs in youth ministry, and may­be it's because I am too personal with the teens. I be­lieve strongly in relationships, and I am a relational person. I have teens that are very close to me and appreciate that I am there for them and on the other hand I have teens that are faithful in attendance but mistake my love for them for being a nosy nag always in their business...

I love teens and desire to see them not only say that they love Jesus but live out their love for Jesus. Today's church has risen previous generations that put no care in relationships and the va­lue of accountability and that goes across denominational lines. It hurts me to hear that the teens, that I love so much, consider me a nosy nag. I have thought about backing off with my relationships with the teens and running a program fo­cused youth ministry. It has been said that good leaders keep their distance from their followers but that is not who I am. How can I lead by example from a distance??? I could be instructing one thing and living another and I would rather be considered a nosy nag than a hypocrite. Should I back off loving and building meaningful relationships with teens because a few think of me as a nosy nag....NOOOOOOOOO!!! I love them and want to see them be mighty young men and women of God. I do not want them to be apart of that abysmal statistic of the teens that leave the church after graduating High School... No, not if I can help it. I will continue to be there, I will continue to love on the young people God has entrusted me with. Who cares what they think now, tomorrow they will say that I kept bothering them but they know I love them and want the best for them...

Father I thank you for this great responsibility, I just ask that you would continue to give me the strength to serve the young people in my care. I love You so much Jesus and will continue to lean on Your arms, continue to seek Your wisdom, and try my hardest to lead as You did. I am in this for the long haul Jesus. I LOVE YOU ABBA FA­THER. In Jesus Name, AMEN.

By Pastor Andre Harrison
Youth Pastor of Christian Pentecostal Church in Staten Island, NY. Pastor Andre has been in full time youth ministry for 5 years. We started with 15-20 teens and now we are running 30-35 faithful and experiencing spiritual and physical growth with additional young people from time to time.
