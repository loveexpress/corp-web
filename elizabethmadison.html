Summer is Over - Time to Focus

By Elizabeth Madison

It's September, summer's over. Did you get off track with your healthy eating habits? If so, it is time to get refocused. Here are three tips I give my clients to jumpstart them back on the road to a healthy weight and good health.

Take a break from your beverages. One of the first areas I focus on is beverage intake because this is a health pothole most people don't see coming. You can consume over 500 calories a day or more just from your favorite drinks. Like your coffee light and sweet? That’s 150-200 calories. Why so high? One packet of sugar is 15 calories; one tablespoon of half and half, 20. The math may shock you depending on how much is in your cup. That decadent flavored iced coffee or Coolatta© will cost you 400-800 calories. Your refreshing soda will set you back 240 calories for 20 oun­ces. Cutting back or eliminating some of these beverages will decrease your sugar intake and  help you drop one pound a week.

Start strong with breakfast. Most people won’t hesitate to recharge their phones but won’t recharge their bodies with breakfast. Your cell phones need energy to function, and so does your body – especially after almost 12 hours from your last meal. I know you’ve heard it before, but breakfast truly is the most important meal of the day. Why? Eating breakfast not only gives you energy, but  studies show this meal, eaten daily, can lower cholesterol, maintain normal blood glucose levels –critical for people living with diabetes –and help maintain a healthy weight. As much as adults need breakfast, children need it even more.Their aca­demic performance improves significantly when they eat breakfast.

So what to eat? Your breakfast should always contain one protein and one carbohydrate. That means your usual sausage, egg and cheese on a roll (that’s three proteins and at least 3 bread servings) or your buttered bagel (4 bread servings, no protein, and lots of saturated fat) are clearly not the best choices. Instead, try these options:a Morningstar turkey sausage wrapped in a whole grain waffle – that’s yummy and filling. Make your oatmeal with dairy or soymilk instead of water to add protein. Add 1-2 tablespoons of nuts for a little more protein and healthy fat. Greek yogurt trumps regular yogurt with triple the amount of protein. Peanut butter or a hard-boiled egg on 100% whole wheat bread or thin are also tasty and healthy options.There are lots of healthy options, so get started on your heal­thy breakfast today!

Brown bag it. Buying your lunch can cost you financially and calorically. An average lunch costs $7 and can contain 600-1000 calories due to the large servings and the amount of fat - which you don’t know is there because you don’t know how the food is prepared (no matter what they tell you). Think your chicken wrap is low calorie? The wrap alone is 240-300 calories  - thin and flat does not equal low calorie. Salads with all those add-ins pile on the fat and calories. Truly too much of a good thing is still too much. Instead, pack your own lunch to give your body the nutrients it needs and save mo­ney.

Make your sandwich with 100 calorie, 100% whole wheat thins or pitas. Spread ripe avocado on your sandwich instead of mayonnaise for a nice dose of fiber, potassium and antioxidants. Prepare your salad with lots of different veggies, but only one protein and one high fat item. For example, choose chicken OR tuna OR beans. Add cheese OR avocado OR regular dressing. This can literally shave hundreds of calories while packing in tons of vitamins, minerals, and other essential nutrients that will make you feel great. God desires you to be in your best shape so that you can have life and have it more abundantly (John 10: 10b). Start today by doing your part.
