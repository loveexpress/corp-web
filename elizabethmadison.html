Probiotics - Q & A

By Elizabeth Madison

Walk down the supermarket aisle and you’ll see a growing array of products infused with probiotics. They all pro­mise amazing benefits. How do you know which products are effective? How much do you need? Are supplements safe? Probiotics are actually critical to your health in many ways. But incorporating this vital nutrient into your daily food plan requires knowledge, understanding and wisdom. So here is a Q&A of probiotics to get you started. 

What are they? Probiotics are “good” bacteria that live in your digestive tract. They keep disease-causing bacteria in check. Since your immune system is located in your gut, the more good bacteria you have, the better functioning your immune system can be.

What are some of the benefits of probiotics? There are many strains of probiotics, and they offer different benefits. You may already know that probiotics can promote a healthy digestive system, alleviating conditions like constipation, abdominal discomfort, and gas and bloating. However, they do a lot more! Some strains can: help reduce symptoms associated with taking antibiotics, e.g. diarrhea; alleviate symptoms associated with irritable bowel syndrome; fight infection by boosting the immune system. Some probiotics even play a role in increasing mineral absorption. Recent research provides evidence that a well-balanced gut flora (bacteria) may play an important role in the development of obesity and insulin resistance. We are learning more about these powerful organisms every day.

Where can I find probiotics? Probiotics occur naturally in fermented dairy foods like yogurt, kefir, and aged cheeses. Some non-dairy alternatives include kimchi, sauerkraut, miso, tempeh, and soy beverages. Probiotics are also available as supplements, however, it is always preferable to obtain your probiotics from natural food sources, as you’ll benefit from the vitamins, minerals and other nutrients that are part of these foods’ nutritional profile. Be cautious about products that do not naturally contain probiotics, like granola bars or cereals. Their efficacy has not been proven.

Are probiotic supplements safe? It is important to do your homework before choosing a probiotic supplement due to the various strains and dosages available. Read the ingredients label carefully. It should state ‘live’ or ‘active’ cultures, and provide a list of the strains.  It should also state the purpose of the product–what medical/ health condition it addresses – and a link to user-friendly scientific studies that support the claim. If you choose supplements, start slow and pay attention to your body’s response: large doses may cause side effects like upset stomach or diarrhea. Those who are acutely ill or have a compromised immune system are cautioned to speak to their physician before taking a probiotic supplement. In fact, anybody considering a supplement should first consult with a nutrition professional, e.g., Registered Dietitian, with experience in probiotic supplementation.

How much do I need? There is no standard recommended daily requirement for probiotics, hence, it’s important to speak to a qualified nutrition professional.

Probiotics are a necessary part of your health toolkit. As with all things, seek God first, ask for discernment and guidance for the care of your temple, and then proceed with confidence.
