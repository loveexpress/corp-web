Antioxidants - Don't Believe All The Hype

By Elizabeth Madison

When working with a new client, I always ask if they are taking antioxidant supplements like vitamin A, E or beta-carotene. Their answer is usually yes. When I ask why they take these supplements, I get answers such as to prevent or cure cancer, reverse the aging process, or to even eliminate cataracts. At this point, I launch into teaching mode! As a Registered Dietitian, I want to educate my clients and help them avoid any harmful side effects resulting from taking the wrong products. Ensuring my clients avoid wasting their money on products with false promises is an extra bonus! I’d like to do the same for Love Express readers.

Before we talk about antioxidants, let’s first talk about oxidation. Oxidation is the chemical process related to how things break down. A partially eaten apple turning brown is an example of oxidation.  Oxidation, a fundamental part of our biology, occurs whenever you eat food or exercise. Oxidation also occurs when your body is exposed to stressors like smoke, air pollution and sunlight.

Oxidation reactions produce a by-product called free radicals which can trigger cell damage. There is a strong link between free radicals and conditions like cardiovascular disease, premature aging, cancer, and cataracts, to name a few. Because oxidation is a basic biological function, your body has a built-in mechanism, a supply of antioxidants, to protect itself against free radicals. Your body makes these antioxidants and also gets them from food sources.

The general belief is that we can help our bodies fight off free radicals by taking antioxidant supplements. Unfortunately, this is where marketing and science part ways.  Research has found no evidence that taking antioxidants in supplement form can prevent disease or aging. In fact, taking antioxidant supplements like vitamin E can actually be harmful, especially for people taking certain medications. The reason why research outcomes have been so disappointing is that the experiments have focused on taking the antioxidant in a form where it has been removed from its original source - the natural food. The understanding now is that antioxidants work best in their natural form – contained within healthy, natural, unprocessed foods. For example, getting your vitamin A from carrots or vitamin E from avocadoes is a much more effective way to get your antioxidants.

Though supplementation has not been shown to be effective, a healthy diet rich in fruits and vegetables has been shown to contribute to the prevention of certain diseases. Remember, it is not only the antioxidant that’s keeping you healthy, but the whole food and all of its components. So the goal is to pack your daily food plan with nutrition powerhouses, or what are sometimes referred to as “Superfoods.”

Here are my favorite Superfoods which deliver antioxidants plus other essential nutrients:

Beans – high in fiber, protein and potassium.

Cranberries, blueberries, raspberries and blackberries – good sources of vitamin C, fiber and potassium.

Kale, collards and other dark leafy greens – great sources of vitamins A and C.

Nuts – walnuts and almonds are a good source of omega-3 fatty acids.

Sweet potatoes (baked, not fried!) – excellent source of potassium and good source of vitamin A.

So don’t believe everything you hear in the media or read on the front of food product packages. Stick to the basics for optimal health – you’ll save money and feel great. As it says Proverbs 24:4
