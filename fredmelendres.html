Praise & Prayer Rally

for the Philippines

by Pastor Fred Melendres

Prayer Rally

Since 2004, the Filipino Born Again Christian in New York and New Jersey had been gathering together every month to praise and worship the Lord and specifically pray for their nation, the Philippines. These Filipino Christians believed that Praise / Worship and Prayer are some of the key components of God’s commands in order to promote peace, prosperity, reconciliation and unity in their beloved country.

Last June 14, 2008 Saturday, this monthly event was special because it coincided with the celebration of 110th year of their country’s independence. The Philippines was under Spanish rule from 1565 to June 12, 1898. The Praise and Prayer Rally was held at Household of Faith Church, 212-40 Jamaica Avenue, Queens Village, NY, from 1pm until 6pm.

The event was attended by various churches; Household of Faith Church, Word Int'l Ministries, Fil-Am Christian Community Center, Brooklyn Evangelical Church, Universal Faith Ministries, Body of Christ Christian Church, and other churches from the tri-state. Each church was assigned to lead the whole congregation for a 30 minutes praise, worship and prayer for the Philippines. Prayer concerns of various issues like idolatry, religiosity, superstition, political instability, poverty, moral redemption, government officials, and the millions of dispersed Filipinos around the globe had been lifted up and cried out to the Lord God Almighty.

Aside from the Philippines, there are also other prayer focuses that they had been prayed for. Pastor Elisha Sohan prayed for the United States of America; Pastor Melendres for the State of Israel; Pastor Malto for Persecuted Churches; and Pastor Agtani for Myanmar and China which had been struck by the calamities recently. “It is time for Christians in America to rise up and be the Lord’s vessel of righteousness in such a time as this,” says Pastor Sohan. “As the state of Israel celebrated her 60th year of Independence, may the God of Abraham continue to surround the Holy Land with His fire and glory,” prayed by Pastor Melendres.

During the praise and worship time, one worship leader commented saying that, “it is time for us Filipino Christians here in NY and NJ to lead our people in unity and reconciliation.” Indeed, if we Christians will not unite ourselves, how God can hear our prayers for our country’s unity and reconciliation. Let God be our audience and let Him see the spirit of humility and meekness among His people.

At the end of the rally, Pastor Dichoso closed the affair with thanksgiving and prayer for the glory of the Lord Jesus Christ. Immediately, the dinner was served. Next month’s prayer rally will be held in New Jersey, and everyone is cordially invited.
