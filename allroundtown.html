AllAroundTown

November 2014

By Ms. Gee

The First Stop was at Calvary Baptist Church in Manhattan, where Pastor Epstein is the senior Pastor, for the City serve-City Fest. This ministry is hosted by the Luis Palau Foundation. What I found most amazing about this conference was the coming together of many churches regardless of color and religion. I looked to my left I saw my Latinos brother and sisters, mixed in with my Afri­can American brothers and sisters, mixed in with white, Chinese and so on. It remin­ded me how it would be in heaven, all of us in one accord, with one thing on our minds praising our Father. City serve, City Fest is not about one person but all God’s children serving the community and letting the community know about the good news of Jesus Christ. The key note was presented by Luis Palau, if he ever wanted to change careers, he would be a great comedian. 

Elder Palau just expressed it will take all us to come together to make this great, not just one of us or a few of us. His son Kevin said well “This is where we put down our egos & logos and lift up the name of Jesus,” Luis Palau went on to say it is the time to celebrate & accelerate to impact different ways to serve the church. City Serve will first have local city walks which includes painting cleaning and sprucing up the neighborhoods. Regional outreach will be all over the tri-state area that will showcase different fa­cets of this ministry. Lastly but of course, there will be a huge multicultural outdoor concert. It really is not just about cleaning up the neighborhoods, creating gardens, but most importantly creating relationships and introducing the community to Christ. 

So many pastors came through to show their support, (too many to name eve­rybody) Dr. A.R. Bernard, Pastor Floyd Flake, Pastor Althea Taylor, Youth Pastor Jeremy Rios Esq., Bishop Hector Banano, Pastor Cymbala, and we cannot forget Bishop Hezekiah Walker, who directed everyone attending to sing the number one song in the country and written by Elder David Bratton, “Every Praise.” We got a glimpse of heaven seeing everyone singing on one accord.  If you would like to be a part of this great movement just go to www.NYCIty Serve.org/launch.

Next Stop was in Brooklyn, NY at Full Faith Christian Center, for Beauty for Ashes Global Outreach Mi­nistries Third Anniversary hosted by First Lady Jea­nette Willi­ams. This was one of the most anoin­ted and powerful anniversary I have ever had the pleasure to go to and the honor of being the MC. The dance ministry who started off the service was “For His Glory”, an awesome dance ministry and Lady Candace Edge brought us in the presence of the most high. The icing on the cake was my big sister and anointed woman of God, Apostle Patricia Wiley, gave us a mighty word that will last me a life time. I pray that Beauty to Ashes last forever, for it is a much needed ministry.

Next stop we landed in New Haven CT. for the ordination to Eldership of our Min. Elder Larnel Satchel held at Mt. Calvary Holy Church Revival Center, where the Senior Pastor is Elder Daniel B. Bland. What made this so very special is that Elder Bland himself preached this ordination and our sister, Loretta Satchel, also was ordained as a trus­tee. It was a pleasure for doc and I to join our family on such an astounding occasion. Congrats to all the candidates and may God bless you all and thank you Elder Clay Johnson for leading us to worship.

Next we slid into Canticles at the Gospel Lighthouse for Panel Discussion on Mu­sic, ministry and marriage-how artists balance all three. This was a great opportunity for artists to understand and perfect their craft. It was a great opportunity for anyone who had questions but did not know who to turn too. Our panelists were, Dove winner Elder David Bratton, Timothy, Gaby Cudjoe, Missy Billups who also blessed us in song with a new look and a new sound. This was one event every artist needed to be there to help with their ministry.

Next stop Germantown PA for the surprise party for Dr.D’s sister Sherri. Dr. D was the surprise DJ and believe me she was very surprised when she saw Dr. D and I. It was so wonderful to see our Godmother Ma Ton­ya and to meet our new niece. Anytime you are with people who really love you it is a pleasure to go out of your way. Great to see Pam, her mother, Trevor, little Dee, Tamika Patton and April and let us not forget the wonderful parents who gave birth to this awesome woman who look out for the underprivileged and always finding ways to help people in any way she can.

Very last stop Crest Hollow Country club in Woodbury, NY for the wedding of our niece and new nephew Mr. & Mrs. Jamaal Miller. Our niece Shaunte was so beautiful and Jamaal, was just so handsome. If you have never been to Crest Hollow the food is divine and the atmosphere perfect. It was a beautiful occasion, along with family and friends. Seeing a man and woman up holding the principals of God’s word is always pleasing. Congrats Shaunte and Jamaal wishing you God’s speed.

Ms. Gee
