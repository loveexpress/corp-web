Grace Cathedral Int’l
Bishop R. W. Harris

On January 16th, 2014 Bi­shop Harris along with several outstanding men of Long Island received a “David Award” which was presented by Net­working Magazine. The Love Express congratulates Bishop Harris on his outstanding service to the community here and abroad.

A man of deep faith and conviction, Bi­shop R. W. Harris showed his propensity to community service and religious ministry and from an early age. Born in St. Pe­tersburg, Florida, he grew up in East Harlem, NY, where he became a junior member of the NAACP at the age of 17. Under the guidance of Percy Sutton, a prominent black Ameri­can lawyer, elected official, civil rights activist and business leader from the 1950s to the 1970s, the young Harris learned about community involvement, and traveled on the NAACP bus to the March on Wash­ing­ton. There, he was inspired by the thought-provoking speech delivered by Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.

Harris graduated from John Jay College of Cri­mi­nal Justice and the F.B.I. National Academy. He served New York City for 21 years as a member of the Housing Police De­part­ment, and during that time, was dispatched to facilities throughout the five boroughs. In 1986, he retired from law enforcement as a Detective Lieutenant.

In his last years of City employment, Harris sharpened his vision of service to God and man. He entered the Church of Christ Bible Institute in Harlem and re­ceived a Bachelor of Arts degree in Religious Educa­tion and Theology, and was ordained in 1983.

Harris and his wife No­vella, along with their four children, began religious services on Wednesday evenings in their home in Uniondale, Long Island. To­gether, they established the Grace Cathedral In­ter­na­tio­nal church. Under Har­ris’s ministry, the church prospered, and he as­sumed the full time position of pastor. In 1995, the church found a permanent home on Jeru­salem Ave­nue. Soon after, Grace Ca­thedral purchased land from Nassau County to construct a facility to provide quality, affordable child care, and in May 1995, opened the Sure Foun­da­tion Child Care Cen­ter. The day-time and the after-school programs are under the di­rection of Har­ris’s wife, an elementary school teacher with a Mas­ter’s degree in education.

Ten years later, the church expanded again. With funding from Nassau County, Town of Hemp­stead and New York State, the congregation built its Grace Multi-Cultural Cen­ter, which connects the pre-school building to the church. While funding was available, the church provided a Saturday morning free re­spite program, giving families of special needs children a break from the stres­ses of life.

Two years into his mi­ni­stry, Harris began leading international crusades to Ghana, Africa, where his fa­ther had ministered before him.

“One can’t attend to people’s spiritual needs when they are hungry,” re­marks Harris, and on his vi­sits, he not only preaches to the people, but also distributes food, clothing and other necessities. In Gha­na, he also constructed a small orphanage. In addition to three visits to Ghana, Har­ris has traveled to Ethiopia, Nigeria, Kenya, Uganda and South Africa. He conducted two crusades in Madras and Ooty, India, as well as Cebu City in the Philippines.

More recently, Harris journeyed twice to Haiti to assist in the relief effort after the 2010 earthquake that rocked the small country. After assessing the needs of the people, he made a second crusade to Haiti, ac­companied by some 20 people - many of whom were nurses, teachers and medi­cal professionals. For this trip, he had also coordinated with the Nas­sau County Medical Center to bring me­dical supplies.

Harris is mindful of the social challenges and peer pressure that young people face, and he notes, “If you don’t provide young people with enough positive things to do, they will naturally tend toward destructive ac­tivi­ties.” The church, therefore, began a youth group, called GANG, God’s Anoin­ted New Generation. Three students accompanied Har­ris on a crusade to Ethiopia in the fall of 2013.

Celebrating its 30th an­niversary, Grace Cathe­dral International has grown to include over 500 parishio­ners from as far west as Queens and Brooklyn and as far east as Suffolk Coun­ty.

Harris was appointed as one of three founding Commissioners of the Nas­sau County Con­di­tional Re­lease Commission (Parole Board). He served three years on the Com­mission before resigning to accept a chaplaincy position with the County’s Police De­partment, and is now serving in his 22nd year. Harris also serves Nassau County as a member of the Crimi­nal Justice Coordinating Coun­cil.

Partnering with the community service programs of the Nassau Coun­ty Department of Probation and the Education and As­sistance Corporation, Har­ris has enabled numerous youth to successfully complete hundreds of community service hours as an al­ternative to incarceration. In addition, he’s partnered with the Nassau County De­part­ment of Social Ser­vi­ces to provide a training site for Work Experience Pro­gram clients.

For the past two years, Harris has initiated a food distribution program at the Grace Cathedral location. In cooperation with Shop Rite, the church prepares 500 bags of non-perishable groceries that are distributed free-of-charge to the community.

Harris retains membership in NOBLE (National Organization of Black Law Enforcement Executives), the Guardians Association (Police Chaplain) and the Board of Directors on One Hundred Black Men of Nas­sau and Suffolk Counties. He’s a Silver Life Member in the Freeport-Roosevelt NAACP, and serves as pre­sident of the Uniondale Clergy Association and a member of the Long Island Conference of Clergy.

Uniondale residents since 1975, Harris and his wife, Novella, are the parents of four grown children who are continuing the family tradition of making a difference in our community. Darlene Harris McCabe, Esq. is a Commissioner on the Nassau County Assess­ment Review Commission; Robert Wayne Harris is an educator; Richard Troy Har­ris has served as Uniondale Fire Commissio­ner; and Ja­lana Harris, LMCSW is a Ph.D. candidate.
