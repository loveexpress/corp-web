The Gospel, Politics & The Future

John Perkins
Brian McLaren

One Thousand People, Sixty Leading Scholars, Artists, Activists, and Pastors. Twenty Learning Tracks. A broad array of theological perspectives, all focused on one thing. Christian Engagement in the Public Square. The Envision 08 Conference at Princeton University, June 8-10, 2008.

On June 8-10, dozens of America's most prominent evangelical, and mainline Christians will gather at Princeton Theological Seminary to draft and adopt Envision the Future, a statement charting Christians' role in advancing the common good in the public square.


Kay Warren

Samuel Rodriguez

The large gathering will include luminaries such as Kay Warren, David Gus­hee, Jim Wallis, Richard Cizik and Samuel Rodriguez among its distinguished panel of evangelicals.

The conference will present a rare opportunity for Evangelicals, Mainline Protestants, Pentecostals, African Americans, Asians, Hispanics, Native Americans, Whites and every other group represented in the church universal to come together to talk about the church's role in society in what promises to be one of the most significant Christian gatherings of the decade, these leaders who stand at the center of the Christian community's rising justice movement will draw on their diverse experience and shared faith to forge common ground for the next century of evangelical public engagement.

Guests include: Jay Bakker, Randall Balmer, Alise Barrymore, Rita Nakashima Brock, Malinda Berry, Bart Campolo, Noel Castellanos, Richard Cizik, Shane Claiborne, Miguel de la Torre, Jeremy Del Rio, R. M. Keelan Downton, Deborah Fikes, Mimi Haddad, Nancy Haradesty, Lisa Sharon Harper, Obery Hendricks, Bruce Herman, Al Hsu, David Kuo, Alexei Laushkin, Daisy L. Machado, Kathy Maskell, Michael Mata, Terry McGonigal, Brian McLaren, Claude Nikondeha, Ruth Padilla DeBorst, Doug Pagitt, John Perkins, Raymond Rivera, Samuel Rodriguez, Gabriel Salguero, Brenda Salter McNeil, Lara Scott, Samir Selmanovic, Ronald Sider, Andrea Smith, Michael Smitheram, Bowie Snodgrass, Richard Twiss, Moroslav Volf, Jim Wallis, Kay Warren, Paul Knitter, and Randy Woodley.
