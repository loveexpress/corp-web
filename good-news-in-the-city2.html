The Harvest Is Ripe

Over 85,000 have heard the "Good News in the City"

All Roads Leading to July 11th Free Festival on the Great Lawn of Central Park

More than 85,000 people in the New York Tri-State area have heard the “Good News in the City” as shared by Luis and Andrew Palau at NY CityFest, NY CityServe and other related events over the past several weeks.

To date, free NY City­Fest festivals have been held in Harlem (May 30), Manhattan (May 30), Flushing Mea­dows (June 5), Bridgeport, CT (June 6) and Bronx (June 6), drawing tens of thousands and featuring musical performances by do­zens of notable music artists from the pop, rap, reggae, Latino, gospel and rock genres such as American Idol standout Madisa, multiple Grammy-winners For King & Country, Grammy-winning gospel star Bishop Hezekiah Walker, Grammy-nominated gospel singer-songwriter Va­Shawn Mitchell, Christian hip-hop artist and producer Andy Mi­neo, and many more. At each event, Luis and Andrew Pa­lau have shared the “Good News in the City” message of hope through Jesus.

In addition to the many attending the festivals, tens of thousands have been served by NY CityServe, an outreach that launched in NYC in September 2014, when hundreds of leaders representing more than 1,200 churches in the NYC area joined with local business leaders, political leaders and the Luis Palau Association, and pledged to love and serve all of New York Ci­ty. Hundreds of events have taken place since then, and many more are planned in the upcoming months.

“There is a great gos­pel movement happening right now in New York Ci­ty,” said Luis Palau, one of the most prominent evangelists in the world today. “New York is probably the most culturally diverse ci­ty to have ever existed on the face of the Earth, and everywhere we turn in the 5 boroughs and surrounding communities, the enthusiasm for hearing the message of the gospel has been way beyond our expectations. While recent studies may show more and more people turning away from organized religion, I can tell you that in New York City right now, those people are not turning away from faith in Je­sus Christ.”

There are three more regional NY CityFest festivals: Long Island (June 12), Elizabeth, NJ (June 27) and Clinton, NJ (July 5), and a special one-night-only, free concert at Radio City Music Hall (July 7) featuring four-time Grammy winner Third Day, five-time Dove Award-winner, Newsboys, & America’s Got Talent finalist David Garibaldi.

Then, on July 11th, all of the Tri-State area will come together to celebrate the “Good News in the City” when NY CityFest with Luis Palau, a massive free festival on the Great Lawn in Central Park, will bring a constellation of stars from music, television and sports together to perform and inspire, including Grammy-Award winning artists Mandi- sa, Toby Mac, Marcos Witt, Israel Houghton, Matt Redman, and Hezekiah Walker, alongside celebrity speakers such as NY Yankees standout Mariano Rivera and Latin star Myrka Dellanos. Luis Palau will present a special address during the massive gathering, to reflect on the city’s diversity, as churches of all denominations and ethnic backgrounds have come together from all five boroughs and the Tri-State area to make the festival possible.

About Luis Palau: Lu­is Palau has personally shared the Gospel with more than 30 million people through evangelistic events in more than 75 nations, and hundreds of millions more through radio and television. Luis Palau festivals have produced some of the largest audiences ever recorded in ci­ties from south Florida to South America. His ra­dio broadcasts in both English and Spanish are heard by millions on more than 5,000 radio outlets in 48 countries. He has authored close to 50 books, contri­buted articles on issues of faith to countless publications, and counseled business leaders, political lea- ders, and heads of state around the world.

For details on the festival and how to attend NY CityFest events, visit www.  NYCityFest.org.
