Staying Healthy On Vacation

By Elizabeth Madison

Vacations are all about relaxing and enjoying yourself, but we don’t want to return home feeling heavier and your clothes fitting tighter. Here are 10 easy strategies to help keep your waistline intact:

 #1 Take God with You. If you haven’t made conversation with God and daily bible study a regular habit, vacation is the perfect time to start because you won’t feel rushed by your busy schedule. 

 #2 Be Prepared. It’s easy to rack up extra calories while vacationing. Maintain your usual eating schedule while on vacation-don’t skip meals or wait too long to eat, or hit the midnight buffet.

 #3 Fit in Fitness. Aim to exercise at least 45-60 minutes daily. It doesn’t have to be strenuous - you can take a hike, play volleyball, or sign up for an aerobics class. It all counts towards good health.

 #4 Indulge in Moderation. Be picky. What do you really want? What have you never tried? Select those foods that really appeal to you and enjoy them.

 #5 Have Five A Day. Aim to have at least 5 servings of fruits and vegetables daily—1-2 servings for breakfast, 2 for lunch, and 2 for dinner. This strategy lets you fill up on low-calorie, highly nutritious foods, while making room to indulge.

 #6 Wash Hands Often before handling food. If you know you will be outdoors for long hours without access to a washroom, keep sanitary wipes or sanitizers handy.

 #7 Pack Safe. If you are bringing food, choose shelf-stable treats that are easy to enjoy on the go. These snacks can also help tide you over between meals. Healthy options include cereal, trail mix, nuts, peanut butter sandwiches, crackers, fruit and vegetable sticks.

 #8 Stay Hydrated. Whether you’re traveling dusty ruins or waiting in line at amusement parks, drink plenty of fluids. If you feel thirsty, you are already dehydrated.

 #9 Practice Portion Control: Make every plate ½ veggies, ¼ protein, ¼ starch. You’ll still enjoy your favorite foods in moderation and get your daily dose of nutrients.

 #10 Practice Your Pause Button. Stay tuned into your hunger. Before you grab for something, ask yourself: am I physically hungry? If you are craving only certain foods, that’s a sign of emotional hunger. If you recognize that you’re not hungry for food, then find something to do instead of eating.
