Spirit of Christmas!

Edith M. Stranges Editor / Publisher

Philippians 4:6-7 Do not be anxious about anything, but in everything, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving present your request to God, and the peace of God which transcends all understanding, will grant your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus.

Today, the Christmas season creeps in earlier and earlier. Gift catalogs begin arriving at the end of summer, decorations appears in stores way before Halloween, as the push toward December 25 and New Year’s; it’s so much easy to forget the spiritual meaning of Christmas.

It is a struggle for families, single moms, students, hurricane victims and those who lost a job. The pressure is one to buy gifts, send out cards, holiday dinners, decorate the office and home. Christmas is a time to achieve a balance between our inner lives and the hectic pace of the outside world. On Christmas Day, many are left exhausted and anxious to get everything over with; somehow we missed something. Where was the peace and joy that supposedly came at this time. The message the angels declared to the shepherds. Peace on Earth? Where, oh Lord is that peace?

I would cry “Where is the spirit of Christmas, where’s the joy, until I got quiet. That’s the key; quietness in your soul. I heard the Lord say to me “I’m your joy all year long.” What a liberating moment, to let all the stuff go, it was just stuff. What’s important is our relationship with Jesus. The world views Christmas as just another opportunity to party, and have a family gathering. I know. I used to think that way, and I’m sure before Jesus came into your life, your belief system was the same. Today be liberated and enjoy this time with your family. If you don’t have enough money to purchase gifts, send them a homemade card with a list of things you will do for them. Be encouraged. Tell someone today, Jesus loves them. Get busy working for the Kingdom of God.

Remember to support your church and visit your local Christian bookstores. Continue to support local ministries who help the less fortunate.

P.S. Be alert, ask questions, don’t be fooled, don’t be misled by anything that is called “Christian.” The goal of The Love Express is not to be a pa­per of competition, strife or jealousy, although sometimes it appears that way. My heart is for the reconciliation of Body of Christ.

Remember January is Fasting Month.

Edith
