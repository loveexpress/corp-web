An Overcomer's Story:
of the Founder of Collyde & Collyde Summit

By Jinu Thomas

One of my favorite quotes is from C.S. Lewis’ The Problem of Pain, where he says “God whispers to us in our pleasures, speaks in our conscience, but shouts in our pains.” Pain, sufferings or life’s uncertainty has a strange way of allowing us to hear God's voice more clearly. I believe that sometimes life's hardest moments gives birth to heaven-inspired dreams and visions. Collyde, a small mi­ni­stry based in New Jersey, was born during one of the most desperate times in my life.

It was February 2006; I was 30 years old. I seem to have a good career, a great family and things seem to be, by American standards, really comfortable. Except for a holy unrest that had risen in my heart. Am I here on earth to fulfill a purpose? Does God have a specific plan in my life? Was I created with a unique calling? David writes in Psalm 139 – “…all the days ordained for me were written in your book before one of them came to be.” That meant: I am not random. I was created with intention by my Creator and He has a plan for me. I started fasting and praying for a new vision in my life and I was given this one line, which became the founding vision statement of Collyde – “Create a meeting place where belief and unbelief collides in a perfect collision”.

Collyde, today, has three initiatives (to learn more, see www.collyde.org), but the one that we are really excited for in 2014 is the Collyde Summit. The Collyde Summit is a gathering of passionate believers in the New York, New Jersey and Pennsylvania area, who love Jesus and are convicted to become world changers. Collyde Summit 2014 will be taking place Sept. 19th and 20th at Christ Church in Rockaway, NJ. We anticipate 1200 believers and this year, for the first time, the Collyde Summit is kicking-off four specialized tracks for attendees, of all ages and interests, to choose from: Leadership Essentials, Creative Arts & Media, Worship Experience and Justice, Culture & Redemption.

We will be joined by ar­tist, singer, and song-writer, Jeremy Camp and some of our speakers include Dr. David Ireland, Rebekah Lyons, Tim Lucas, Naomi Zacha­rias, Eugene Cho, Brad Lomenick and many others. The Collyde Summit will be filled with energy, you’ll find, and deep yearnings for change. It is a time of calling out to God, asking for our lives and world to be different. It is a time of worship and praise, communion and remembering all God has done, and all He will do. To register, go to collyde summit .com

“Overcome” is this year’s theme, based off Je­sus’ words of preparation to His disciples, in John 16:33, before His death and resurrection. “In this world you will have trouble, but take heart! I have overcome the world.” We’re told to expect trials, heartache and setbacks, yet still hold onto hope. Our trials and pain are not the end of the story, you see; He overcame! Collyde’s story is one of Overcome and the truth is, we all have stories of Overcome. What’s yours?
