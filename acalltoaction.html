What about Us? A Call to Action

On October 29, 2010, the organization known as FACES NY was informed by the Human Resources Administration that the 2.5 million dollar HIV/AIDS housing contract would be terminated effective March 2011. What about us?

FACES NY was foun­ded in 1986 and is the oldest minority-owned and operated AIDS services organization in New York City. During their 25 years of faithful service to the community FACES NY has developed a proven track record of excellence in HIV/AIDS housing services.

The contract in question provides housing for 100 HIV/AIDS clients and their families. Termination of this contract could possibly displace one hundred or more families living with HIV/AIDS in the Harlem, Upper Manhattan and Bronx communities. If this attempt is successful, it would not only affect the many families who live in these apartments, but would have a significant effect on the agency itself, and would result in the loss of 48% of the agencies over­all budget and 50% of the agency staff. What about us?

This call to action requires a mass mobilization of the faith-based community on behalf of this stellar non-profit organization, in order to reverse this attempt to displace these families and disrupt the stability of many lives. As faith leaders, we have an obligation to respond to the needs of our parishioners, as well as those of the residents in the communities where we worship, live and work. In that spirit, I am calling on you to join this effort and participate with us in a series of community action activities aimed at bringing serious attention to this issue. Your support is critical to assisting us in sending out a message to the City of New York that we will not allow our communities to be treated in this manner.

On February 21, 2011, we will be hosting a community forum at Kelly Temple Church of God in Christ, located at 10-12 East 130th Street, Harlem, NY, to bring together Clergy/Faith based community, People living with HIV/AIDS, Community Residents and Elected Officials. This forum will provide the community with current information and solicit feedback on this critical issue.

Also, during the next few weeks, we will be calling on you to join us as we conduct meetings, circulate flyers, petitions, participate in hearings, and make phone calls and a whole series of related activities to address this grave injustice in our community. We urge you to take a stand, and respond to this battle cry! Include your churches voice in this chorus of intolerance. Your support and active participation is needed to ensure that this attack on our communities is not only fought but won!!

Submitted by Pastor Sharlene Nash-Pryor, Greater Works Deliverance Church and Pastor Jay Gooding, Fellowship Tabernacle and Miracle Revival Temple, Bronx, NY. Tel: (718) 652-7620
