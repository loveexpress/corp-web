Appointed President of Concerts of Prayer Greater NY

by Dimas Salaberrios

By unanimous vote by its Board of Directors, Concerts of  Prayer Greater NY (COPGNY) has chosen Pastor Dimas Sala­berrios as its new President. He has been on the COPGNY Board of Directors for several years, and is the Foun­der and Pastor of Infinity Bible Church in the South Bronx, NY.

COPGNY’s core objective is to involve the spiritual leadership of Greater New York to build a united prayer movement to intercede for four issues: revival in the chu­rches, reconciliation between churches and races, reformation of society, and spreading the Gospel to the glory of His Son. With Dimas’ vast experience around the world, with the youth in the inner city, and his intimate involvement throughout the city of New York, he will bring a fresh new prospective to the core COPGNY initiatives as well as, implementing new ways to reach out to the local Body of Christ to see COPG NY’s core objectives realized. He is already lending new ideas to the 2014 Pastors’ Prayer Summit, taking place January 27-29 at Tuscarora Inn, by collaborating with multiple ministries to unite leaders in prayer.

In 2004, the Billy Graham Evangelical Association (BGEA) appointed Dimas to Co-Chair the Youth Committee, and serve on the budget and finance committee for their 2005 Greater New York Crusade. He coordinated the BGEA Prayer Breakfast and was the opening speaker during the Saturday evening service of the crusade to an audience of over 240,000.

As a worldwide missionary, teacher, and speaker, Pastor Dimas has shared the Gospel of Jesus Christ on every continent except Ant­arctica. He planted a church in Kenya and smuggled Bi­bles to the persecuted church in China. In 2010, Dimas and the Infinity team provided over 50,000 tons of food, wa­ter, and supplies to Haiti after the earthquake; and in 2012 received a Proclamation by the Council of the City of New York for service as a first responder in providing aid and delivering nearly $500,000 worth of supplies to Super Storm Sandy victims across NYC.

Dimas was central in the New York City “Right-To-Worship” movement, which ignited cross-congregational prayer gatherings throughout the city, and sought to protect places of worship for over 17,000 congregants from unwarranted eviction from rental spaces owned by the city of New York.

Dimas, in partnership with Tim Keller and Redee­mer City to City, founded Infinity NY Church, planted in the South Bronx at Bronx River Housing Projects. Infi­nity Bible Church targets the at-risk, gang-ridden youth in the neighborhood and has spearheaded numerous outreach events and discipleship groups. Dimas has mobilized weekly prayer walks, which have spread from the Bronx to Manhattan and Queens. Since the church’s arrival, nearly eight years ago, homicides and other violent crimes have been virtually eliminated at Bronx River due to the prayer walks. Police Commissioner Raymond Kelly credits Pastor Dimas and Infinity for the notable drop in crime. As President of COPGNY he will add his first hand experience from these prayer walks to the PRAY NY! initiative which has been mobilizing over 300 churches to prayer walk their zip codes throughout the five boroughs since 2004.

Dimas holds a Master of Divinity degree from Alliance Theological Seminary and resides in the Bronx with his wife Tiffany and their two daughters.
