Build Your Legacy Now

By Jack Redmond

Building legacy is a pro­cess. If you want your life to make a difference now and for generations to come, the more you build now, the more people you will impact for decades to come!

Maybe that’s a little too heavy, a little too serious for some, but it’s true, it’s reality. So first what is legacy?

Basically, legacy is anything handed down from the past, as from an ancestor or predecessor. So we all leave legacy, good or bad and yes, leaving “nothing’ to someone is a legacy. We have also received a legacy which we can build on if good, and multiply the blessing for ma­ny. For most, our goal is to leave  better legacy than we received.

In a sense we pass on who we are to others. We pass on a family, spiritual, educational and even a financial legacy to others. I have received a fairly good family legacy from my parents. I have received from some very good sports and educational legacies from the schools I attended.

Spiritually, I received no legacy from my family but am part of a rich spiritual legacy from my church. Like most people, the legacy I received is a mixed bag. Most people I talk to also often feel like they have been building a mixed legacy to hand down to their children, their city and society as a whole.

Legacy Must Be Built!

The issue is that a great legacy must be built! For ma­ny people, they come to Christ and trade a foundation of sinking sand for the rock or Jesus Christ but don’t purposely start to build a strong legacy. They are like one of those weather guys who get sent to stand in the midst of a hurricane as it hits land and are trying to not get blown away! This is how ma­ny Christians live. Yes, they have chosen Christ as a foundation but have not built a strong house to actually live in.

Focus on the Basics

By focusing on basics, each basic can be like a pillar of the house you are building to pass down to others. Some pillars we have identified include:

  • Prayer
  • Persona
  • Purity
  • Purpose
  • Priority
  • Perseverance
  • Power

Though these do not address every area of life, they can help you build the type of legacy that will be a blessing as it is passed on to others. Wherever you are at this point, I would recommend that you identify seve­ral areas of your life that you would like to intentionally build your legacy. If you would like resource and gui­dance in this process, you can see our newest book: The 7 Pillars of a Legacy Minded Man which can act as a road map as you navigate building an awesome legacy!

Building legacy now will make your life better in the present and leave an impact for generations to come. Don’t just settle for what you have been given, grow into more! And don’t settle on passing on a mediocre le­ga­cy because God wants you to powerfully impact people both in your life now, and in generations to come. Hap­py building!

Jack Redmond is an author and local church pastor who can be reached at www.4thgen.org. To learn more about The 7 Pillars of a Legacy Minded Man and other Men’s Mini­stry resources and confe­rences, go to www.legacy mindedmen.org.
