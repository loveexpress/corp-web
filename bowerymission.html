Entrepreneurial Crack Addict

Last Christmas, Harry was assaulted on the subway platform. He woke up on the train tracks with a broken leg, ankle and ribs.

Harry Ecclesiastes’ drug use cost him everything. He was faced with living on the streets. But Harry always has an angle. He turned an abandoned room in the subway into an apartment. Then he formed his addicted friends into a work crew to salvage copper from abandoned subway areas, which they sold for drug money.

One day Harry noticed one of his drug dealers driving a brand-new Lexus. Harry said, “Hey, man. How did you afford this?” The drug dealer answered, “I brought this just off the money I’ve made from you.”

“It knocked me over,” says Harry. “I realized: If I wasn’t doing drugs, I’d be rich!” Harry went to The Bowery Mission, quit drugs, graduated, got a job, and began saving money.

As he focused more and more on making money, he had less time for church or friends. He drifted into the wrong crowd, started coming up with ‘creative’ ways to get by. “I always think I can beat the system,” he admits. “I think God wanted to get my attention before it was too late.”

Last Christmas, Harry was assaulted on the subway platform. He woke up on the tracks with a broken leg, ankle and ribs. He spent months in the hospital.

None of his newfound friends visited him. He lost his job and his savings. By the time he completed his rehabilitation, he was facing living on the streets again.

“I thought of The Bowery Mission,” says Harry. “Would they take me back?” Of course. At the Mission there is always a 2nd, 3rd, 4th, chance.

What will Harry do differently this time? “I’ve got to change the way I think,” he says. “When I have a problem I can’t blame God or someone else. I can’t think I can do it myself. I need other people. The right people.”

Connect with God. Connect with people. Be honest with yourself. Good advice for any of us.
