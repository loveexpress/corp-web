Social Networking Part II
Teen & Tweens (Watch Out for "Creepers")

by Gary Scarano

To see this interview on ReachOUT.tv, please go to www.ReachOUT.tv and click on Show Times on the left for broadcast times in your area. You may also watch online.

Click here to view past articles written by Gary: www.ReachOUT.org/loveexpressarticles

In a National Ad Council video, a 13-year-old girl makes some online post, later, she is with friends at school and a ‘cute’ guy she does not know says "hi." Later, the Football Coach yells "Love the new Tattoo." At a movie, the ticket handler asks her what color underwear she has on. Pretty creepy, but an accurate description of what could happen if a child is not wise in their online posts. You can view this and other videos about social networking on our account at www.YouTube.com/ReachOutCom.

You have heard about sexual predators on the internet trying to connect to teens and tweens. Recently, an acquaintance of the author had their Facebook account broken into. All of their “friends” were contacted with an inappropriate website link. A few months ago, Facebook deleted almost 100,000 suspected sexual predator member accounts. We must face it; our spiritual enemy is after our youth and will use ANY means to get to them, including social networking.

In our intro article last month (www.ReachOUT.org/loveexpressarticles) Pastor Tim Hart explained that nothing on the internet is private and although children may not want their parents to see their posts, other people do. If your child is on Social Networks, then you can be sure many of his/her friends are also connected to them. Some adults may also be connected to your child. It is possible they will know more about what is going on in your child’s life then you. Finally, some unwelcome guest could be part of your child’s ‘community’ and some of them may be "creepers" as my daughters call them.

There are basically four threats to social networking, Sexual Predators (Creepers), Cyber Bullying (Posting unkind things about someone), Identity Theft, & Malware (Bad software). We are talking about creepers today.

How to Avoid Creepers:

• Let’s not be naïve. Look with a critical eye at the pictures/videos being posted on your children’s sites. To them it may seem innocent, but they may attract “creepers.”
• Follow the same rule online as you would on the street, Don’t talk to strangers.
• If you visited your grandparents, would you be embarrassed if they saw your online posts?

What Can Parents Do?

As a parent or guardian, there are basic suggestions I give in order of or preference:

1. Have the user names and passwords to all of your child's accounts and check them often (one parent may be enough).
2. Have unannounced sessions with your child and view their posting history.
3. Join the social networking phenomena and be connected to your children. You can see what they are posting, but you will not be able to view private conversation.
