Principles For Lasting Change

One of the universal desires of human beings is the desire to improve. It’s universal. We all want to grow. We all want to get better. We all want to improve. We all want to make something better of our lives.

America spends billions of dollars every year on pro­jects, products, gizmos and gadgets that they think will improve our lives or make our lives better. But sadly the results don’t really last. If we want Real change you’ve got to go back to the owner’s manual. The Bible.

The principles of lasting change begins when we Commit our body to God.

That’s the starting point. For change to happen in any area of your life whether you want financial, vocational, educational, mental, relatio­nal – it actually works best to begin with the physical. I commit my body to God. This is what the Bible says. Paul tells us that we can use our bodies as an act of worship:

“Therefore, I urge you, brothers, in view of God's mercy, to offer YOUR BODIES as living sacrifices, holy and pleasing to God--this is your spiritual act of worship.” We can commit our bo­dies to the lord as an act of worship in several ways:

 1) We can cleanse it.

I’m talking about detox. I’m talking about not putting poison in my body. By cleansing my body, it can be an act of worship. There may be some stuff that you’re putting in your body that you need to stop putting in your body. You need to cleanse your body of those toxins, those poisons, that bad stuff.

In 2 Corinth. 7:1 “Let us purify ourselves from everything that contaminates the body and spirit, perfecting holiness out of reverence for God.

Fasting and eating heal­thy is a great way to worship God, it’s a way to honor God as well cleanse your body at the same time.

 2) By caring for my body.

I can care for my body. That’s an act of worship too. I can care for my body daily. Caring for your body is an act of stewardship because remember, what you think you own is really on loan.

The Bible says this in Ephesians 5:29 “No one hates his own body [at least in his right mind] but lovingly cares for it, just as Christ cares for his body which is the church [the family of God].” So he says we care for our bodies just like Christ cares for his body the church. Caring for your body can be an act of worship. How do we care for your body? Keeping it in shape would be a good way to do that. That can be an act of worship to God.

3) By controlling my body.

We can best serve God with our bodies by Cleansing it, Caring for it, and Controlling it. Controlling my body is an act of worship to God when I do it out of gratitude, honor and submission to God.

1 Thessalonians 4:4 says this, “Each of you should learn to control his own body in a way that is holy and honorable.” In other words I control my body, it doesn’t control me. I don’t say, I couldn’t help myself.

1 Corinthians 9:27 “I discipline my body like an athlete, training it to do what it should.” That’s what the Bible says. That’s what God says. We are making a change in 2015 and we are starting with the physical.

The scripture begins with: offer your body. First change, you start with the body. Why? Because no matter what kind of change you want to make in your life – mental, physical, financial, spiritual, social, whatever – every change requires energy. The reason why most people don’t change…. is they don’t have the energy to do it. You’re just too tired to change.

Making a lasting change this year should begin with understanding that our bodies are so important not only to us but to God. Jesus bought it with His own blood, received 39 stripes for the healing of the 39 major diseases that exist today. The Holy Spirit wants to live in our bodies, God will resurrect our bodies and give it mortality in the future. So Honor it. Begin your journey to a lasting change. Begin with your body. Cleanse it, care for it, control it and use it as an act of worship to God.

Happy New Year 2015.

Pastor Matthew & Ka­thy Singh.
