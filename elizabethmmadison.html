Get A Healthy Heart
with the Mediterranean Diet

By Elizabeth M. Madison


May is Mediterranean Diet Month! I love this approach to eating because it is the perfect blend of heart healthy food that is full of flavor, easy to prepare, and has been prescribed as the "diet" for a long and healthy life.

This is not another fad diet. The Mediterranean culture has been studied extensively. There is strong evidence that this food plan can decrease your risk for heart disease, diabetes, cancer, Parkinson's and Alzheimer's disease. It also plays a major role in improving your quality of life and longevity. Even though you don’t live in countries bordering the Mediterranean Sea, you can still incorporate the elements of this amazing diet into your daily life. Try these 8 simple tips to get you started:

1. Fill half your plate with fruits and vegetables. This is key to getting your daily vitamins, minerals, antioxidants and other essential nutrients.

2. Always eat a high fiber breakfast. Aim for at least 8 grams of fiber to keep you full throughout the morning and keep your digestive system in top form.

3. Change the way you think about meat. We eat much more than we need! A serving of chicken, pork, beef or fish should take up no more than ¼ of your plate.

4. Eat fish and seafood at least twice a week to get enough omega-3 fatty acids, critical for optimal heart function.

5. Have a Meatless Monday to start your week off right. Try some bean-based recipes like Vegetarian Chili or Tuscan Kale and White Bean Soup (see Righteous Recipes section).

6. Increase your dairy intake. If you are lactose intolerant, try kefir, a delicious drinkable yogurt that is chock full of probiotics.

7. Use olive oil and safflower oil for cooking and flavoring instead of butter and other foods high in saturated fats.

8. Flavor foods with fresh herbs and spices instead of salt. Try marjoram, tarragon, sage and cumin.

The Mediterranean Diet is also a lifestyle, which includes exercise and social interaction. Move your body for at least 30 minutes a day. Do what you enjoy so you’ll stick with it, be it dancing, swimming biking, etc. Plan healthy sit-down dinners with family members at least three times a week. Not only will this encourage healthy eating habits, but it also provides priceless face time with the ones you love.

The Lord desires for us to have life, and have it more abundantly (John 10:10b). Following the Mediterranean Diet will help you fully enjoy all the blessings God has for you. Have a healthy and delicious May!

Elizabeth M. Madison, MA, CRC, Registered Dietitian, Certified Nutrition & Wellness Coach
