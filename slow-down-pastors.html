Slow Down Pastors

by Dimas Salaberrios

The demands on New York City Pastors are relentless. Manhattan is the most influential island in the world and in every survey the first or second most influential city (only second to London at times) in the world. What happens in NYC goes into hyper-drive through other cities. The surrounding boroughs supply the fuel for Manhattan to operate at its high capacity and its contribution cannot be underestimated. The pressures of this city affect the residents of New York and causes NYC Pastors to face challenges that are unique to this city.

As a pastor you have to be well read, articulate, have high organizational skills and be a successful fundraiser. One Pastor asked me to name one successful church in the city were the pastor was not an exceptional fund­raiser. I thought about it and I could not find one. However, most seminaries do not train on the subject. To say the least New York City ministry is extremely tough on a pastor and his home life.

Concerts of Prayer is screaming from its pulpit to “Slow down pastor and recognize that there are others just like you. Be smart pastor and recognize that your situation is not out of ordinary and most other Senior Elders are facing or have faced the struggles you currently experience.” I remember one busy pastor said to me at the last Pastors' Prayer Summit, “I am too busy to stay,” but then after being there for 6 hours regretted not blocking out the time. He said, "I need this for my soul." There is no­thing like spending 48 hours praying and fellowshipping with leaders that go through the exact same struggles that you face. There is no denominational agenda being presented and you always have an option to hear a presenter or plug in to our 24hr worship and prayer room facilitated by top worship and prayer leaders from chur­ches through-out the tri-state area.

Several pastors fall for Satan's old trick which tells us that our ministry is so uni­que and it will collapse if we do not stay on the job January 19th through the 21st. The other trick is to tell you that you are so awesome that you do not need to go unless you are the presenter. Lastly, some may feel like I’ve been there and done that. However, if a member of their church practiced that same philosophy on church attendance saying, "Pastor I do not need the fellowship anymore I will just watch you or another talented speaker via online or television,” they would sing a different tune. Pastors' we need to slow down annually not with outer town people but with each other to uniquely speak and pray for the city in which we labor in.

We have a blend of presenters but this is not a presentation driven conference, to qualify the presenter must be an example of 1 Timothy 3, “Qualifications of Overseers: This is a faithful saying: If a man desires the position of a bishop,[a] he desires a good work. A bi­shop then must be blameless, the husband of one wife, temperate, sober-min­ded, of good behavior, hospitable, able to teach; not given to wine, not violent, not greedy for money,[b] but gentle, not quarrelsome, not co­ve­tous; one who rules his own house well, having his children in submission with all reverence (for if a man does not know how to rule his own house, how will he take care of the church of God?); not a novice, lest be­ing puffed up with pride he fall into the same condemnation as the devil. Moreover he must have a good testimony among those who are outside, lest he fall into reproach and the snare of the devil.”

Our speakers have believing kids, strong churches and are seasoned leaders with solid reputations. These men qualify as spiritual fathers who have run a good race and have an abundance to pour out to us all. However, we do not operate from a top down approach but from a fellow elder perspective.

This year, after 23 years at the same location, we are switching it up and moving to Connecticut. We look forward to exploring the new facilities and continue to create strong relationships with our peers. You will be able to enjoy the conference and meals together, as well as, some down time in the pool, gym, sauna or just meditating while looking out over the beautiful landscapes on the grounds.

So, slow down Pastor and come fellowship with your co-laborers in Greater New York.

Rev. Dimas Salaberrios is the President of Concerts of Prayer of Greater New York, 46-04 31st Avenue, 2nd Floor, Long Island City NY 11103 (718) 721-2626 x 215
