Timeless Leadership that Revives and Transforms - Part 1

by Jim Biscardi Jr.

Servant leadership is something that the world needs now more than ever. Saying it another way: What the world needs now is love – not the love that is flaunted by Hollywood or loosely thrown around in political speeches – but the love demonstrated by Jesus Christ. Instead of leadership that serves itself, we need leaders who visualize themselves as servants to those who are given to them.

Because we have the Holy Spirit within, we have the power to be the leaders Jesus showed us how to be – leaders who revive and transform our most important enterprises – and ultimately change the world.

As Christ’s leaders in our home, work, church and as ambassadors of Christ, we need to be good listeners; be faithful and build faithfulness; build up and encourage others; openly communicate with our people and involve them in planning; build unity and family; recognize and reward good conduct - not take the easy road of rewarding or not rewarding everyone; give honest feedback by speaking the truth in love (Ephesians 4:15); rate performance with understanding; and develop a servant’s “heart”in others (John 13:14-17).

These behaviors flow from us naturally if we put a priority on preferring others over ourselves (Matthew 23:11); being heart-to-heart – taking time to know others and to be known by them (John 10:14); taking action with pure motives and wisdom (James 3:17); and drawing people rather than “push- ing” [manipulating] them (Matthew 11:28).

Here are a few of the effects that this kind of loving leadership would have on our homes and  churches just through good listening, being faithful and building faithfulness, building up and encouraging our people. Visualize the church and home to be like this:

Good Listening in the Church

Pastor has an open door policy. Prayer requests are solicited using leaflets in the pews or through an email prayer chain. Suggestions are requested for Sunday school, children’s church, Wednesday/Sunday services, and other outreaches. Speakers are often requested by church members. Pastor and other leaders conduct meetings as facilitators and coaches to determine the mind of the Spirit through their people, rather than dictating answers or manipulating to get preconceived results. The board of trustees (or overseeing body of the church) and the pastor work cooperatively with each other and are open to one another’s ideas and opinions.

Good Listening in the Home

Parents foster creativity in children by stimulating imagination with games, stories, videos, music, toys, drawing, etc. Parents ask children what they would do in various situations. If the parent decides a different course of action, the child is told why. Parents look for opportunities to do things suggested by the child. Husbands and wives are open to each other’s suggestions and promptings. They see themselves as equals with different roles, each having ideas that complement the other’s.

Being Faithful and Building Faithfulness in the Church

Church leaders serve the members by lovingly coaching them through leadership, evangelism, teaching, counseling, parenting, work life, and other programs to make them better. Leaders put members’ interests and priorities before themselves. Church policies are not made for the purpose of restricting the “abuser minority” but ra­ther to benefit, enhance, and encourage the majority of church members. There is an active leader visitation program to church member homes. They are visited at home or in the hospital when sick. Also, telephone contact, fellowship programs, and home Bible studies are used by church leaders to show their care for church members. Church leaders are proactive and waste no time in correcting, disciplining or counseling those who bring false teaching, immoral or abusive behavior into the church or into its families.

Being Faithful and Building Faithfulness in the Home

Parents do not allow children to go undisciplined for improper behavior. They are careful not to favor one child over another in this area. Parents are proactive in protecting their children against any corrupt, immoral or abusive behavior entering their lives from any source. Husbands and wives also protect and warn one another in each of these areas. Parents recognize real accomplishments and reward their children equally. Husbands and wives put each other’s interests and priorities ahead of their own. They sacrifice for one another. Husbands provide financial security and affection to their wives. Wives provide admiration and good home-keeping for their husbands.

Building Up and Encouraging in the Church

The overall church leadership model is one of servant-leader not micro mana- ger or fear manager. Trust - between leaders and church members - inspires active participation by the majority. Arm-twisting is not needed. Leaders lovingly take members through stages (direction, coaching, encourage- ment) leading to empowering them for making decisions, actively participating in church activities, and for representing the church. Deacons (or other “officials”) are truly elected from among the current church members by other members with little or no “filtering out” by the current Board or the Pastor.

Building Up and Encouraging in the Home

Parents correct their children in a way that says “I love you and want you to know right from wrong.” Parents are under control when discipline is given so that children do not fear their parents, even though they know they will be punished for improper behavior. Parents don’t jump to conclusions about their children’s behavior but listen to them and use every opportunity to teach them. Husbands and wives trust one another, and always presume the best about each other. After taking children through “direction,” “coaching” and “encouraging” in a particular area, parents trust their children and give them independence in that area. Husbands and wives encourage one another to pursue their “dreams” for improving themselves (e.g. education, job, write a book, etc.). They sacrifice themselves to let this happen.

What a wonderful world that would be!!  Let’s make it a reality TODAY!
