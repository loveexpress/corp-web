Solemn Assembly, Saturday, October 11th;

Feast of Tabernacles, A Window to Seek God for America.

By Michael Obi

In every generation when God is about to do significant things He begins to use signs to confirm. The birth of Christ was confirmed with the sign of a star seen in the east. Signs in the moon and the sun are one of the ways we know when it is a special Kairos season. The book of Joel speaks of a time of shaking in the heavens when the sun will turn black and the moon turn to blood.

October 11th we are coming together during the Feast of Tabernacles not through the call of man but the call of God. Leviticus 23 outlines the Feasts of the Lord. We know when the disciples gathered in the upper room it happened during the feast of Pentecost. These de­signated feast days hold great significance as windows of special urgency and favor for waiting upon the Lord.

We are coming together Saturday, October 11th to consecrate a special time of expectation. God will meet us as we seek Him with fasting and prayer especially as we begin this with the signs of the blood moon on October 8th.

The Solemn Assembly, is a time of prayer, fasting & worship where the hearts of the generations assemble to seek the face of The Lord. This will also fulfill the Ma­la­chi 4:6 call for the healing of the generations to see the hearts of the fathers turning to their children and the hearts of the children turning to their fathers. And we believe that God will break the curse of fatherlessness over the nation. It is a time of worship and praise, repentance and prayer seeking God’s face on behalf of America, our Churches and our individual homes. To register  http://bit.ly/1pTc7bd for more details go to aslpn.org.

“Calling the Church & Our Nation Back to Jesus Christ” is this year’s theme, based on Joel 2:12-32. Joel “12 Therefore also now, saith the Lord, turn ye even to me with all your heart, and with fasting, and with weeping, and with mourning:13 And rend your heart, and not your garments, and turn unto the Lord your God: for He is gracious and merciful, slow to anger, and of great kindness, and repenteth him of the evil. 14 Who knoweth if He will return and repent, and leave a blessing behind Him; even a meat offering and a drink offering unto the Lord your God? 15 Blow the trumpet in Zion, sanctify a fast, call a solemn assembly.”

In desperate times as this, the sound of the pro­phet Joel reminds us it is time to seek the face of The Lord for the church and our nation. The African Strategic Lea­dership Prayer Network invites you to join us in a national Solemn Assembly on the Feast of Tabernacles, Saturday, October 11th, 10am to 3pm at Natio­nal Church of God, 6700 Bock Road in Fort Washington Maryland.
