New Brown Memorial Baptist Church

By Rev. A. R. Jamal

located in Red Hook, Brooklyn survived Super Storm Sandy

In the aftermath of this Storm of the century, Rev. A. R. Jamal and his congregation was a beacon in the community - providing spiritual guidance, feeding many and distributing clothes and furniture that were donated by stores and friends of the church.

It has been over ninne months since the impact of this hurricane and Red Hook community is still struggling to get back on its feet. The entire community was flood­ed, some parts were well un­der more than 4 feet of water. Lives were lost. Electrical and telephone lines were down for extended periods - no heat or hot water. Some of New Brown’s congregation and other community members had no homes. Their cars and a host of other possessions were gone. Businesses were destroyed and probably will never be rebuilt. A few are struggling to stay open.

The FEMA assistance and other government funds that were promised to  many residents never materialized; but despite a flooded basement, New Brown Memorial Baptist Church endured and celebrated with a community coming back together.

Three weeks after this devastating hurricane ripped apart the Red Hook Community, New Brown Memorial Baptist Church, with the help of their dear friend James McBride, hosted a Thanksgiving Dinner for the entire Red Hook Community. This was a touching expereince as we prayed and ate as one - fostering the feeling of hope and togetherness.

Drawn together by the love God and the strength of this community, Rev. Jamal is rebuilding. New Browns slogan for 2013 “Growth” is not chosen by chance. Rev. Jamal’s strong determination to stand firm in his fatih and to continuously grow in God’s love.

Pastor Jamal believes that every moment is a time to give God thanks regardless of the situation or condition. - Psalm 136:1,2,3 “Give thanks to the Lord, for He is good; His love endures forever. Give thanks to the God of gods. His love endures forever. Give thanks to the Lord of lords: His love endures forever.”

Rev. A. R. Jamal, Pastor, New Brown Memorial Baptist Church, 609 Clinton Street, Brooklyn, NY 11231. Tel:(718) 624-4780
