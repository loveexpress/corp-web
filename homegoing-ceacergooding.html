Bishop Ceacer Gooding

The life of Bishop Cea­cer Gooding was cele­brated on April 5th and 6th 2014 at Miracle Revival Temple in the Bronx NY.

After being incapacita­ted over the last 15 years in James J. Peters V.A. Medical Center in the Bronx NY, he transitioned on Wednesday April 2, 2014.

Bishop Gooding was born Sept. 18, 1931, and The Lord blessed him with 82 years of longevity. He was married to Evangelist Jose­phine Gooding for 61 years. (11/29/1952)

On Saturday April 5th 2014, was the memorial wake celebration. Bishop's daughters Min. Chanel Gows, and Evangelist Joann Gooding Canion facilitated the night, which featured a mini concert from the award winning gospel artist "The Ecstatistics" (of which Supt Jay Gooding is a former member). The night also included remarks of many including Bishop Robert Girtman.

On Sunday April 6th at 12pm was the local church service which also included the membership of Fellowship Tabernacle COGIC, along with Miracle Revival Temple, "One church, Two locations" pastored by Bi­shop Gooding's youngest child, Superintendent Jay Gooding Sr. Officiant was Rev. Sidney Hargrave, and reflections were made by many members and friends.  The sermon was delivered by Pastor Gilbert White (God in Action COGIC).

The final homegoing ce­lebration service was held at 6pm. It was attended by hundreds, including many pastors, clergy and friends from the metro area. An overflow crowd viewed the service by a live feed telecast in the upper sanctuary.

The service was officia­ted by Supt. Gary Rhett Jr (Evergreen COGIC), those on the program with eulogistic expressions included: Bi­shop James Gaylord, Bishop David Wallace, Bishop Mat­thew Johnson, Supt John Myers, Supt. Robert Madison, NYPD Chief Thomas Purtell, State supervisor Marcelle Harris, and Supt. Robert Washington.

Musical selections were given by Rev Roger Hambrick (Green Pastures Baptist), and Elder David Wright and NY Fellowship choir.  

Family tributes were shared by son Ceacer Gooding Jr., daughter Evang. Jo­Ann Gooding Canion, and selections from daughter Min Chanel Gows, and eldest granddaughter LaTiesha Gooding. Granddaughter Erika Canion Coy read the condolences and obituary.

The service also inclu­ded a U.S. Armed Forces sa­lute and presentation of the American flag to the widow, Evang. Josephine Gooding.

The eulogy was delivered by his son Supt. Jay Gooding Sr. titled  "I understand it now", which reflec­ted on a father/son relation- ship.

Bishop Gooding is survived by his wife Evang. Jo­sephine Gooding, 4 children: JoAnn Gooding Canion (Bishop Rodney), Ceacer Jr (Leonia), Chanel Gows (Greg), Supt Jay Gooding Sr (Nicole). One brother Calvin Gooding, 13 grandchildren, and 19 great grandchildren.

Bishop Gooding's earth­ly remains were laid to rest on Monday April 7, at Calverton National Veterans Cemetery in Calverton N.Y.

On behalf of Evange­list Josephine Gooding, Supt. Jay Gooding Sr., and the entire family of Bishop Ceacer Gooding. They wish to express their love and gratitude to all who were in attendance, sent condolences, and all acts of love shown during their time of bereavement.
