2015 New York Empowerment Summit

Labor Day Weekend Sept. 5th & 6th
@ Madison Square Garden

Day of Praise, Night of Worship and A Night of Empowerment.

Labor Day Weekend Sept. 5th & 6th The Theater at Madison Square Garden with Shirley Caesar, Marvin Sapp, Cece Wi­nans, Tye Tribbett, Dr. Jasmin Sculark, Dorinda Clark-Cole, Martha Munizzi, Ta­sha Cobbs, Wess Morgan, Marvia Providence, Uche Agu, Kevin Downswell, Travis Greene and more.

Come experience the 2-day NY Empowerment Summit. An Inspirational journey of a lifetime. Tickets are now on sale to the public.

The 2015 NY Empowerment Summit will bring toge­ther national and international speakers, music artists and over 10,000 fans for a histo­ric two-day event. The focus of the NY Empowerment Sum­mit is “To promote spiritual unity and increase God’s love within our communities, to strengthen our spiritual leaders and encourage the body of Christ with words of Empowerment.” said Visionary Pastor Joseph Wil­liams of the Glory to God Christian Center.

"I am looking forward to participating in the 2015 New York Empowerment Summit in NYC's Theater at Madison Square Garden Sept. 5th. I look forward to seeing you." said CeCe Wi­nans

2-day event details:Gos­pel Concert filled with workshops, praise and much more will take place at 2pm on Saturday, Sept. 5th and Sunday, Sept. 6th at the Theater at Madison Square Garden.

Workshop details: Workshops will take place Saturday, Sept. 5th starting at 9am. Workshop topics will include leadership, entrepreneurship, family matters and real estate.

“Our goal is to build participating guests spiritual practice and spur community prosperity, to empower our youths and strengthen the family bond”, stated Co-Pastor Funmi Williams. This event is already coined the biggest Empowering and Motivatio­nal event of the summer and fall.

Tickets for the NY Empowerment Summit will be available at the Madison Square Garden Box Office, All Ticketmaster Outlets, Ticketmaster charge by phone at 1-866-858-0008 & NewYork Empowermentsum­mit.com.
