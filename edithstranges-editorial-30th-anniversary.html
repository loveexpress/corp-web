Editorial: 30th Anniversary of The Love Express

Edith Stranges

Reprinted from April 1986, to help clarify and redefine the objective and purpo­ses of The Love Express.

“Walking in the Vision”

I have always believed that one of the greatest strengths of The Love Express is that it’s more than just a collection of interesting, biblically sounding articles, but rather it is a very important factor in God’s overall vision of unity and revival for New York City.

I am sure that many pastors, laymen, businessman are still skeptical as to whe­ther The Love Express is for the whole Body of Christ, or just to promote one ministry. It is my desire in this brief article to present the vision and purpose of The Love Express, that God has not only stirred in my heart; but in the hearts and minds of believers and pastors throughout this metropolitan area.

As a wife of a pastor, a former preacher’s kid and one who has lived and worked in Manhattan; I can say that I am committed to helping churches, ministries and businesses of all sizes be more effective in reaching the people of New York City. Many who have already advertised in The Love Express are saying, “This is exactly what we have needed to streng­then our outreach to our community and this city.”

This pleases me, because the goal of The Love Express is  to assist believers in our joint calling of evangelism in New York City. For those of you that have come to know me, either personally or through The Love Express know that the heart of my efforts is the reconciliation of the Lord’s church, the body of Christ; and that The Love Express is not building a separate new ministry or any ministry already in existence.

The Love Express is a network of communication between the body of Christ, “all of God’s people working together, the world might know that thou hast sent us. (John 17:21).

In the last 8 months since the miracle of The Love Express was born in New York City in 1985, there have been 10 folks who have been born again as a direct result of the printed word of God in The Love Express. Nearly 100 of you have written in to have The Love Express sent to a friend.

Working in the Love Express has taught me much about patience, love, forgiveness, and humbleness. I ne­ver consider myself a leader, a frontman, a prophetess, yet those are the names that people use when they speak of me. I consider myself a servant of the Lord.

I don’t speak for any denomination, fellowship or organization, but for the thou- sands of Christians in the Tri-state area.

This however, is how The Love Express runs. I have completely trusted the Lord for these last 30 years and you know it takes waiting on God, and patience, but it’s worth it all to know this is God’s newspaper. This paper is a reflection of what it will be like in Heaven.

Some have said to me “What are your demographics? What market are you reaching?” “It’s ok to have healthy competition.” My answer is, “I have never known competition to be healthy. It creates bad feelings, envy, jealousy and confusion for the Body of Christ, and that is not healthy.” “What market are you reaching?” I answer, “God’s marketplace where He said, “The harvest is ripe,” where He sees people of all color, nations and cultures.” “What are your dempgraphics?”

We are The Love Express, One Body, One Lord, One Spirit, One God; One way to heaven through Jesus Christ. Jesus said, “that they may be one;” all un­der one umbrella, one pa­per to the tri-state area.

A few years ago there were Love Expresses in New Jersey, Long Island and in NYC. At that time I had the responsibility for NYC. Some pastors, who were from NJ and LI, desired to be in one paper. Because of the work volume and finances, the other Love Expresses stop­ped printing. Then after much prayer and counseling, what came forth is the paper you are now reading.

The Love Express is not a Baptist, Methodist, Lu­­theran, Nazarene, Pentecostal, AME, COGIC, Church of God, Charisma­tic, Christian Center or any other denominational pa­per. Nor is it a Black thing or White thing. It is a Christian Newspaper accessible to all in the Body of Christ.

Edith M. Stranges
