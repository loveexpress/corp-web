Editorial:

Edith Stranges

30 years; it is wonderful that God’s mercy and grace has been on this paper.

My heart is the heart of the Father and he has given to us the ministry of reconciliation and I am so grateful that the Movement Day has put together the “Movement Declaration” which is an opportunity for us as the Body of Christ to mutually declare our commitment to each others and to our respective ci­ties. Read the declaration in the article “Movement Day 2015.”

You are precious to me, my readers, but you know as well as I know there’s got to be more: more love, more integrity, more power, more commitment. Integrity is the soundness of moral principle and character. It has to do with honesty and uprightness.

In these days, integrity is a rare commodity. In business or ministry, true soundness of moral principles is hard to find.

In my own life, I find that I’m tested on the principle of integrity. Most of my dealings are with pastors. I would say 99% are good, honest, and truthful men of God. I thank God for them and continue to uphold them in prayer. Yet the 1% like to challenge us. They have their own selfish agendas without any regard for the whole Body of Christ. They deliberately attempt to disrupt the move of God because of jealousy and carnality. I know this is the hour to be mature, responsible, resisting the devil, and to love and respect one another in the Body of Christ, love God and be obedient to His Word. If you fall or yield to temptation, quickly repent, fast and pray, live holy unto the Lord, and don’t just talk the words; live it, breathe it.

I want to say to the pastors, business people who walk through the doors of The Love Express, or who are in communication with us by phone, “I’m sorry if I or the staff of The Love Express didn’t show you courtesy, respect or love. I repent as the Editor, and I take full responsibility. What I say in the editorial is also for the staff and myself. We must live the life that we teach.

Again I thank all supporters, advertisers, pastors of The Love Express. May God continue to bless you as you have been a blessing to this present ministry.

The Love Express is not a Baptist, Methodist, Lu­­theran, Nazarene, Pentecostal, AME, COGIC, Church of God, Charisma­tic, Christian Center or any other denominational pa­per. Nor is it a Black thing or White thing. It is a Christian Newspaper accessible to all in the Body of Christ.

Edith M. Stranges, The Love Express
