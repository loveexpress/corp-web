Grants for NYCHA Residents

By Donald Reid, MBA

Looking for ways to avoid the last-minute rush in preparing your taxes? Then here are the top Six Golden Rules to help you navigate this last minute journey:

1. Organize Your Tax Records. Tax preparation time can be significantly reduced if you develop a system for organizing your records and receipts. Start with the income, deduction or tax credit items that were on last year’s return.

2. Don’t Procrastinate. Resist the temptation to put off your taxes until the last minute. Your haste to meet the filing deadline may cause you to overlook potential sources of tax savings and will likely increase your risk of making an error.

3. Have Your Refund Deposited Directly to Your Bank Account. Another way to speed up your refund and reduce the chance of theft is to have the amount deposited directly to your bank account.

4. Don’t Panic If You Can’t Pay. If you can’t immediately pay the taxes you owe, consider some stress-reducing alternatives. You can apply for an IRS installment agreement, suggesting your own monthly payment amount and due date, and getting a reduced late payment penalty rate.

5. You also have various options. You can charge your balance due on a credit card, either as part of an electronic return or send a check in the mail.

6. Request an Extension of Time to File. If the clock runs out, you can get an automatic extension of time to file. Note, an extension of time to file does not give you an extension of time to pay.
