Youth 2006

Called to Work

With the economic turmoil in recent months, organizers of this year's annual Called To Work Conference are focusing on helping people to "overcome detours at work."

The 14th Annual workplace Christianity conference will be held on Friday evening, March 27, 7:30pm and on Saturday, March 28 8am to 4:15pm at Rutgers Community Christian Church, 71 Cedar Grove Lane. The conference is open to high school and college students, and adults of all ages.

On Friday, March 27 at 7:30 pm, the conference will kick off with prayer, praise and worship, and a keynote address by guest speaker Doug Hunter, President of the Fellowship of Companies for Christ International in Atlanta. Hunter is a seasoned Christian businessman and ministry leader who has ministered to working people around the world. Attendees are encouraged to bring family, friends, and co-workers to this inspirational session, after what may have been a long and stressful week.

On Saturday, March 28 at 8am, the conference will begin with registration, continental breakfast, and resource center. Doug Hunter will give the morning keynote address on Overcoming Detours @ Work, followed by a series of highly informative and relevant workshops on Overcoming Job/Career Detours by Fred Eng, Overcoming Financial Detours by Michael DiMaio from New Focus - NJ and Kingdom Advisors, and Overcoming Relationship Detours by Dereth Ellen Van Etten and Bernice S. LaMotta from the Pastoral Counseling Center. After lunch, there will be additional workshops, resource center time, and a panel discussion on How to OvercomeDetours @ Work. The conference will end at 4:15 pm. Early-bird registration for the all-day Saturday conference is $39 for adults and $25 for high school and college students, which in­cludes continental breakfast and lunch on-site.

Complete information, promotional tools, mail and online registration are available at www.CalledToWork.org. Interested parties may also call Northeast Chris­tians At Work, co-sponsor of the event, toll-free at 1-800-JOY-9075.

Founded in 2000, Northeast Christians At Work is an all-volunteer, 501(c)3 not-for-profit ministry that joyfully makes, motivates and matures Christians in workplaces across New England, NY, NJ, and Pennsylvania.
