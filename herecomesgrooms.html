Here Comes the Grooms (with Their Wives) - And They’re All Pentecostal Ministers

Freeport-Based Diocese Takes a Bold Stand for Traditional
Biblically-Defined Marriage in a Landmark Valentine’s Weekend Exchange of Marriage Vows - by the Hundreds

Last June, when the U.S. Supreme Court released its opinion finding Section 3 of the Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA) to be unconstitutional, a fire­storm was ignited in the American dialogue, inflaming a debate that seems to transcend idle Monday morning water-cooler chat.

Bishop Frank Otha White, who presides over the Freeport-based Third Ecclesiastical Jurisdiction of Eastern New York, Church of God in Christ, firmly upholds the opinion of its parent body whose Memphis Headquarters has proclaimed “the va­lue and worth of every human being regardless of sexual orientation. But, we passionately and unapologetically, defend the right of faith communities to maintain the integrity of their message, mission and identity.”

By no stretch is this position an unpopular stance in evangelical circles. However, the volume is being cranked up plenty of notches higher as this flood of pastors whose congregations comprise the jurisdiction - from Niagara Falls to Southhampton - will escort their wives to the main altar at Zion Cathedral on Saturday, February 15, 2014 at 11am to avow their dissonance with their legislature’s input in the matter and, in the same breath, reaffirm God’s say in the matter.

All things being equal, “this is what we believe” says Bishop Frank Anthone White, pastor of the 2,000 member cathedral which will host this historic ceremony. White, who has been hailed nationally as a exceptional expositor goes on to assert “the union between one man and one woman is strictly defined, sacred, ordained by God and cannot be undone by any public referendum of the people.”

In practice, this movement, spearheaded by the example of more than 50 pastors, will embody the representation of all of their married congregations. Together, the symbolic strength of these pastors reaffirming “I do” will echo the convictions of a highly anticipated onslaught of support by some 500 couples - “possibly even more” adds Walter C. Willie, the Executive Secretary of the Diocese.

“Whenever we follow Godly commands, we’ll get Godly results” says the senior White who will co-officiate the ceremony. “Just look around. Our society cannot continue  to sustain generations of behavior which drift further and further into an unprincipled whirlwind where just about anything goes.”

This united and unpre­cedented show of force is already sending shockwaves across the Tri-state area stirring up a rally of support among like-minded citizens whose response can be summed up in the attitude of newlywed Marlyn Days Whe­thers of Freeport who insists “it’s about time somebody did something! Our pastors (and their wives) are going to stand up and stand out.”

Bishop White, who recently celebrated 53 years of marriage does not tread lightly upon the public argument. Though his charismatic style and warm-hearted approach to reaching out is embraced with overwhelming popularity, he advises, “there must be a foundation.” He drives the point home plainly, “Simply put, we believe marriage is what God says it is.”

The Third Ecclesiastical Jurisdiction Eastern NY is governed by the Church of God in Christ, the lar­gest Pentecostal organization in the United States. It’s worldwide membership is estimated to be between six and eight million members with more than 15,000 congregations throughout the world.
