Best-Selling Author Brian McLean

by Brian McLaren

Comes to the Latino Pastoral Action Center in the Bronx, one stop on his 11th U.S. City 2008 Everything Must Change Tour this May.

One of Time Magazine’s 25 most influential Evangelicals in America, described as a "paradigm shifter" capable of bringing liberals and conservatives together and as board chair of Sojourners, working closely with Jim Wallis, Tony Campolo, Shane Claiborne, and other Christian activists and progressive voices, comes to the Bronx, NYC, to share on his newest book, "Everything Must Change: Jesus, Global Crises, and a Revolution of Hope."

Bestselling author Brian McLaren and Deep shift.org will stop in New York City, the Bronx, May 2-3, 2008 as part of their 11-city tour from February to May, 2008. The book seeks to address what McLaren calls “two questions that have shaped my life”: What are our world’s top global crises? What does the message of Jesus say to those crises? The book summarizes and synthesizes recent literature on global crises and presents a systemic approach, identifying their interlocking crises relating to the planet, poverty, and peace-making. At their center is the fourth crisis, which, McLaren, asserts, is a failure of our world’s religions to provide a message capable of motivating people to address the first three crises. He believes that needed message is actually present in the original message of Jesus, but is too often marginalized even among the most orthodox of Christians.

The second half of the book resituates Jesus in his original political and social context, from which a fresh vision of Jesus emerges, one that shows striking relevance to contemporary crises in our globalizing, ecologically-stressed, polarizing, and war-torn world. Of special interest to biblically knowledgeable readers will be McLaren’s fresh takes on Jesus’ miracles, parables, and central teachings regarding the kingdom of God.

“This tour represents a chance to bring this important message to key cities around the country,” McLaren explains. “It will be a highly interactive, multi-media, participatory experience of teaching, visual art, prayer, music, conversation, reflection, and more. A wide range of denominations are participating, both Catholic and Protestant, Mainline and Evangelical, along with plenty of ‘spiritual but not religious’ people too.”

Although there are national hosts and sponsors which include the Sierra Club, Mars Hill Graduate School, Faith at Work, Sojourners, and Emergent Village. There is still a need for local sponsors to join the Latino Pastoral Action Center and the Latino Leadership Circle bring this event to fruition with its unique NY flavor.

Other cities the tour will take place in are Charlotte, Boise, Dallas, Tampa, DC, San Diego, Chicago, Seattle, Kansas City, New York City, & Northern Indiana. The tour will arrive in NY on May 2 through May 3, 2008 and will be held in the Bronx at the Latino Pastoral Action Center. The local emergent cohort co-sponsoring the event is the Latino Leadership Circle (http://latinoleadership circle.typepad.com).

“The members of the Latino Leadership Circle feel that this promises to be one of the most prophetic-educational seminars of its kind” said Rev. David Ramos, founder of the group. “We are especially hoping that all those who are asking the same questions on what it means to be the church in a world struggling to make meaning of the Jesus we preach while there are major calamities in the world to solve will come out to the event!,” said Rev. Dr. Raymond Rivera, Founder and President of the Latino Pastoral Action Center. Rev. Dr. Liz Rios, the tour coordinator for the NYC site boils it down to this, “there is a new breed of people, young and old, with varied religious and political backgrounds, who are calling for change, who are no longer afraid to ask the questions of the institutional church and who know without a shadow of a doubt that the Jesus we serve would most definitely be involved in a revolution of hope, this new breed needs to be at LPAC May 2-3.”

Registration information can be found at www. deepshift.org or www.brian mclaren.net.
