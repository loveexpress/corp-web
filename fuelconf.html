Fuel - The Movement 2013

By Jacob Burgei

I’m a lover of God’s Word and love to study it, but one thing that brings life to it is when you see it acted out with a response.

We have many hearers of the Word but few doers. You see there is a big difference between a reaction and a response. Many Times in my life I’ve reacted to the things of God but never responded to them. We have all been there where we hear the preaching of God’s Word, we are moved emotionally, and our spirits are leaping inside us. Yet we walk out the doors of our churches with no response to the message that just rocked our world.

I have three children, and many times I see this with them, where they react to the present connection, but their response five minutes later says something different. They reacted at the time because they knew Daddy wasn’t playing any games, but when they got out of my presence their response had changed. How many times do we do that with God and His Word?

As we embark on our third year of the Fuel Movement, I have many people ask me this question: “How did you get this many different denominations, different cultures and different chur­ches involved in this movement?” The answer is simple —we responded to Psalms 133:1-3 (MSG): “How wonderful, how beautiful when brothers and sisters get along…Yes, that’s where God commands the blessing, ordains eternal life.” That’s right, we’re always talking about UNITY, because it starts with the youth pastors and leaders unifying first before we can see our young people becoming united. I remember more than three years ago meeting with nine original Fuel Executive Team members and asking them, “What if God could use us to launch a grassroots movement in the Greater NYC area that would equip and train leaders and students to stay fueled up with Jesus all year long?” It sounds like a simple question, but because the nine responded, we have simply seen something take place in the Northeast with young people and ministries uniting that is like no other.

Just a few weeks ago, our Fuel Leadership Training saw more than 200 youth pastors and leaders in attendance and the atmosphere was electrifying!  Four different states were represented and almost every borough in NYC. You see, it’s more than just a conference, it’s a movement! We are working together with other great initiatives such as GBIMC, Spanish Eastern District AG Youth, Latino Leadership Circle, Rapfest, Battlecry and more.
