Editorial: Loving One!

Editor & Publisher: Edith Stranges

In Luke 19:11-28, Jesus spoke a parable unto His disciples concerning the advantages of spiritual investments, accountability, opportunity for service, neglect of duty, and the final outcome of that parable, privi- leges withdrawn. Let us look at these points of interest together and seek the wisdom of God, concerning truths contained in this parable that relate to us as believers to­day.

Jesus Christ, the Captain ofour salvation, has commissioned all of us to go into our world (New York City) and preach the Gospel, with signs following. Jesus has given unto us the ministry of reconciliation. He has placed within our hands talents, gifts and opportunities of service. Are you taking advantage of each of these special abilities that God has given to you?

Today we need for God to raise up more believers who will honestly take a good look at the spiritual condition of the church, nation, the world, and the city we live in. Each block, community and neighborhood should be saturated with believers, each of us taking the opportunity of service to make a statement to this city that our God reigns.

Let us not be among those, who make excuses for their neglect of duty as we see the influence of drugs, aids, suicide and the increase of satanic worship reaching deeply into many lives; our children, family and friends. We must not slack off and be among those whose spiritual privileges are being withdrawn. As you can plainly see, this is not time for quitting.

And while we are on the subject of quitting, I thank the Lord for the many churches and ministries that have not quit on Unity or Reconciliation. You may ask, Why should I care about Unity? Because it’s close to the heart of Jesus. Division tears us apart. Division is actually a lie.

We are at the brink of a great move of God. Let us not be hinder in anyway by our own flesh dictating to us what is or not of God.

Jonathan Edwards, reporting of the great awakening in the 18th Century, asked a question concerning the revival, Was this really of God? He answered with sure evidence that the revival was truly from God. Quoting from (William De Arteaga, Quickening The Spirit).

Another area of grave concern, one which cannot be ignore is Racism. John Perkins writes “When the time comes that the world can look at the church and see christians of all nationalities and skin color working together, worshipping together and loving one another, a revival of unbelievable proportion will explode all across the land.”

The world is watching New York City, for whatever happens in New York City influences the world in the areas of fashion, theater, the arts, finances, world affairs, so it is only natural that out of this city God will flow a mighty move of His Spirit that will affect and change the course of many lives.

I’m excited about what God is doing this summer, street meetings, rallies, concerts, prayer meetings and seminars. Be Blessed.

Remember, September begins our 30th year of publication.

Edith M. Stranges
