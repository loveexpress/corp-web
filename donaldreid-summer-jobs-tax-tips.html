Summer Jobs Tax Tips!

By Donald Reid, MBA

There are opportunities galore to give a job or get a job with tax benefits to all parties concerned and here is how it works:

Tip # 1

Job hunting expenses are deductible. If you’re out of work, or even if you’re still employed but looking for a new job, all of your job hunting expenses are deductible as miscellaneous itemized deductions.

Such expenses would include resumes, phone calls, postage, travel costs and any other expenses related to your attempt to get a new job.

Creativity here can be rewarding. For example, if you take a friend to lunch in an attempt to use him as a reference or referral, you can use the cost of the lunch as a job-hunting expense.

Tip # 2

For the self employed there are some benefits as well, as follows: Pens, paper, a calculator, special tools, a computer and even a briefcase used in your business are deductible.

If your job requires you to travel, a business suitcase would also be deductible. The key here is to relate the item to your business.

Tax Tip # 3

Rather than buy your investment newspapers and magazines at the newsstand, subscribe to them and use your check as the receipt.

If you use your computer for investment purposes (more than just tracking a few stocks), or subscribe to an Internet service for investment purposes, those expenses also become deductible.
