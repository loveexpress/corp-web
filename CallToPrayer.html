A Call To Prayer

Thousands Gathering in Times Square
to Pray for our Nation.

Thousands of people representing over 200 churches of all denominations from throughout the New York tri-state area are expected to gather in Times Square on Sept. 7th for Prayer in the Square. The one-hour event, to be held from 3pm to 4pm on Broadway and 44th Street, drew 10,000 people in 2007.

The objective is to pray positively and intelligently for our nation. Concern about the economy, the welfare of our troops abroad, cultural and racial inclusiveness, government leaders, and the presidential election will be among the various issues prayed for. They will also be praying for families, children growing up without fathers in the home, and the rising rate of high school drop-outs and youth suicides.

"We believe these are critical times for our nation. There is a growing uncertainty about the future and many are losing hope," states Carter Conlon, Senior Pastor, Times Square Church. "We are simply gathering as the church of Jesus Christ, in humility and unity, saying 'Lord, our nation needs you to come down and help us again."

A 180-voice gospel choir will sing between intervals of prayer. Twelve individuals, including church leaders, a single mom, a college student, a war veteran and a police officer, will pray for three minutes each. The event will be broadcast live on STAR 99.1 FM. Churches around the world, including Holland, South Africa, Ireland, Zambia, and Guatemala, will be joining to pray via a live webcast on the event website at www.nycprayer.org.

The event is open to the public and to churches of all denominations. Mayor Bloomberg is expected to issue a Mayoral Proclamation declaring Sept. 7th Prayer in the Square Day.

"I am going to Prayer in the Square because there is a certain power in gathering together to pray," explains Mac Pier, President of Concerts of Prayer of Greater New York. "It creates a kind of hope that God is ready to do something very powerful in our midst because we've called him to do something together."

"Every New Year's Eve hundreds of thousands gather in Times Square, looking skyward to a descending glass ball and hoping for a better day ahead. So often that momentary euphoria has left countless empty and disappointed," adds Rev. Conlon. "We want to come together for Prayer in the Square in this very same place to put our hope in a God who never disappoints, for the future, safety and well-being of our nation."

Times Square Church is an interdenominational, multinational congregation, located in the heart of Times Square at 51st Street, between Broadway and Eighth Avenue, founded by the Rev. David Wilkerson, renowned author of the best-selling book The Cross and the Switch­blade.
