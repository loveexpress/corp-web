Editorial:

Edith Stranges

God’s anointed Word is full of blessings for us! The word “bless, blessed or blessing” is used 566 times in the Bible, so you know God’s desire is to bless us. Blessed are the merciful,  Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, Blessed are those who are peacemakers, Blessed are those who are persecuted for righteousness sake.

In these rocky times we are living in, we need to stand on the Word of God, for our strength, comfort and hope.

To be a peacemaker, you are often called a wimp for your stand. Many years ago, before I knew the Word, I would fight and argue for what I thought was right; it didn’t matter who was offended as long as I got my point across. Thank God for Jesus who showed me the right way to handle different situations and to approach every argument with His Word.

Learn to be Blessed; be a peacemaker; you may say “that is so difficult;” yes it is without the Lord’s help.

Pastors, you may be tempted to fight with your congregation, wife, or children; remember you are blessed. Congregations, you may hold something against a brethren, you’re blessed. Learn to forgive. Resolve to practice forgiveness. Do we have a grudge against our pastor, spouse, children, pa­rents, another pastor? Forgive today. The benefit of forgiveness is a healthy spirit. Forgiveness is a miracle.

Let us, who know the moral values taught by Je­sus, learn to walk in those truths by walking in forgiveness.

If your church is not receiving The Love Express, call and ask how your church can receive the paper.
