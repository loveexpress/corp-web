True Spirit of Christmas

By Arthur Townsend

The Spirit of Christmas that of the giving of gifts is commendable. The Wise Men brought their gifts to Jesus. We should follow their example rather than that of an Ebenezer Scrooge.

The poison of self-interest was eventually forced out of Ebenezer’s life, however; he had a change of heart. The convincing, scheming, old reprobate discovered that there is more to life than the amassing and hoarding a pile of money.

Nobody in his right mind would change places with the Wise Men and their gift-giving and move over into the camp of a skinflint like Ebenezer.

However, with all of our gift-giving, freely bestowed on relatives and friends at Christmas time, we could miss the true meaning of Christmas. A little girl said, “Mother, whose birthday is December twenty fifth?” Mother replied, “That is the day when we remember the birthday of the Christ Chid.” The little girl thought deeply for a moment and then said.

“When I have a birthday, people give gifts to me. When Jesus has a birthday, why do people give gifts to each other, but nobody gives a gift to Him?” That question was a good one and hard to answer.

Nevertheless, the Lord Jesus, if He walked on earth today, would not take the doll or the train out of the hands of any little girl or boy. He would not stop the giving of gifts from husband to wife, from daughter to mother, from father to son. He would not take the love light out of the eyes of people by taking away the Spirit of Christmas giving.

The Lord Jesus is not in the joy destroying business. People who follow way-out, strange, man-made teachings and extreme beliefs would do away with the Christmas season entirely, but that is absurd.

If the Lord Jesus would come into your home at Christmas time, He would sit down with you and watch the children’s joy in the giving and receiving of gifts. He would smile and say, “It is more blessed to give than to receive.

He would be pleased to see a hard-working, loving mother receive her gifts from her family. His eyes would shine along with hers: The giving of gifts at Christmas is still wholesome and good. Only a dismal, soul—shrivelled skinflint Scrooge would cry, “HUMBUG!!” Nevertheless, it is possible to miss the true meaning of Christmas and become lost in the labyrinth of bypaths of our own making. Christmas time is not simply a season of giving and receiving of gifts; it is one time of the year when we especially remember the birthday of the King! And that is as it should be. Apart from this we cannot have a full Christmas with full joy, peace, and full happiness. It is better simplified with an illustration. To the tune of “Jingle Bells,” tiny tots sometime sing:

“J-O-Y, J-O-Y, Surely this does mean, Jesus first, yourself last and others in between.”

When we put Jesus first, others next, and self last, we have the right order. It will make every Christmas day bright, and bring about lasting joy and peace, in heart and life.

“Seek ye first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these (other) things will be added unto you. “That’s the True Spirit of Christmas
