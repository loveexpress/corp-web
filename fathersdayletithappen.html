Father’s Day, Let It Happen Everyday

By Gaile Newsome

This annual celebration seems far too seldom. For if one has been favored with the blessings of a good father in their life, there is a need to celebrate every single day. Our father, Eve­rett C. Newsome, Sr., also known as Bishop Everett C. Newsome, I, causes his children, and those that know him to celebrate consistently. 

Our father was born in  1924 in Brooklyn, NY, the fourth of five children. He experienced the death of his father due to pneumonia before the tender age of five years old. At this time due to hardship, his mother felt the need to separate her three youn­ger sons for the betterment of survival. The oldest of the three to a patriarchal aunt to be raised as her son, our father was sent to Barbados, W.I. to be raised by his matriarchal uncle, and the youn­gest son kept by mo­ther.

Dad, our father, also called “Daddy,” “Poppa”, “Poppa-Daddy,” “Pop-Pop”, “Bishop,” “By-shop,” “Pop”, has shared that there were some favorable and unfavorable acts of treatment toward him during those early years. Those acts though, especially the unfavorable ones, did not foster an attitude of hatred and arrogance, but with the gentle spirit that God gave him, they generated a determined attitude of love and hospitality. This we have noted from his younger cou­sins, the children of his uncle, who speak of Dad and interact with such love and adoration toward him that it truly warms our hearts. This love and hospitality has been constant in our home and in the church.

After a period of 17 years, Dad returned to the United States. Upon returning he met a friend who invited him to church (Pentecostal) and also introduced him to Mother Louise Atherly and her family. The Pentecostal faith proved to be a new horizon for Dad, since the Newsome’ roots were grounded in the Anglican/Episcopal faith till this day. Over the years he would reminisce of how he was nurtured by Mother Atherly in the scriptures along with wonderful meals and joyous family interaction. During the summer of 1947 Dad responded to an altar call at the Emmanuel Christian Mission Church and accepted Christ as his savior, thereafter continuing to go to church regularly. He was baptized and became a full pledged member. He also participated in a group named The National Young People’s Organization of Greater New York. This group formed an evangelistic committee to conduct services within the participating churches; he was appointed and served as vice president of this committee.

The president of this committee, a young deacon at the time, introduced Dad to the deacon’s favorite cou­sin, Sister Olga Gwendolyn Howell. Although they met in 1948, they became friendly in the early part of 1949. Dad and Mom were engaged in the late summer of 1949, and united in marriage on December 3rd, 1949.

When first married, they lived in Manhattan, but later moved to Queens in 1957, yet still continuing to go to church in Manhattan. This became tiresome and promp- ted them to look around Queens for a church, for them and the children God had blessed them with, to fellowship in. While conducting prayer meetings they were moved in 1967 to orga­nize the Saint Albans Gospel Assembly, which is still vibrant and growing today.

Throughout all this we have witnessed and daily can testify that Dad loves his children dearly; his wife (deceased June 2005), his family beyond measure and is still actively attending church weekly and in the week also. He has made certain to be with us for daily meals except when he was at work, present at recitals, little league, graduations for children and grandchildren. He could not attend our brother, Kim’s +high school graduation exercise on the 3rd Sunday in June 1981, Dad had to speak at church for “Father’s Day,” but Mom atten­ded the exercises. We’ve seen him increase in abundant wisdom perpetually.
