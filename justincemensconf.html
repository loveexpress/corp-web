Justice's Men's Conference

Saturday, Nov. 1st, 9am-3:30pm

What is JusticeNYC? It's a men's conference featuring hundreds of men and over a dozen national organizations and churches that seek to combat sexual exploitation of women - whether that is hooking up, pornography, prostitution, sex trafficking, or even abortion - through education, storytelling, and action steps. We would love the extend this message to your audience. The men's conference comes at an unprecedented time of opportunity for men to take action, given the recent White House campaign "It's On Us" which encourages men and wo­men to end sexual assault on campus, and the recent UN Campaign "He for She" last month, which calls men to uphold gender equality. It's a SUPER timely topic culturally, and extremely relevant for the Christian community to understand and MOBILIZE around this transformational event. If the body of believers doesn't step up to take action and help bring about the kingdom, who will?

Over a Dozen National Organizations and Churches Convene to Present the First Annual Justice NYC

Conference: A Transformational Men’s Event to Combat Sexual Exploitation

Authors, CEOs, over a do­zen churches and organizations, and hundreds of men gather in NYC to address the

growing problem of sexual exploitation and its effect upon women and society, at the inaugural Justice NYC Conference on Sat, Nov. 1, 2014 from 9am-3:30pm at Trinity Baptist Church. Spea­kers include Kevin Ryan, William Stru­thers, Susanne Metaxas, Kevin McCullough, Matt Bennett, Dimas Sala­berrios, and more.

Last month, the White House released their “It’s On Us” campaign, calling for men and women to help stop sexual assault on campus through greater awareness and education. Shortly thereafter, Emma Watson presen­ted a game-changing speech at the UN to launch the “He for She Campaign,” a solida­rity men’s movement to mobilize one half of the po­pu- lation in support of the other half to reach gender equality. With sexual assault and exploitation a grave danger to women and girls globally, oneupcoming conference will educate and engage men to seek solutions and combat the cultural scourge of sexual exploitation of women.

On November 1st, over a dozen nationwide organizations, churches, hundreds of men, and thoughtleaders

will convene for the Justice NYC Men’s Conference to address the growing problem of sexual narcissistic behavior from sex trafficking to prostitution, pornography to hooking up and how this feeds harmful realities such as increasing poverty, depression, and abortion. The inaugural JusticeNYC Men’s Conference will be hosted by Paul Horrocks in partnership

with the Chiaroscuro Foundation, Christian Union, Avail, Trinity Baptist Church, New Calvary Baptist Church, Bi­ble Church, Lower Manhattan Community Church, and Abundant Grace Christian Church. Justice NYC was founded by Paul Horrocks with the belief that “sexual objectification leads to dehumanization. I believe in a world where every man treats every woman the way he would want his daughter to be treated: with dignity, respect, and justice.” Justice NYC Speakers include: Ke­vin Ryan, President & CEO of Covenant House William Struthers, Author of Wired for Intimacy, Professor at Wheaton College

Paul Horrocks, Founder of JusticeNYC

JusticeNYC will be held at Trinity Baptist Church. Attendees may participate and continue the conversation using the hashtag #endsexualnarcissism. For more information and updates, visit JusticeNYC.com.

Paul Horrocks is the Founder of Justice NYC, a parachurch organization that offers events, education, and mentorship to challenge men to end sexual exploitation of women. As an advocate for cultural renewal, Paul is dedicated to exposing the harm of sexual narcissism on our society. To address this growing problem, Paul left his role as Vice President at NY Life where he previously managed a portfolio of the company’s retirement income products with over $1 Billion in annual sales. Paul has dedicated many years to ad- vancing mercy and justice efforts in NYC. He serves as the Board Chair of Hope for NY, an organization that partners with chur­ches to provide volunteers, grants and capa­ci­ty building expertise to over 40 charities in NYC and was a mentor in the Big Brother’s program for eight years.

Paul holds an undergraduate degree from the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania and a MBA from Columbia. He lives and serves in Manhattan.
