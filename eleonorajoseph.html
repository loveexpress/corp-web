Joy - The Believers Fruit (Gal 5:22)

by Dr. Eleonora Joseph

Webster’s New World Thesaurus describes the word joy as cheerfulness, delight, liveliness, good spirits, jubilation, celebration. During my childhood I always liked to laugh, be in good spirits and loved people around me. Little did I realize how true the words of Nehemiah 8:10...”The joy of the Lord is your strength” would mean to me. There is the fruit of joy (chara) and inner gladness, a deep assurance and confidence that no matter what is happening around you, you will retain that joy and inner peace. Happiness depends on what is happening around you to make you feel fulfilled or sad. We sing a little chorus that says, ‘This joy that I have the world can’t take it away’. The joy of the Lord is not the same as the joy of the world.The joy of the world is a temporary pleasure. It is nagged by some unfulfilling thing or missing ingredient. There’s no sense of completeness, assurance, confidence or satisfaction. There is, however, the awareness that something can go wrong to upset your plans. This is what the world refers to as ‘Murphy’s Law’; whatever can go wrong will go wrong. Where is the joy in that?

Yes, circumstances can change but the believer’s joy is quite different. It is given by God and has no dependence upon happenings or circumstances. It’s a joy implanted by God and anchored in the Lord. This type of joy overrides all things in life and death. Psalms 5:11 states ‘but let all those that put their trust in thee rejoice; let them ever shout for joy because thou defendest them; let them also that love thy name be joyful in thee.’ (KJV) Joy comes and spring forth through faith, believing His Word and walking in it. Thou will shew me the path of life; in His presence, there is fullness of joy; at thy right hand there are pleasures for evermore’. (Psalms 16:11 KJV). Having a relationship with God brings joy and a peace that far exceeds any human understanding. His Word and all the promises therein, bring joy: ‘For the kingdom of God is not meat and drink; but righteousness, peace and joy in the Holy Ghost’ (Romans 14:17). There’s a joy that comes from praising Him even when we don’t feel like it in the natural. However, as soon as the praises begin to flow, we experience so much joy. If your attitude is such that you can’t think of anything to praise Him for, simply sit down with pen and paper and make a list of all the things you should be thankful for that come to mind. Thy hymn writer said, ‘and it will surprise you what the Lord has done.’ The kind of joy that God wants us to have is not the ‘here one minute and gone the next’ kind of joy but it’s that deep, abiding joy and contentment that only comes from the Holy Spirit.

Seasoned Saints, understand that your circumstances may not be joyful at this time but you can depend on the Lord to minister peace and joy to you if you ask Him and His joy knows no bounds. Jesus was compassionate, loving and joyful. As believers, we must learn to cultivate a sense of humor. Sometimes we can even laugh at ourselves and our mistakes; there are so many. But we must never lose our joy no matter what the case is or the circumstances we may find ourselves under. It’s no fun being around the kind of people who are always stiff and serious and never smile. The Lord wants us to have His joy and inner peace. I’ll leave you with John 16:24, ‘Hitherto have ye asked nothing in my name; ask and ye shall receive that your joy may be full’. (KJV).
