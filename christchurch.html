Christ Church

opening of its interim sanctuary in rockaway township, new jersey

Dr. David & Pastor Marlinda Ireland

On June 28, 2009 Christ Church had announced the opening of its interim sanctuary at 140 Green Pond Road, Roc­kaway, New Jer­sey. Worship services are now being held on Sundays at 9am and 11:30am at the 1,067-seat sanctuary.

Christ Church, an independent, non-denominatio­nal Christian congregation, is expanding beyond its current location in Montclair, New Jersey to ease the de­mands of serving its growing congregation of some 6,000 members.

The church acquired the 107-acre property from Agilent Technologies in 2005 and moved its administrative offices from Verona, NJ, to the Rockaway Township site that same year. In Oc­to­ber 2007, the interim sanctuary won approval from the Rockaway Township Plann­ing Board, and construction of its interim sanctuary be­gan six months later.

The church was awar­ded its Certificate of Occu­pancy from the Rockaway Township Building Depart­ment on June 1, 2009. Construction of the interim sanctuary began August 1, 2008 and was completed at the end of May 2009. The in­terim sanctuary is expected to advance Christ Church’s history of facilitating community beautification and economic resour­ces to Mont­clair and its surrounding areas by enriching the worship, business, and cultural diversity of Morris County area parishioners and residents. The church will conti­nue serving people in and around Essex County through its five weekend ser­vices in Montclair. The two Sunday services in Rocka­way will be additional opportunities for the church to serve its growing community of worshippers that pre­sently represents some 40 different nationalities.

“We are delighted to have received the Certifi­cate of Occupancy from Rockaway Township,” said The Rev. Dr. David Ireland, senior pastor of Christ Church. “Seeing the fruition of our plans to offer worship in Morris County has been long awaited and we are excited to open our doors to our neighbors. In fact, many neighbors have been dropping by daily to inquire about when the church will begin services because they want to participate. Now, we are able to provide them with a clear answer that is sure to generate excitement in the community.”

Christ Church will offer a wide array of community-based programs and services aimed at supporting and enriching the lives of members and visitors of all ages through its new facility.

Features of the Interim  Sanctuary: The 1,067-seat sanctuary includes, state-of-the-art audio/visual/lighting systems, high-definition televisions for ease of viewing throughout the sanctuary, wood grain flooring, and decorative carpeting. Other key features of the sanctuary include:

  • 845 parking spaces available for the congregation
  • Four children’s classrooms and a separate Teen Church
  • Translation Room offering translation services from English to Spanish
  • A family-care room for parents to care for babies and children
  • Parent Pager, a compute­rized check-in system us­ing photo recognition to safeguard children when parents drop off and pick up little ones at Children’s Church.

The interim sanctuary also features stained-glass murals, theater-style seating, wood grain flooring and decorative carpeting. The worship space is also equipped with Parent Pa­ger, a computerized check-in system using photo re­cognition to safeguard children when pa­rents and guardians drop off and pick up little ones at Children’s Church; a media studio for the playback of videos; and state-of-the-art audio/visual/lighting systems to en­hance clarity of the entire worship experience and theatrical productions.7

About Christ Church

Christ Church is an in­dependent, non-denominational, church comprised of members from diverse ra­cial,cultural, and ethnic backgrounds representing approximately 40 nationalities. Christ Church was founded in June of 1986 by Reverends David & Mar­linda Ireland. Since that time, the church has en­joyed steady growth and currently has some 6,000 members. Christ Church has been recognized by the business, educatio­nal, family, political and religious sectors as an inherent va­lue to the community.
