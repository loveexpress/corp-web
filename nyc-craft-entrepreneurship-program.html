NYC Craft Entreprenurship Program

Lauren Noon came to New York to pursue a career in fashion. On a whim, she began making bracelets, and Lauren’s friends were so impressed that they encouraged her to start selling them. While Lauren had a talent for making bracelets, she wasn’t as familiar with starting a business, she participated in the NYC Craft Entrepreneurship Program in March 2014 to gain a better understanding of what it takes to start and run a business. As a result of her inspirational instructor and the tips she learned from the program, Lauren created “Chains by Lauren,” - an Etsy online shop specializing in jewelry and accessories for men and wo- men. Since the class’s conclusion, Lauren has gone on to sell her products online, and at craft fairs and trade shows as well.

The NYC Craft Entrepreneurship Program started in January 2014 as a partnership with the NYC Department of Small Business Ser- vices (SBS), Etsy, and Citi Community Development. The free program is designed to teach makers of craft goods the necessary skills to quickly start a small business online and earn ex­tra income from the sale of handmade products. Students are taught how to take advantage of their creative skills by launching an online outlet to sell their goods.

Classes are led by experienced online craft-sellers, who teach students in five sessions about basics such as marketing, online branding, and product photography. Students apply what they’ve learned to create their own online store on Etsy, a leading marketplace for handmade goods. The program uses Etsy's platform as a real-world learning lab, and combines SBS' experience in helping small bu­sinesses start, operate, and grow to provide the business training. Past participants have exhibited and sold a wide range of products such as jewelry, toys, clothing, paintings, and even furniture.

Since the program’s start, over 230 entrepreneurs have graduated from the two week course, which has been held in all five boroughs and conducted in both English and Spanish. The course consists of five classes, each with its own distinct theme, and includes topics such as “Building and Marketing a Brand,” “Growing Your Business,” and “Pricing Your Work.” By the end of the course, participants have familiarized themselves with a wide range of basic business principles that can be transferred to both on-and-offline marketplaces. In addition, the curriculum includes financial education and opportunities for coaching provided by the NYC Office of Financial Empowerment.

The next round of NYC Craft Entrepreneurship Program classes will be held in early 2015. Eligible applicants must reside in New York City have at least one handmade craft item ready to sell, commit to attending four weeknight classes and one Satur- day class, have never made any sales on Etsy.com, have a debit/credit card and checking account (necessary for sales transactions) and have regular access to a computer with an internet connection.

For more information, and to fill out an application, visit http://bit.ly/NYCCEP. You can also support NYC Craft Entrepreneurs by shopping at their stores on www.etsy.com.
