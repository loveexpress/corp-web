30th Anniversary

by Michael Leonard James

Wow! Has it actually been 30 years since we started this awesome ministry tool? As difficult as it is to believe that much time has passed, I've shared before how The Love Express has impacted my personal life. I met the young lady who would one day become my wife of 25 years, (this September 1) and the mother of my three babies Michael II, Rachael & Mit­chel, currently ages 23, 21 & 12 respectively.

I still have memories be­ing in a station wagon, driving around to various churches, ministries and businesses trying to communicate the vision of unity in the paper and getting people to understand importance of being a part of that. Not to be negative, but we were met with a few doubters and some folk who just didn't get us. There were people who questioned our untested motives or who cautiously watched to see where we were going with this little newspaper. And yes, there were those who tried to "compete" or actually wanted to see us fail because they thought this venture was about making a bunch of money.

Well here we are 30 years later and I believe we have responded to every naysayer that we've faced by simple staying the course assigned by our loving Heavenly Father. When you do what God has given you to do, you're not responsible for the outcome. In this case the outcome however has pro­ven to be worth every challenge. Ministry leaders who would never have even heard of each other or even those who were suspicious of each other, have now come toge­ther to realize how much they have in common while celebrating the rich diversity that comprises this remarkable body of Christ (Psalm 133:1 - Behold how good and how pleasant it is for brethren to dwell together in unity).

I pay homage to Cliff and Elaine Dubrowsky who sought us out to help carry this vision to New York City. We went to Connecticut eve­ry month and spent all day and night literally cutting and  pasting each component of our periodical. We had no idea what we were doing - looking for inspiring articles and stories, searching for clip art and attempting to resize them to fit those articles. Before that, we had to solicit advertisers to finance this effort. This became our job, our careers, our ministry and we had to figure this out with no experience to speak of. God used Cliff and Elaine to teach us everything we needed to know to get this thing off and running.

After a couple of years they move out west and we were left in the capable hands of Robert Morgan in New Paltz, NY. At that time there were editions of the Love Express in various ci­ties across the country. Soon we would outgrow all of the other publications and had to illicit the help of a local typesetter. Thanks for your tireless efforts Ray, in the Bronx. With the advent of desk top publishing we had to delve in even more uncharted territo­ry. I was terrified and felt ill equipped to tackle such a task. I was never a techie and had barely learned how to make entries into a CRT working at SAKS Fifth Avenue. (Remember those ancient two color computers?). But I was staying up to 3 or 4 in the morning while my new wife was home pregnant with our first born. By the time he was born, after 1 day of QuarkXpress lessons and many late nights, some of this was starting to make sense and I was getting a little more proficient at mani­pulating the "mouse."

More churches and Christian businesses were starting to buy into the concept and we no longer had to beat the pavement to find advertisers. We still made lots of phone calls to invite new business but in the booming technological age learned how to work smarter rather than harder. My wife Rhonda with my son stretched out on a table, came on board to help us crank out those articles. No need to call Child Services, he was well taken care of. Anyway the design ideas started to flow, and we all began to specialize, each having his/her own set of responsibilities in getting this paper printed and distributed to those anxiously awaiting its publication.

For 30 years, every month without fail, it has come out. For quite a few years, beautiful color ads have graced the pages. It has been as small as 8 pages and as many as 108 pages. More and more advertisements come camera ready and others are still produced in The Love Express office. Seve­ral staff member past and present have sat in front of the computer including, Amie, Sidney. Sehon, Sha­nelle, Melville, Gregory, Tam­my, Natalie to name a few. I can't forget us sponsoring New York City's first ”March for Jesus." What an impact that made on the Christian and secular community.

It would be impossible to really recap all these years in one article. Many great men and women of God have gone on to be with The Lord, in 30 years while existing families are expanding and new ministries are birthed throughout this fair city. The circle of life continues. I'm just glad to have been a part of The Love Express legacy. Edie, William, Naomi & distributors, thank you for 30 years of service to the  Body of Christ. Deb and others thanks for stepping in.

Michael L. James, former assistant editor of The Love Express. 1985 - 1996 (Still a current distributor in the Bronx)
