Christ Pentecostal Temple

Rededication Service

A "Rededication Service of the Sanctuary" was held Sunday, October 4th at Christ Pentecostal Temple. It was the first time in four months the congregation of Christ Pentecostal Temple worshiped in their newly completed, totally renovated brand new sanctuary. They had been worshipping at services that were being held at PS 40, since the sanctuary was closed in the beginning of June. The church, located at 109-45 157th Street, was built in 1960, and has been completely renovated, both outside and inside.

Since being appointed Pastor in 2004, District Elder Derrick Farmer has transformed the church, not only by increasing community outreach and more than doubling the size of the congregation, but has been dedicated to rebuilding the church. The new exterior includes new stained glass windows and a beautiful new entrance and new lobby, which were completed in 2006. The sanctuary was stripped down to it's concrete walls and totally rebuilt. Though he was appointed in 2004, Pastor Farmer has been with this church since his early teens. As a teen, he served as Youth Vice President and Youth President, and was also a choir member. Before becoming Pastor, he was a Minister and became an Ordained Minister of the Church.

The church has increased it's outreach to the community, with ministries visiting hospitals, prisons, long term care facilities and shelters. For Thanksgiving, they prepare between 50 to 100 food baskets with turkey and all the trimmings to prepare an entire Thanksgiving dinner for the needy. At Christmas time, they go caroling through the neighborhood, distributing gifts for needy children.

Christ Pentecostal Temple Church reopened at it's original location at 109-45 157th Street, Jamaica, NY. Call 718-529-3900.
