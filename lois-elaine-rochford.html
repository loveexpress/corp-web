Pastor Lois Elaine Rochford

Who can find a virtuous woman? This question is a common biblical reference of the quest to identify a woman of godly character, nobility, grace and honor easily answered, by those who knew and loved Lady Lois Elaine Rochford. The quintessential First Lady, who was also a pastor, preacher, prayer warrior, mother, wife, entrepreneur and style icon, embodied the essence of Proverbs 31, and lived her life with unwavering commitment to her call. Lady Lois fulfilled her life’s work on earth and on October 18, 2014, transitioned from labor to reward at the age of 59 years old.

Born and bred in Brooklyn, New York, Pastor Lois thrived as a young woman and gave her life to the Lord as a teenager. Her adolescence was marked by her commitment to ministry and spiritual growth, as she grew up under the tutelage of Bi­shop Roy Brown and the Pilgrim Church. She demonstra- ted commitment to prayer and gleaned from the mothers of the church who taught her to pray and intercede; an anointing and conviction that would follow her into adulthood.

As she grew and matured in faith, life and mini­s­try, Lois assumed her most beloved roles as wife and eventually mother, when she met and married a young evangelist, know then as Re­verend Robert Joel Roch­ford. From this union came her two children, Melaine and Robert II, who she abso­lu­te­ly adored. For 34 years, Lady Lois walked diligently beside her husband Bishop Roch­ford, as the two built and established New Life Cathedral, where she founded life-changing ministries and groomed men and women to walk in the fullness of  their God-ordained purposes. She walked diligently beside him and served as the Executive Pastor until her transition.

Lady Lois was a powerhouse and personality all her own. With a preaching style and gift for prayer, counsel and spiritual impartation, she impacted lives through service and had a conviction and anointing to minister especially to women. She mentored and mothered with dedication and care, adopting many spiritual children and groomed young first la­dies who sought her counsel and gleaned from her wisdom. She was well known for her candor, humor and practical teaching, as well as her pristine sense of fashion and style. As she traveled the country, she left her mark on the hearts of those who came to know her and her “Lois-isms”; the phrases she often used to share her love for life and those in it.

Evidence of the fruit of her life and legacy was noted by a two-day celebration on October 25–26, 2014, where nearly 5,000 people crow­ded both the New Life Cathedral and the Pilgrim Renaissance Center to honor and celebrate her legacy. Countless tributes shared anecdotes that were true to her loyalty, generosity and commitment to friendship, family and fun. She was truly a gift to the body of Christ, and to all who knew her.

To honor her memory and celebrate her birthday, there will be a benefit concert on February 6, 2015 and a memorial service on Sunday, February 8th; both held at the New Life Cathedral, located at 110 Junius Street in Brooklyn, NY. The concert will support the Lois Rochford Memorial Fund, a newly formed organization that will support young wo­men in need and train them with life skills, readying them for both ministry and professional growth and success.

For information on these services, please contact New Life Cathedral or visit the website at www. newlifeny.org.
